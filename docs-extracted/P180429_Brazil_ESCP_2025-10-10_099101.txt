1. The Government of the State of Bahia (the Borrower) will implement Bahia Sustainable Rural Development
   Project (Projeto de Desenvolvimento Rural Sustentável do Estado Da Bahia - Fase II) with the involvement of
   the Development and Regional Action Company (Companhia de Desenvolvimento e Ação Regional - CAR) and
   the Water Engineering and Sanitation Company of Bahia (Companhia de Engenharia Hidrica e Saneamento da
   Bahia - CERB), as set out in the Loan Agreement. The International Bank for Reconstruction and Development
   (the Bank) has agreed to provide the original financing (P180429) for the Project as set out in said agreement.

2. The Government of the State of Bahia shall ensure that the Project is carried out in accordance with the
   Environmental and Social Standards (ESSs) and this Environmental and Social Commitment Plan (ESCP), in a
   manner acceptable to the World Bank. The ESCP is part of the Loan Agreement. Except as otherwise defined
   in this ESCP, capitalized terms used in this ESCP have the meanings given to them in said agreement.

3. Without limitation to the foregoing, the ESCP sets out material measures and actions that Borrower shall carry
   out or cause to be carried out, including, as applicable, their respective timeframes, institutional, staffing,
   training, monitoring and reporting arrangements, and grievance management. The ESCP also sets out the
   environmental and social (E&S) documents to be adopted and implemented under the Project, all of which
   will be subject to prior consultation and disclosure, in accordance with the ESSs, in form and substance
   acceptable to the Bank. Once adopted, such E&S instruments may be reviewed from time to time with the
   prior written consent of the Bank.

4. As agreed by the World Bank and the Borrower, this ESCP will be revised from time to time, if necessary,
   during the implementation of the Project, to reflect adaptive management of Project changes and unforeseen

 MATERIAL MEASURES AND ACTIONS                                                                                             TIMEFRAME                        RESPONSIBLE ENTITY
       Establish, publish, maintain and operate an accessible grievance and interaction
       mechanism, to receive and facilitate resolution of concerns and grievances in relation to
       the Project in a manner that is promptly and efficient, transparent, culturally
       appropriate, and readily accessible to all parties affected by the Project, free of charge
       and without retaliation, including with respect to concerns and grievances referred
       anonymously, consistent with ESS 10.

         The grievance mechanism should be prepared to receive, record and facilitate the
         resolution of complaints of sexual and psychological harassment, including through the
         referral of victims to gender-based violence service providers, in a safe, confidential and
         victim-centered manner.
