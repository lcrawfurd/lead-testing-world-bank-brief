    1.2.3 Environmental protection enforcement organizations

    During the construction of the project, the contractors will play a key role in
environmental management, pollution control and implementation of prevention
and control measures, therefore, the following requirements need to be made
for the contractors:

    (1) The contractors must have the relevant organizational structure,
personnel and budgetary arrangements to ensure that the environmental
management plan is effectively implemented;

    (2) The contractors’ bidding documents and bidding offers shall give full
consideration to the environmental impact mitigation measures and their costs
during the construction period, and shall eventually be implemented in the
project contracts as a contractual requirement for the project contractors.

    (3) The contractors, their designated environmental staff, and the
construction supervisors, etc., must receive training on environmental
protection and environmental management prior to the commencement of work
to ensure that they are competent to perform the work.

    (4) The contractors of the different sections are required to have a sufficient
number of full-time environmental staff to monitor and record environmental
activities during construction, and to provide environmental records to the
owner and environmental protection supervision organizations on a regular
basis for supervision and review.

    (5) During the construction process, the contractors shall maintain
communication and consultation with the public in the area of the project by
erecting bulletin boards in the construction units to notify the public of specific

2. Environmental Impacts of the Project and Mitigation

Measures

    Based on the identified environmental impacts, corresponding mitigation
measures have been developed (Table 2). The design institutes and the
contractors will incorporate the mitigation measures into the design, bidding
documents, construction contracts and operation management under the
supervision of the PIU, Guiyang PMO, and the supervision company. The
effectiveness of these measures will be assessed based on monitoring results
from the supervision and external monitoring consultants to determine if
adjustments and improvements to these measures are needed.

3 Environmental Monitoring Plan

    The purpose of environmental monitoring is to provide a comprehensive
and timely grasp of the pollution dynamics of the proposed project, to
understand the implementation and execution of the project's environmental
management measures, to provide timely feedback to the competent
authorities, to make timely adjustments and responses, and to provide a
scientific basis for the project's environmental management.

5 Grievance Redress Mechanism

    (1) Grievance Redress Mechanism (GRM)

    In order to better safeguard the interests of the project communities and
residents, the project will establish an accessible, open and effective GRM,
whereby affected persons can lodge a grievance at any time regarding any
issue in the implementation of the project. This GRM is suitable for all affected
persons in the project area.

    The principles of the GRM include:

    1) Ensure the participation and consultation of the above beneficiary
groups in the implementation of the project, as well as ensuring that the project

6 Monitoring and Reporting System

6.1 Purpose and Methodology

    In accordance with China's regulations on environmental management of
construction projects and the requirements of the Bank's operational policies,
the Borrower shall be responsible for the preparation of the Evaluation and

    Environmental management requires the necessary exchange of
information between different departments and positions in Guiyang PMO,
owners, contractors, and operators as well as the organization's communication
of relevant information to the outside (interested parties, the public, etc.).

    Internal information exchange can be carried out in a variety of ways, such
as meetings and internal briefings, but there must be one formal meeting per
month, and all exchanges of information should be documented and archived.

1 Introduction

1.1 Project Background
    Guiyang Green Urban Development Project aims to reduce greenhouse
gas emissions through the implementation of green measures in urban areas,
increase carbon sequestration by restoring ecosystem productivity, and
enhance the protection and sustainable management of land area and inland
water area for nature-friendly urban development. The activities of the project
include facilities such as the greenway around Lake Aha and supporting
stations and pipelines, roads and parking lots; biodiversity monitoring and
protection works in the wetland park, restoration of bird habitats, restoration of
the ecological buffer zone in Lake Aha, river training works in Lanni Gully, and
river training works in Lanni Gully; as well as the wastewater interception and
lifting pump station around the lake, upgrading of the wastewater interception
ditch in the Xiaoche River Basin, and the wastewater clearing and diversion
works in the Lanni Gully, Baiyan River Youyu River Basin rural water pollution
prevention and control project, wetland nature education, in addition to low-
carbon smart platforms and green accounts, climate-smart and nature-friendly
urban spatial planning, carbon sequestration methodology research, urban
green and low-carbon transportation research, study tours and training, and
project management, and other technical assistance activities not involving
construction. To address the issue of possible negative impacts on biodiversity
during the implementation of sub-projects involving construction, this evaluation
has prepared a Biodiversity Management Plan (BMP) in accordance with the
Bank’s Environmental and Social Standards (ESS) 6: Biodiversity Conservation
and Sustainable Management of Living Natural Resources, which puts forward
management requirements to mitigate possible negative impacts on
biodiversity brought about by the project, and at the same time, strengthen the
positive impacts of the project on biodiversity.

1.2 Purpose of Preparation
    The purpose of this plan is to provide guidance on biodiversity conservation
during the implementation of restoration, ecological restoration, and

                                           1
     Biodiversity Management Plan (BMP) for Guiyang Green Urban Development Project


construction-related sub-projects located in Lake Aha National Wetland Park,
within the wetland, and other bird habitats. It includes implementation
organizations and responsibilities, biodiversity impact mitigation measures,
training, monitoring and reporting.

1.3 Scope of Application
    This plan applies to all ecological protection and restoration sub-projects,
permanent or temporary occupation of the red line of ecological protection,
wetlands, forest land in non-urban concentrated construction areas, or civil
engineering sub-projects adjacent to nature reserves.

2.2 WB Environment, Health and Safety Guidelines (EHSGs)
    This project is applicable to the General Guidelines and the Environmental,

    2.2.1    Environment, Health and Safety General Guidelines

    Effective management of EHS issues requires that EHS factors be
considered in the project management process and that this be done in an
organized, hierarchical manner, including the following steps:

        Identify potential EHS risks and hazards at an early stage during the
         preparatory phase of project construction, including consideration of
         EHS impacts in site selection, planning, design, and construction
         documents.

        Involvement of professionals who evaluate and manage EHS impacts and
         risks, including the use of techniques recommended in this Guide as
         applicable to the project when preparing plans and procedures for the
         project or specific operational activities.



                                                 4
     Biodiversity Management Plan (BMP) for Guiyang Green Urban Development Project


       Understand the likelihood and danger of EHS risks: the nature of the
        project activity, whether it will generate large quantities of wastewater
        or exhaust, whether it will involve the use of hazardous substances or
        hazardous processes. The potential impact on the environment if
        appropriate risk prevention measures are not taken, and the
        preparation and rehearsal of an emergency plan.

       Prioritize risk management strategies to achieve an overall reduction
        in risks to human health and environment, with a focus on preventing
        irreversible and/or significant impacts.

       Prioritize prevention at the source, e.g., siting and routing to avoid
        critical habitats, adopting green construction techniques, etc.

       Continuous monitoring of the environmental performance of a business
        or facility and its pollutant outlets, coupled with effective and efficient
        accountability, leads to improved EHS performance.

3.3 Status of Conservation of Lake Aha National Wetland
    3.3.1 Plant Diversity Conservation Program

    In the process of protecting plant diversity, Lake Aha National Wetland Park

has taken a series of effective measures to maintain the integrity and stability

of the wetland ecosystem. The first is to protect the native vegetation in the park

and reduce human interference. The park management strictly limits the

activities of tourists to avoid direct damage to the vegetation. At the same time,

the park also implements a strict ecological protection system, strengthens the

daily management and maintenance of the vegetation, and ensures that the

natural renewal and succession process of the vegetation is not disturbed. The

park has also strengthened the protection of rare plants and established a

detailed plant list and monitoring system to understand and grasp the growth

status of plants in a timely manner, providing strong support for scientific

protection. In terms of afforestation and greening, Lake Aha National Wetland

Park has carried out large-scale afforestation and greening work in suitable

locations. The park has selected suitable tree species and vegetation types

according to the climate and soil conditions of the wetland, and improved the

green coverage of the park through scientific planting and management. These

trees and vegetation not only provide a good ecological environment for the

wetland, but also become an important part of the wetland ecosystem, providing

habitats and breeding places for organisms in the wetland.

    Lake Aha National Wetland Park has also invested a great deal of energy

and resources in the restoration and reconstruction of vegetation in damaged

habitats in the park. Through the restoration and reconstruction of vegetation,

the park has effectively improved the ecological environment of the wetland and

enhanced the self-regulation and stability of the ecosystem. At the same time,

the park also focuses on the scientific and sustainable nature of vegetation

                                          16
     Biodiversity Management Plan (BMP) for Guiyang Green Urban Development Project


restoration and reconstruction to ensure that the restored and reconstructed

vegetation can be truly integrated into the wetland ecosystem and contribute to

the long-term development of the wetland.

    Through the implementation of these measures, the plant diversity of Lake

Aha National Wetland Park has been effectively protected, and the wetland

ecosystem has shown good development. The park has a rich variety of

vegetation and a stable ecological structure, providing a good habitat and

breeding environment for organisms in the wetland. At the same time, the park

also actively carries out science popularization and education activities to raise

public awareness of plant diversity and its protection, which lays a solid social

foundation for the protection of plant diversity.

    3.3.2 Animal Diversity Conservation Program

    In Lake Aha National Wetland Park, thanks to strong ecological protection

measures, rare species such as the green-headed duck, a bird under national

first-class protection, and the white pheasant, a wild animal under national

second-class key protection, have been found in the park, and the discovery of

these species not only set a new record for the number of animal and plant

species in the park, but also reflected the effectiveness of the protection work.

    In addition to direct conservation measures, publicity and education are

equally important. Strengthening animal protection publicity and education can

enhance public awareness of animal diversity and protection. The public is an

important force in animal protection. Through publicity and education, more

people can be made aware of the value and ecological functions of wild animals,

so that they can consciously participate in animal protection actions. For

example, animal protection lectures, exhibitions and other activities can be

organized on a regular basis to enable the public to understand the ecological

habits and conservation knowledge of wild animals and enhance their
                                          17
       Biodiversity Management Plan (BMP) for Guiyang Green Urban Development Project


conservation awareness. Meanwhile, animal protection courses can also be

strengthened in school education to cultivate children's awareness of animal

protection from an early age.

       In addition, the prevention, control and monitoring of animal diseases is

also an important part of wildlife protection. Wild animals may carry various

viruses and parasites, posing a threat to humans and other animals. Therefore,

the Park Management Office has to strengthen the prevention, control and

monitoring of animal diseases to detect and deal with animal outbreaks in a

timely manner to ensure the health and safety of wildlife. This includes

strengthening the monitoring and early warning of wildlife epidemics, enhancing

the development and use of vaccines, and improving the capacity of animal

epidemic prevention. Through these measures, the risk of occurrence and

spread of wildlife epidemics can be effectively reduced to protect the health of

wildlife and the safety of human beings1 .

3.4 Ecological Problems in the Project Area
       (i) Higher levels of disturbance to the terrestrial vegetation in the Lake

Aha basin

       Drylands are widely distributed in the flat valleys and areas with gentle

slopes of the Lake Aha basin. Tree woodlands are mainly found in areas with

large slopes that are not suitable for cultivation as farmland, and the distribution

of broad-leaved evergreen forests is narrow, with only small patches on steep

slopes at the mouth of the Baiyan River, which is less traveled by people. There

are large tracts of farmland located on hilltops and gentle slopes along the

waterfront that are abandoned. In the watershed, due to the impact of town

development and construction and human activities, the topsoil is broken


   1
       Master Plan of Lake Aha National Wetland Park, Guiyang, Guizhou, China

                                                       18

without timely greening, and some areas have low vegetation cover and

exposed rock and soil, resulting in soil erosion. To address this problem, this

feasibility study carries out a forest vegetation restoration project on the

north side of Lake Aha.

    (ii) Poor state of the aquatic ecosystems in the Lake Aha basin

    The Lake Aha basin has low coverage of submerged plant communities

and a high variety of exotic and even invasive species in wet, emergent and

floating plants. There are few species and quantity of fish. Snail populations are

unevenly distributed and have a high mortality rate. Crustaceans such as

shrimp were not seen. The apple snail, an exotic invasive species, is widely

distributed. There is an urgent need to restore healthy water ecosystems in the

Lake Aha basin to enhance the self-purification function of the water body by

restoring the diversity of aquatic communities. To address this issue, this

project will carry out a river water ecological restoration project in the

Lake Aha basin, removing invasive species, releasing fish and benthic

organisms, and planting submerged and water-supporting plants in order

to improve the ecological environment.

    (iii) Few species and quantity of birds in the Lake Aha basin

    Birds with high numbers in the Lake Aha basin are all widespread and

common species, such as egrets, little grebes, yellow-rumped bulbuls, and so

on. The distribution of waterbirds is closely related to the type of habitat they

require, e.g., brown river crow, white-topped brook robin, and red-tailed water

robin are distributed in the channels of various tributaries, and gulls are

distributed in the waters of the great lake of Lake Aha, but all are in small,

single-digit numbers. This is related to the high degree of disturbance of

terrestrial vegetation and the poor ecosystem structure of the water body. This

project addresses this issue by carrying out interception of pollution around the
                                          19
     Biodiversity Management Plan (BMP) for Guiyang Green Urban Development Project


lake, water ecological restoration and bird habitat restoration activities to

improve the quality of bird habitat and increase bird biodiversity.

    (iv) Introduction of invasive alien species by inappropriate wetland

restoration actions

    Existing artificial wetlands used for water purification in the Lake Aha area

are mainly planted with exotic plants, such as waterweed, pontederia cordata,

canna indica, thalia dealbata, etc. The eggs and young snails of the apple snails

will be introduced into the lake area along with these exotic plants, which will

lead to the current situation of widespread of the apple snails. To address this

problem, this activity needs to take measures to control the population size of

existing invasive species and to clean up the project area of exotic species,

such as upgrading the wetland at the inlet of Jinzhong River, and the gradual

replacement of exotic species such as thalia dealbata, pontederia cordata,

canna indica, and waterweed by native plants in the existing wetland. In the

subsequent restoration of the wetland ecosystem, native plants and animals

should be used to avoid the introduction of invasive alien species to the greatest

extent possible.

    (v) More serious point source and surface source pollutions in the

Lake Aha basin

    According to the project feasibility study information, the wastewater

treatment rate of Lake Aha area is about 50%, centralized treatment rate is 30%.

Although there are urban municipal pipeline network and urban wastewater

treatment plants and other facilities in the towns around the basin, but the

pipeline network coverage is incomplete, old, broken, with issues like

"overflowing, bubbling, dripping, leaking", which leads to wastewater mixed with

rainwater and ultimately discharged into the river. And for the rural areas within

the basin, wastewater treatment facilities are not perfect, there are also quite a
                                          20
     Biodiversity Management Plan (BMP) for Guiyang Green Urban Development Project


lot of wastewater directly or indirectly discharged into the river, bringing impact

to water quality of the branches and even Aha Reservoir. In response to this

problem, this activity carries out the construction of Lake Aha Interceptor

Pipeline Network, which collects and transports the regional domestic

wastewater to the existing Erqiao Wastewater Treatment Plant and Xiaohe

Wastewater Treatment Plant for treatment. Domestic wastewater along the

Baiyan River and the Youyu River is collected centrally and used for agricultural

irrigation after being treated to the standard by the wastewater treatment station.

5 Biodiversity Impacts and Mitigation Measures

5.1 Design Period
    The design phase of the project will not directly cause environmental risks

or impacts, mainly through the optimization of the design scheme, and auxiliary

design of impact prevention and control measures and other design phase work

to avoid or mitigate some of the environmental risks and impacts of the

construction and operation phases.

    5.1.1 Protection against pests and diseases

    The 5 hectares of forest revegetation activities involved in this project may

cause pest and disease problems.

    The main impact mitigation measures adopted by the project include

rational selection and allocation of tree species, and improvement of resistance

to pests and diseases through species diversity.

    5.1.2 Prevention of invasive alien species

    The ecological restoration and greening works involved in this project may

cause invasive alien species. The main impact mitigation measures adopted by

the project include the selection of native tree species in accordance with the

principle of appropriate land and appropriate trees, and the avoidance of the

use of exotic species.

    5.1.3 Avoidance of disturbance and damage to wetland park

    The project's greenway around the lake and wastewater interception

project around the lake occupy the wetland park, which may damage the

ecological environment of the wetland park. The main impact mitigation

measures adopted by the project are to avoid the wetland as much as possible

for the project alignment, and adopt measures to reduce the occupancy such
                                          26
     Biodiversity Management Plan (BMP) for Guiyang Green Urban Development Project


as overhanging trestle and controlling the red line if it cannot be avoided.

Ecological restoration techniques, such as artificial wetland and ecological

embankment, are adopted to restore and rebuild the aquatic ecosystem.

    5.1.4 Avoidance of disturbance and damage to critical habitats

    Construction activities may occupy animal habitats and cause disturbance

and damage to vegetation.

    The selection of project sites and routes should try to avoid the north side

of the woodland, Kailong Village and bird habitat in the southern part of the park,

to prevent ecological damage from the beginning. If it is not possible to avoid

them, the project will try to minimize the disturbance and damage to the habitats

through the adjustment of siting and routing or partial optimization, such as

overhanging trestle, natural road surface and other measures. Increase the

protection of bird habitat in the design, strengthen the protection of bird habitat

in the area of Kailong Village, set up low closed fences on the periphery, install

artificial nesting boxes, strengthen publicity, and increase the density of patrols

to reduce the disturbance to birds from the work and life of people nearby,

protect the naturalness of the habitat, and provide a good habitat for birds.

Combined with the construction of protection and isolation shrub forest belt,

adopt natural restoration measures of wetland vegetation, supplemented by

artificial planting, choose native wetland plants such as reeds, moss grass,

cattail , etc., while replanting some berry plants as the food source of birds,

improve the environment of waterfowl habitat, and enhance its ecological

carrying capacity.

    In order to reduce the obstruction of the road to animal activities, the height

of the road edge is reduced in the design, while the side ditch avoids the use of

vertical structures, and it is preferable to use a sloping form or install a cover to

prevent animals from falling into the ditch.

5.2 Construction Period
    5.2.1 Disturbance to wildlife by construction activities

    Construction and related traffic activities may impede animal access,

generate sound, light, and vibration that may cause disturbance to wildlife, and

vehicles or mechanical equipment may directly cause injury to wildlife.

    The main impact mitigation measures adopted by the project include

training of construction personnel, reasonable arrangement of construction and

transportation time and period, stipulation of construction areas and

transportation routes, traffic management measures, and strengthening of

equipment and road maintenance. Injured wild animals found in each case will

be sent to wild animal shelter and rescue organizations for assistance.

    5.2.2 Pest and disease risks

    Activities such as forest revegetation may introduce or trigger pests and

diseases that may cause impacts such as reduced quality of vegetation and

habitats, and damage to other plants and animals during the control process.

    The main impact mitigation measures adopted by the project include strict

seedling sources, quality selection, quarantine inspection, and prohibition of

transplanting plants with pests and diseases.

    5.2.3 Risk of invasive alien species

    Forest restoration and greening projects may introduce exotic species, as

may the movement of related personnel, vehicles and equipment, resulting in

loss of native species, degradation of habitat quality, etc., as well as damage to

other flora and fauna in the prevention and control process.

    The main impact mitigation measures adopted by the project include strict

seedling type and source selection, quarantine and inspection to prevent the

                                          33
     Biodiversity Management Plan (BMP) for Guiyang Green Urban Development Project


introduction of exotic species, exotic species inspection of relevant seedlings,

personnel and vehicles, and strengthening personnel training.

    5.2.4 Damage or disturbance to vegetation by construction activities

    The project's permanent footprint or temporary construction footprint would

result in the clearing of vegetation in the area, causing a reduction of vegetation.

    The main impact mitigation measures adopted by the project include

identification, avoidance and protection of key protected plants, construction in

strict accordance with the delineation of the scope of construction, and full

consideration of ecological environmental protection measures in the

construction program, such as protective nets, grass curtains, etc., which can

be covered to separate vegetation from the construction area, in order to

minimize the disturbance of the construction to the vegetation.

    5.2.5 Impacts of construction activities on habitats

    Construction encroachment can result in loss of habitat area, and

construction and personnel activities may cause some disturbance to the

habitat environment.

    The main impact mitigation measures adopted by the project include

avoiding the destruction of animal nests, minimizing the damage to vegetation,

and reasonably scheduling construction to reduce impacts on wildlife activities.

    5.2.6 Impacts on ecosystem service functions

    Construction activities disturb and damage surface vegetation and soil

structure, which can accelerate soil erosion and reduce carbon sinks, thus

affecting function of ecosystem regulation service. The loss of partial landscape

affects cultural service function. Loss of vegetation reduces the ability to supply

food sources to animals in the short term, i.e. the support service function may

                                          34
     Biodiversity Management Plan (BMP) for Guiyang Green Urban Development Project


be affected. Due to the small scale of the project greenway, the small width of

the road red line (7 meters for the main greenway route and 2.5 meters for the

access routes), the ecological restoration-based and relatively decentralized

project, and the short construction period of the same site, the degree of its

impact on ecosystem service functions is slight and is short-term and reversible.

    The main impact mitigation measures adopted by the project include

choosing reasonable construction programs, reducing temporary land

occupation, retaining surface vegetation or cover, reducing hydraulic erosion,

adopting drainage and water interception measures, and restoring vegetation

cover in a timely manner.

5.3 Operation Period

    5.3.1 Upgrading the quality of bird habitats

    The bird habitat restoration project covers an area of 53.29 hectares in
Kailong Village in the northwestern part of Lake Aha National Wetland Park
facing the mountain and the reservoir lake area in the southern part of the park,
and restores the structure and quantity of plant and animal communities,
improves the heterogeneity and appropriateness of the bird habitat, further
enriches the diversity of birds, and improves the wetland ecosystem's self-
sustaining ability through the prevention, control and management of pollutants,
ecological restoration project, and the creation of a micro-habitat. In addition,
the project also establishes a biomonitoring system to monitor bird habitats, so
as to grasp the dynamics of bird populations and habitat changes in real time,
and to better carry out conservation work.

    5.3.2 Enhancing ecosystem functions

    The implementation of the project will increase the forest area through
forest vegetation restoration and enhance the quality of wetland ecosystems
through ecological restoration, which can improve the carbon sequestration
capacity of forest and wetland ecosystems and significantly increase carbon
sinks. In addition, the project will have the following positive effects on
ecosystem service functions:

    (1) Enhancement of regulatory services

    Forests and wetland vegetation play a role in intercepting rainwater
through the forest canopy, the shrub and grass layer, the withered leaf layer
and the soil layer, reducing surface runoff and runoff velocity and weakening
the erosive force of rainfall on the surface, thus preventing soil erosion and soil
erosion. Enriching vegetation communities can also further improve soil
structure and play a role in water conservation and water purification. New and
                                          44
     Biodiversity Management Plan (BMP) for Guiyang Green Urban Development Project


improved forest and wetland vegetation can also purify the air, improve regional
air quality, increase carbon sinks and regulate temperature and climate.

    (2) Enhancement of cultural services

    The project carries out forest vegetation restoration, interception of
pollution to improve water quality, shoreline restoration and other projects, and
create natural and cultural landscape nodes, science education nodes, nodes
of local characteristics, etc. The overall landscape of the project area will be
improved, and take on the functions of science popularization and education,
tourism and sightseeing.

    (3) Upgrading support services:

    By increasing the area of forests and upgrading the quality of forests and
wetlands, the soil structure can be improved, soil nutrient improvement and
cycling can be promoted, and the level of primary productivity of ecosystems
can be increased, providing conditions for the maintenance and enhancement
of biodiversity. At the same time the increase in biomass, such as vegetation,
will also improve the level of oxygen supply.

    5.3.3 Disturbance to wildlife by the project activities

    Visitor tours, stewardship patrols, and corresponding transportation
activities after the completion of the greenway might cause accidental injuries
to wildlife such as disturbance or roadkill.

    The main impact mitigation measures adopted by the project include
restricting the excursion routes and time segments, controlling the flow, and
strengthening the management of tourists and staff training. Injured wildlife that
is found in all cases should be sent to wildlife shelter and rescue organizations
for assistance.

    5.3.4 Environmental impacts of pests and diseases and their
management

    Pest and disease problems may occur in each ecological restoration
project, and the management process, if used, may cause loss of other
organisms, and water and soil pollution. The projects adopt integrated pest
management measures, try not to use chemical pesticides, prioritize the use of
preventive means such as forest culture and management, followed by the use
of physical control and biological control methods for pest and disease
management.

    5.3.5 Risk of invasive alien species

    The landscaping works of various ecological protection and restoration
activities and civil works during the operation phase may require replanting of
planted saplings that have not survived, and there may be a risk of invasive
alien species.

    The main impact mitigation measures adopted by the project include the
selection of originally designed tree species, strict inspection and quarantine of
seedlings and related transportation vehicles and personnel, and regular
screening of exotic species.

8 Reporting mechanisms

    Each sub-project contractor, external monitoring consultant, and PIU shall
record the project progress, BMP implementation, and ecological impact
monitoring results and report them to the World Bank and the Municipal PMO
in a timely manner, and shall consolidate them in the semi-annual external
environment monitoring report, which shall include:

    During construction and during restoration of temporary occupied land
after construction: the contractors should report on the implementation of the
mitigation plan for negative impacts on biodiversity on a semi-annual basis;

    One year after completion: the PIU submits a project completion report on
ecological survey summarizing the overall impact of the project on biodiversity.

    Operation period: the PIU submits the results of the biodiversity impact
survey and analyzes the impacts on and changes in regional biodiversity after
the implementation of the project on a semi-annual basis.




    The recommended outline of the report is as follows:

    Project overview;

    Project environmental management agency;

    Project progress;

    BMP implementation;

    Environmental performance;

    Environmental monitoring results and analysis;

    Capacity building and training;

    Use of funds;

    The procedures cover cases of accidental/incidental discovery of
resources such as cultural heritage during the construction phase. This may
generally occur during surface clearance and earthworks. The project should
ensure that workers in the construction scope receive relevant training on the
importance of recognizing and reporting cultural heritage.

    1. Procedures

    For all construction workers who accidentally or seemingly discover
cultural heritage, they shall:

        Stop construction immediately and secure the site.

        Do not disturb or move finds.

        Immediately inform the project environmental management staff of the
         contractor or the urban investment company of this discovery and
         formally suspend excavation.

        The project environmental management staff of the urban investment
         company shall immediately report to the local cultural relics protection
         department (hereinafter referred to as "CRPD").

    The project environmental management staff shall then determine the
follow-up actions according to the procedure in the figure below. All cultural
heritage discoveries shall be documented by the project environmental
management staff of the urban investment company, and the local CRPD shall
determine whether they are cultural heritage and whether construction work
can continue. In general, the local CRPD will arrive at the site within 24h upon
receipt of the report and will provide an opinion on the action to be taken within
7 days. The administrative department of cultural relics may report to the local
people's government to notify the public security authority to assist in protecting
the site. The discovery of important cultural relics shall be immediately reported
to the administrative department of cultural relics under the State Council, and
                                             2
         Chance Find Procedures (CFP) for Cultural Heritage for Guiyang Green Urban
                                 Development Project

the administrative department of cultural relics under the State Council shall put
forward its opinions on the handling of such relics within fifteen days of the
street report.




    2. Follow-up actions

    When salvage excavation is required, the following procedures shall be
followed:




                                             3
    Chance Find Procedures (CFP) for Cultural Heritage for Guiyang Green Urban
                            Development Project

   The site is inspected by the CRPD or professionals with knowledge of
    cultural heritage protection, and an excavation plan is drawn up, which
    includes site investigation and removal of relics.

   Excavation work needs to be approved and agreed to by the CRPD
    before it can begin.

   Upon completion of the salvage excavation, a report containing a list
    and description of the finds shall be prepared and the finds shall be
    given to the CRPD. The construction may resume with the approval
    and consent by the CRPD.
