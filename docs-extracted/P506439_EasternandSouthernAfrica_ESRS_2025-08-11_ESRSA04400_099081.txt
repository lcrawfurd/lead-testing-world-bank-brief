                     C. Summary Description of Proposed Project Activities
                     The regional WASH Access MPA aims to increase access to at least basic WASH services at people’s home, in schools and
                     in health centers, following a common intervention framework across participating countries (country-level phases) and

                     four pillars, each with a different menu of activities and interventions: Pillar 1: Platforms to Accelerate Access to WASH :
                     The national platform approach consolidates government-led efforts to achieve universal, sustainable WASH services by
                     integrating funding and promoting cross-sectoral cooperation. National platforms, steered by political and sectoral
                     leadership, will focus on the preparation and/or implementation of National WASH Investment Plans, as well as on

                     implementation of WASH platforms will involve various government entities. Regional support is provided through
                     benchmarking progress towards SDG6, facilitating knowledge exchange, and attract climate-focused financing to
                     improve WASH access and environmental outcomes across Africa. The Regional WASH Platform (phase I), will serve as a
                     catalyst for countries within the region to embrace more systematic approaches in scaling up access to WASH. Phase 1
                     aims to facilitate countries' progress in accelerating sustainable access to WASH, through capacity building, progress
                     monitoring, while fostering knowledge sharing among participants. Additionally, the Regional WASH platform plays a
                     pivotal role in providing incentives and mobilizing funds, all directed towards advancing the regional WASH agenda.
                     Through these concerted efforts, phase 1 under the MPA seeks to drive meaningful and sustainable improvements in
                     WASH access across the region. Pillar 2: Sector Governance Reforms: This pillar focuses on strategic governance
                     reforms, addressing non-financial barriers, and enhancing regulatory oversight. It will provide incentives and technical

                     D. Environmental and Social Overview
                     D.1 Overview of Environmental and Social Project Settings
                     The Accelerating Access to WASH Regional Project is regional in scope, covering multiple countries across Eastern and
                     Southern Africa under the WASH Multi-Phased Programmatic Approach (MPA). While the operation does not involve
                     physical infrastructure or civil works, it provides technical assistance, capacity building, and financing mechanisms that
                     may inform and/or catalyze future country-level investments in the water and sanitation sector. As such, the project’s
                     environmental and social risks are primarily downstream and indirect, related to the influence of policy, planning, and
                     investment preparation activities.
                     The project spans both urban and rural areas. It targets rural settings in particular through support for monitoring
                     access to WASH services and building the capacity of rural water operators. Urban contexts are addressed through



Public Disclosure
                     activities focused on utility performance improvement and regulatory strengthening. Because many participating
                     countries include underserved and vulnerable communities, the project presents opportunities to promote inclusion,
                     gender equality, and citizen engagement. While Indigenous Peoples are not explicitly mentioned, the regional scope and
                     rural emphasis suggest a possibility of their presence in some participating areas.
                     Several countries in the target region are affected by fragility, conflict, or violence (FCV), and the project may operate in
                     these contexts. Through its focus on strengthening institutions, governance, and regional coordination, the project
                     offers opportunities to support stabilization and service delivery in FCV-affected settings.
                     The project’s support for transboundary aquifer assessments, which are particularly relevant for groundwater-
                     dependent rural populations and ecosystems, may take place in high-biodiversity or ecologically sensitive areas. While
                     this contributes to long-term environmental sustainability and resilience, it also necessitates careful management of
                     environmental risks such as impacts on hydrological services or water balance.

                     D.2 Overview of Borrower’s Institutional Capacity for Managing Environmental and Social Risks and Impacts
                     The African Union Development Agency – New Partnership for Africa’s Development (AUDA-NEPAD) is the
                     implementing agency of the Accelerating Access to WASH Regional Project and possesses strong institutional credibility
                     and extensive experience coordinating regional and continental initiatives. It has played a pivotal role in advancing
                     water, sanitation, and hygiene (WASH) agendas across Africa, including through policy advocacy, infrastructure
                     development planning, and partnerships with key stakeholders such as the African Union Commission (AUC), African
                     Ministers' Council on Water (AMCOW), Eastern and Southern Africa Water and Sanitation Regulators Association
                     (ESAWAS), and the Africa Investment Program onWater Secretariat.
                     While AUDA-NEPAD has successfully managed regional technical assistance (TA) and capacity-building projects with
                     various multilateral and bilateral development partners, it has limited direct experience applying the World Bank’s

                     B.3 Other Salient Features

                       Use of Borrower Framework                                                                                              No
                       The Borrower’s Environmental and Social Framework will not be used. Instead, the project will be implemented in full
                       compliance with the World Bank’s Environmental and Social Framework (ESF). This decision is based on the nature of
                       the project as a regional technical assistance operation, the cross-border scope, and the fact that the Borrower,
                       AUDA-NEPAD, is a continental-level regional institution and not a national government. As such, there is no unified,
                       national legal and institutional framework to apply consistently across all participating countries.

                       Furthermore, AUDA-NEPAD does not currently operate its own environmental and social policy framework for
                       environmental and social risk management of their operation that is equivalent in substance or implementation to the
                       World Bank’s ESF. While AUDA-NEPAD works closely with member states, RECs, and various development partners, its
                       internal environmental and social policies and procedures are still under development and do not provide a structured
                       system for screening, assessment, mitigation, and monitoring of E&S risks and impacts equivalent to the ESF.

                       ESS2: Labor and Working Conditions
                       i) AUDA-NEPAD has internal administrative rules and staff regulations for employment but lacks a comprehensive
                       labor management system aligned with ESS2.
                       ii) There is no formal grievance mechanism for contracted workers or structured monitoring of occupational health
                       and safety (OHS) risks.
                       iii) Labor Management Procedures requirements are embedded as part of the ESCP.

                       ESS3: Resource Efficiency and Pollution Prevention and Management
                       i) AUDA-NEPAD does not have a dedicated institutional mandate or policy instruments for managing resource
                       efficiency, pollution, or emissions across its programs.
                       ii) Capacity to evaluate water use efficiency, pollution prevention, or waste management practices is limited and will
                       rely on sectoral experts and consultants.
                       iii) TA ToRs will be designed to incorporate ESS3 considerations, and an E&S screening form and exclusion list will be
                       developed for activities with potential pollution or resource-use implications.

                       ESS4: Community Health and Safety
                       i) AUDA-NEPAD does not have an established framework for managing community health and safety risks associated
                       with project activities.
                       ii) There are no institutional procedures for assessing downstream impacts related to WASH infrastructure or
                       community exposure to health risks.
                       iii) ToRs for relevant technical assistance will incorporate ESS4 provisions, and project-supported guidance will
                       promote safe, inclusive, and resilient infrastructure planning in downstream operations.

                       ESS6: Biodiversity Conservation and Sustainable Management of Living Natural Resources
                       i) While AUDA-NEPAD engages in natural resource programs at the continental level, it does not have a standardized
                       framework to assess or manage risks to biodiversity as required under ESS6.
                       ii) AUDA-NEPAD does not currently screen for ecological sensitivity in TA design or investment planning activities.
                       iii) Biodiversity considerations will be embedded in ToRs and environmental screening tools, particularly for
                       transboundary aquifer assessments and infrastructure-related guidance.

                       ESS10: Stakeholder Engagement and Information Disclosure
                       i) AUDA-NEPAD has broad experience in stakeholder consultation and convening regional actors but lacks a formal
                       stakeholder engagement framework aligned with ESS10.
                       ii) There is no established grievance redress mechanism (GRM) at the institutional level tailored to project-level
                       implementation.
