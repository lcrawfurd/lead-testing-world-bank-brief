                     C. Summary Description of Proposed Project Activities
                     This project seeks to support Yemen in strengthening a Climate and Water Information Management System in selected
                     water basins to enhance sustainable and resilient water resources management and development. It will support a
                     comprehensive assessment of the enabling environment for the strengthening, upgrading and revitalization of the
                     CWIMS. This will include an assessment of existing data collection infrastructure, the various databases, along with
                     capacity in order to identify gaps at decentralized levels of the National Water Resources Authority (NWRA) and other
                     relevant institutions. Investment plans will be developed to address the gaps identified in the capacity assessment. The
                     project will also support the review and development of inter-institutional rules and regulations at basin level to

                     strengthen the technical capacities of decentralized institutions in using these systems and the development of end-
                     user products.


                     D. Environmental and Social Overview
                     D.1 Overview of Environmental and Social Project Settings
                     The proposed project aims to support several key institutions responsible for water management in Yemen, including
                     the National Water Resources Authority (NWRA), the Ministry of Water and Environment (MWE), the General Authority
                     for Rural Water Supply Projects (GARWSP), the Environment Protection Authority (EPA), and the Ministry of Agriculture
                     and Irrigation (MAI). As the initial phase of a longer-term support program, the project will focus on establishing and
                     managing a Water Information System (WIS) at selected water basins. This includes developing a cloud-based data
                     repository, a web-based visualization tool, and end-user products. Additionally, the project will provide capacity building
                     and promote inter-institutional coordination to enhance the sustainable and resilient management and development of
                     water resources.

                     D.2 Overview of Borrower’s Institutional Capacity for Managing Environmental and Social Risks and Impacts
                     FAO has Country Representation in Yemen and will be directly responsible for implementing the project. FAO has a
                     Project Coordination Unit (PCU) based in Sana’a, and technical staff deployed in its Aden sub-office, including all
                     fiduciary, environmental, and social aspects, as well as monitoring and reporting. That structure builds on the

                     II. SCREENING OF POTENTIAL ENVIRONMENTAL AND SOCIAL RISKS AND IMPACTS

                     A. Environmental and Social Risk Classification (ESRC)                                                                  Low


                     A.1 Environmental Risk Rating                                                                                           Low
                     The environmental risk rating of the project is low. The project is not expected to cause any mentionable
                     environmental risks as it does not support the procurement of any materials nor support rehabilitation or civil works
                     which may result in negative environmental impacts.
                                                                                                                                             Low
                     A.2 Social Risk Rating
                     The proposed project is anticipated to have a low social risk based on its components and interventions. Potential
                     social risks may include the capture of information by elites within certain institutions, lack of data sharing, weak
                     capacity of staff within the targeted institutions, and limited literacy or knowledge in using information systems.
                     There is a low risk of Sexual Exploitation and Abuse/Sexual Harassment (SEA/SH) among workers if a hostile
                     workplace environment is not properly managed. The proposed interventions are not expected to cause any

                     B. Relevance of Standards and Policies at Concept Stage

                     B.1 Relevance of Environmental and Social Standards

                      ESS1 - Assessment and Management of Environmental and Social Risks and Impacts                                     Relevant

                      The potential environmental and social risks are rated as low, given that the activities involved do not pose any risk
                      on environment, project workers nor the local community. However, potential social risks may include the capture of
                      project benefits by elites and challenges related to the low capacity of staff in the targeted institutions, particularly in
                      data collection, data entry into the cloud, and digital literacy. The risk of Sexual Exploitation and Abuse/Sexual
                      Harassment (SEA/SH) is expected to be low, as the workplace environment will be free from hostility, and awareness-
                      raising activities, including the signing of a Code of Conduct (CoC), will be conducted. No Occupational Health and
                      Safety (OHS) risks to workers or the community are anticipated, and no child labor is expected. Potential social risks
                      will also be addressed prior to the implementation stage through the development of necessary instruments, as
                      agreed upon between the implementing agency and the Project Team.

                      ESS10 - Stakeholder Engagement and Information Disclosure                                                          Relevant

                     C. Overview of Required Environmental and Social Risk Management Activities

                     C.1 What Borrower environmental and social analyses, instruments, plans and/or frameworks are planned or
                     required by Appraisal?
                     An ESCP will be prepared prior to appraisal by FAO and will be an integral part of the Loan Agreement. The ESCP will list
                     any potential assessments and/or instruments which may be required to address any of the identified risks and
                     impacts, with a timeframe ensuring that appropriate instruments are prepared prior to any project activities and
                     include mitigation measures and recommendations to maximize the benefits and emphasize an inclusive approach.
                     Furthermore, a preliminary Stakeholder Engagement Plan (SEP) will be prepared during concept stage. The SEP will be
                     updated, disclosed, consulted upon, and cleared by the WB prior to Appraisal, as appropriate and proportionate to
                     project-associated risks. In addition, a Labor Management Procedures (LMP) may be prepared, if needed, before the
                     actual implementation of activities starts.
