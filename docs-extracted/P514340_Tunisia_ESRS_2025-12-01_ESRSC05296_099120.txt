                     D. Environmental and Social Overview
                     D.1 Overview of Environmental and Social Project Settings
                     The project has a national scope, focusing on urban and peri-urban areas in Sfax and Sousse two major coastal
                     governorates facing acute water stress and high demand from urban, industrial, and tourism sectors. According to the


                    Dec 01, 2025                                                                                                     Page 2 of 9
                              The World Bank
                              Potable Water Security And Resilience Project (P514340)



                     census 2024 (INS), the governorate of Sousse has 762,281 inhabitants and 96.4% of 228513 households are connected
                     to the potable water network while Sfax registered 1047468 inhabitants and 89.3% of 304562 households connected to
                     the potable water network. The "reasonable" use of seawater desalination and the renovation of water supply and
                     distribution networks are among the National Water Strategy 2050 objectives and are relevant to the context of the two
                     governorates: Sousse is marked by the additional water demand generated by Tourism activities while Sfax is featured
                     by unprecedent urban development, industrial development and international migration dynamics.
                     Key environmental settings include coastal zones near Sousse, where the desalination plant will be expanded, and
                     dense urban areas in both governorates where the water distribution network will be rehabilitated. While not located in
                     protected or high-biodiversity zones, the surrounding marine and urban environments will inform project planning and
                     mitigation. However, the project also presents opportunities to enhance environmental sustainability by reducing
                     physical water losses, promoting climate resilience through non-conventional water supply, and improving energy and
                     resource efficiency.
                     The Medina of Sousse, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, lies within the city’s urban perimeter. While initial assessments
                     confirm the desalination plant expansion is not within or in direct proximity to the site, this will be verified further
                     through the ESIA.
                     The project prioritizes urban and peri-urban coverage in Sfax and Sousse. However, SONEDE’s urban-centric approach
                     risks excluding rural communities having the same water needs.

                     D.2 Overview of Borrower’s Institutional Capacity for Managing Environmental and Social Risks and Impacts
                     SONEDE, the implementing agency, is a state-owned utility under the Ministry of Agriculture (MARHP) responsible for
                     potable water production and distribution nationwide. While it has robust technical capacity in water supply




Public Disclosure
                     infrastructure, SONEDE has limited or no prior experience implementing projects under ESF and does not have an
                     established Environmental and Social Management System (ESMS). It also does not have a dedicated E&S unit with
                     qualified staff and procedures aligned with ESF. To address institutional capacity gaps, the project will support the
                     phased establishment of a functional ESMS within SONEDE under Component 3. The ESMS will be an institutional tool
                     for SONEDE, intended to remain in place beyond the scope of this project. It will define internal procedures for
                     environmental and social risk management, including screening, classification, oversight, compliance monitoring, and
                     reporting, in line with national regulations and international best practices. The project will also mobilize third-party
                     monitoring support to ensure independent oversight and regular supervision of works, especially given the project’s
                     nationwide scope and SONEDE’s unfamiliarity with ESF standards. In addition to Bank supervision, enhanced hands-on
                     support from the Bank’s E&S specialists will be provided during the early phases of implementation to build institutional
                     capacity, support stakeholder engagement, and ensure adherence to ESHS requirements.
                     Broader governance challenges, including trade union influence and slow institutional reform processes, may pose
                     additional implementation risks. These will be mitigated through targeted technical assistance, strategic
                     communications, and proactive stakeholder engagement activities embedded in project design.

                     A.1 Environmental Risk Rating                                                                                Substantial

                     The environmental risk rating is assessed as Substantial, based on the nature and scale of the planned physical
                     interventions, the sensitivity of the project’s coastal setting, and the current limitations in the institutional capacity of
                     the implementing agency. The operation involves extensive civil works in densely populated urban and peri-urban
                     areas, including the rehabilitation of potable water networks and the expansion of a coastal desalination plant. These
                     activities carry the potential for a range of adverse environmental impacts, including pollution of the marine
                     environment, particularly from brine discharge, localized air and noise pollution, generation of hazardous and non-
                     hazardous waste, and high energy consumption during both construction and operation phases. The brine discharge,
                     if not properly managed, may alter salinity levels and affect benthic ecosystems and marine life, including species of
                     ecological or economic importance. While the project does not currently affect designated protected or critical
                     natural habitats, a Critical Habitat Assessment will be conducted as part of the ESIA to confirm the presence or
                     absence of sensitive marine receptors. Although some of these risks are expected to be site-specific and reversible,
                     they may involve a degree of technical complexity and interdependence that required enhanced monitoring and
                     mitigation. At this stage, SONEDE does not have an operational Environmental and Social Management System
                     (ESMS) aligned with ESF and lacks prior experience with Bank-financed projects under the ESF, which may affect its
                     ability to fully meet ESF requirements without targeted support. Institutional strengthening measures, including
                     development of the ESMS, capacity building, and third-party oversight, will be included in the project to ensure that
                     environmental risks are managed in accordance with the ESF.

                     A.2 Social Risk Rating
                     The social risks associated with this project are deemed substantial. Social risks of the two project components
                     entailing Construction activities for desalination and water supply network rehabilitation may adversely impact local




Public Disclosure
                     communities, their livelihoods and wellbeing. Labor and Working Conditions Risks may include labor influx and
                     potential social conflicts regarding job opportunities, unfair treatment, discrimination and wage gaps; lack of
                     documentation regarding the terms and conditions of employment; lack of freedom of association and collective
                     bargaining and absence of grievance mechanism for workers. Child and forced labor are project social risks especially
                     in Sfax where international migration dynamics may exacerbate those risks on irregular children and women migrants
                     in addition to their exposure to Sexual Exploitation and Abuse/Sexual Harassment (SEA/SH) risks. Construction
                     activities may pose community health and safety risks, including accidents and SEA/SH incidents. Project activities
                     may result in access to land, land acquisition and economic displacement. Traditional fishmen activities may be
                     threatened by brine discharges in marine ecosystems. The improvement of water distribution network component
                     may impact cultural heritage including historical buildings and Medinas of Sousse and Sfax . Moreover, there is a
                     serious risk of inadequate and weak consultative processes during project design and implementation because of
                     political obstacles hindering participation and public consultations.

                      ESS10 - Stakeholder Engagement and Information Disclosure                                                      Relevant

                      A systematic approach to engage stakeholders throughout the project lifecycle in addition to a robust, inclusive,
                      accessible and transparent consultative process are required for this project given its geographic scope, components
                      and reform-based activities that will rely on citizens’ inputs and feedback . Ensuring agency and voice of vulnerable
                      groups and individuals through meaningful and inclusive consultations is crucial. Active involvement of CSOs and
                      effective feedback tools are also highly needed. Stakeholders mapping and Stakeholder Engagement Plan (SEP)
                      preparation and updates in line with ESF to outline strategies for effective participation will ensure stakeholders are
                      informed and fully engaged in the decision-making processes. Effective information disclosure is a key component
                      and will require the dissemination of project-related information in accessible formats and languages. A functional
                      and effective GM will be set up to promptly handle complaints for this project.

                      ESS2 - Labor and Working Conditions                                                                            Relevant

                      The project will ensure labor management and working conditions are consistent with the ESF and the Borrower’s

                      labor framework, based on findings of the Comparative Analysis underpinning the Overview Assessment, which
                      covers national policies, and legal and institutional frameworks. The project involves direct, contracted, and
                      potentially primary supply workers. Civil works are expected to be large for desalination and water infrastructure

                      ESS3 - Resource Efficiency and Pollution Prevention and Management                                             Relevant

                      Project activities involve high energy and water use, as well as risks of pollution and waste generation. Key concerns
                      include energy consumption and GHG emissions, brine discharge, air emissions, noise, dust, and the generation of
                      solid and hazardous waste. Water network rehabilitation may cause temporary water losses, increased sediment
                      loads, and accidental leaks; these will be mitigated through phased works, pressure control, and rapid repair
                      protocols. Brine will be managed through GIIP-compliant outfall design and continuous effluent quality monitoring.
                      Energy efficiency measures will be defined and tracked to ensure sustained performance. A waste management plan,
                      including handling and disposal protocols, will also require close oversight. These aspects will be assessed in the ESIA
                      and integrated into the ESMPs. This is particularly important given Tunisia’s institutional gaps in monitoring energy
                      use and waste. Ongoing monitoring and adaptive follow-up will be key.

                     B.3 Other Salient Features

                       Use of Borrower Framework                                                                                           In Part
                       The project will not fully rely on the Borrower’s E&S Framework to meet the requirements of the relevant ESSs.
                       Although Tunisia’s E&S framework has solid legal foundations, the gap analysis identified shortcomings in
                       enforcement, institutional coordination, and E&S risk monitoring. Based on findings of the Comparative Analysis for
                       the Overview Assessment, the team will explore partial reliance on the Borrower framework for specific aspects of
                       ESS2, particularly where the national system shows alignment with the ESF. The project includes a subcomponent to
                       support implementation of the Overview Assessment Action Plan: a roadmap to progressively align the national
                       system with international standards and outline reforms and capacity-building priorities across regulatory,
                       institutional, and technical domains. The project will operationalize selected actions, particularly in pollution control

                     C. Overview of Required Environmental and Social Risk Management Activities

                     C.1 What Borrower environmental and social analyses, instruments, plans and/or frameworks are planned or
                     required by Appraisal?
                     The Borrower will prepare a set of instruments in line with the relevant ESSs to assess and manage Environmental and
                     Social risks. The following instruments will be prepared, consulted upon, and disclosed prior to Appraisal:
                     •        An ESIA/ESMP for the desalination plant, including annexes on brine discharge, hazardous materials and waste
                     management. A Critical Habitat Assessment will be included in the ESIA; if Critical Habitat features are identified, a
                     Biodiversity Management Plan (BMP) will be developed during implementation and referenced in the ESCP.
                     •        An Environmental and Social Management Framework (ESMF) for the entire project to guide the screening, risk
                     classification, and identification of appropriate E&S instruments
                     •        A SEP will be prepared to capture public consultations and put in place a GM for this project
                     •        An ESMS Roadmap will be prepared by appraisal, outlining milestones and responsibilities.

                     The following instruments will be prepared after Appraisal and before Board approval, in line with the streamlined ESF

                     •       A Labor Management Plan (LMP) to address labor-related risks and working conditions.
                     •       A SEA/SH Action Plan to mitigate the risks of sexual exploitation, abuse, and sexual harassment.
