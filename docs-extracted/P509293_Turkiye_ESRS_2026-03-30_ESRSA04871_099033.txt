                     C. Summary Description of Proposed Project Activities
                     The proposed Green and Future Cities (GFC) Project is a coordinated World Bank Group (WBG) initiative that integrates
                     International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (IBRD) and International Finance Corporation (IFC) support to
                     ILBANK and metropolitan municipalities in Türkiye to deliver scalable, sustainable and resilient urban infrastructure and
                     services. The initiative will help participating municipalities address urban development and municipal investment
                     finance challenges by leveraging the international expertise and technical knowledge of IBRD and IFC to provide a
                     broader range of tailored financing instruments. Under the IBRD operation, the Project will deliver a package of
                     investments and technical assistance to the participating metropolitan municipalities to improve access to sustainable

                    Mar 30, 2026                                                                                                      Page 2 of 15
                              The World Bank

                     and resilient municipal infrastructure and services. The Project will have three components: (i) Component 1.
                     Integrated, multi-sectoral investments in participating municipalities: The component will support municipal
                     investments contributing to more livable, productive, and climate resilient urban infrastructure and services in the
                     participating municipalities. The eligible sector list is expected to include urban transport, and water and sanitation. This
                     component will cover Antalya and Konya Metropolitan Municipalities; (ii) Component 2. Strengthening capacity of
                     ILBANK and municipalities for future investment projects: The component will help ILBANK and eligible municipalities
                     strengthen institutional and technical capacity in municipal finance and sustainable, resilient urban management, and
                     prepare technical documentation for viable municipal infrastructure investments. (iii) Component 3. Project
                     Management: The component will finance provision of goods, consulting and non-consulting services, operating costs,
                     and training to ILBANK for Project management and implementation support activities.


                     D. Environmental and Social Overview
                     D.1 Overview of Environmental and Social Project Settings
                     The Project aims to promote sustainable and resilient urban development and capacity at the municipal levels, leading
                     to improved livability, efficiency, productivity, and resilience to disasters in the selected cities. The primary beneficiaries
                     of this Project will be the people living in the Metropolitan municipalities of Antalya and Konya and/or covered by
                     affiliated utilities. Residents of the selected cities will benefit directly from improved livability, accessibility, connectivity,
                     infrastructure, and services, as well as enhanced resilience of the cities to climate- and nature-related hazards. The
                     geographic and socioeconomic conditions of Türkiye make it particularly vulnerable to the impact of climate change and
                     other environmental hazards, making adaptation and resilience high priorities. Türkiye is assessed as highly vulnerable
                     in nine out of 10 climate vulnerability dimensions, compared with the OECD median of two out of 10, in the latest



Public Disclosure
                     Türkiye Country Climate and Development Report (CCDR) (2022). People and assets in Türkiye are increasingly
                     concentrated in cities, with above-average exposure to climate risks exacerbated by inadequate infrastructure and
                     services. As sub-projects are expected to take place in semi-urban and urban areas, there can be risks of impacts on
                     culturally and naturally sensitive areas given the historical importance of some cities and the proximity of areas with
                     important biodiversity such as Konya. While the geographical conditions in municipalities identified may differ, all are
                     densely populated with a growing population and economy. The rapid urban population growth, paired with insufficient
                     consideration for climate risks and mitigation, is resulting in increasing exposure of urban populations and assets to
                     climate hazards and rising urban emission intensity. Antalya contributes about 3.5 percent to national Gross Domestic
                     Product (GDP) and is rapidly urbanizing with strong tourism, agriculture and smart city investments. However, it faces
                     growing risks from rising temperatures, water scarcity, increasing wastewater volumes, heavy precipitation, coastal
                     flooding and wildfires that threaten tourism and urban infrastructure. While Konya is an industrial and logistics hub,
                     especially in agriculture and machinery manufacturing, contributing about 2.1 percent to national GDP, it is highly
                     exposed both to heavy precipitation causing flash floods and drought, groundwater depletion and desertification,
                     affecting agricultural productivity and water security. Both of these municipalities encounter operational problems such
                     as high-water losses, inadequate water treatment facilities, inadequate access to wastewater collection, and lack of
                     wastewater treatment due to deficiencies in financial capacity and insufficient institutional capacity. The rapid
                     urbanization of cities has increased the share of air pollution and over 90% of the emission of harmful compounds into
                     the air is attributed to motor vehicles. Increased population density also has a negative impact on the number and size
                     of green areas. In order to improve the quality of life in cities, it is necessary to adopt new visions for the development
                     of urban space and their effective management.

                     A. Environmental and Social Risk Classification (ESRC)                                                         Substantial


                     A.1 Environmental Risk Rating                                                                                  Substantial
                     The project’s environmental risk rating is “Substantial.” Component 1 will finance linear construction and associated
                     activities (including material sourcing) for urban transport and water and sanitation infrastructure. These investments
                     may adversely affect air, water, and soil quality through discharges and emissions of pollutants if appropriate
                     measures are not applied during site selection, planning, construction, and operation. Linear works may cause traffic



Public Disclosure
                     disruption, utility relocation, and construction-related air, noise, and vibration impacts, which could negatively affect
                     adjacent historical buildings in Antalya and Konya. Construction of some facilities may also lead to temporary or
                     permanent changes in local drainage patterns. Occupational health and safety (OHS) risks are expected for workers
                     operating in or near water, in confined spaces, at heights, and in proximity to moving traffic in dense urban areas.
                     Given the proximity of some cities to areas with important biodiversity values, adverse impacts on biodiversity cannot
                     be ruled out. However, as these locations have long been subject to human presence and activities, fully natural
                     conditions are unlikely to be encountered and potential impacts are expected to be incremental and largely
                     temporary, such as localized habitat degradation or disruption of species movement at the edges of sensitive areas.
                     At the same time, the project is expected to generate significant positive environmental outcomes. These include
                     improved planning for climate action; resource-efficient and resilient urban infrastructure; enhanced protection
                     against floods, droughts, wildfires, and extreme heat; improved water supply and sanitation services; expanded non-
                     motorized transport infrastructure; greening of transport corridors; reduced air and water pollution; and overall
                     improvements in the quality of life for urban residents. ILBANK’s ESMS is fully aligned with national legislation.
                     ILBANK has prepared an Environmental and Social Management Framework (ESMF) that describes ESMS procedures,
                     including initial environmental and social screening against exclusion lists aligned with the IBRD exclusion list. The
                     assigned risk rating determines whether an Environmental and Social Impact Assessment (ESIA) and/or other
                     assessment and management instruments are required. High E&S risk operations will be excluded from financing, and
                     this will be reflected in the E&S instruments and screening protocols. Provisions for screening, assessment,
                     management, and reporting are incorporated in the E&S instruments and the Environmental and Social Commitment
                     Plan (ESCP) agreed with IBRD. ILBANK has already conducted preliminary E&S screening for identified subprojects in
                     urban transport, wastewater treatment and reuse, and construction of wastewater networks. Identified impacts

                     A.2 Social Risk Rating
                     The social risk for the project is classified as “Substantial”. The Project will result in positive social outcomes including
                     improved urban mobility, and enhanced access to safe water and sanitation. Short-term construction employment
                     and longer-term productivity benefits are anticipated, contributing to local economic opportunities and making cities
                     more attractive for investment. Potential key social risks and impacts are: i) land acquisition and restrictions on land
                     use associated with construction of urban transport and water and sanitation, infrastructure (Component 1) may
                     result in temporary or permanent loss of land, land use restrictions, rights-of-way/easements, removal of
                     assets/structures, and physical/economic displacement (loss of livelihoods/income); ii) labor and working conditions
                     risks, including OHS for construction works in dense urban settings (excavation, work at heights, exposure to
                     hazardous substances such as paint, asphalt, asbestos, moving machinery, traffic interface, struck-by hazards, noise




Public Disclosure
                     and dust). While Türkiye has comprehensive labor and OHS legislation, enforcement gaps may occur during
                     implementation; iii) community health and safety risks stemming from construction activities (exposure to
                     contaminants like sludges, wastewater, traffic safety, dust/noise, worksite interface with communities) and possible
                     influx of temporary workers in certain sub-project sites causing increased SEA/SH and security risks; iv) Risks for
                     vulnerable and disadvantaged groups, including elderly persons, persons with disabilities, informal users/occupants,
                     and others who may face barriers to engagement or be disproportionately affected or may not have equitable access
                     to services. Although the project is not expected to create significant adverse impacts on vulnerable groups, their
                     needs and expectations will need to be integrated into project design. While these risks are significant, they are
                     predictable and can be managed well through application of appropriate mitigation measures. Moreover, ILBANK and
                     participating municipalities and utilities have adeqaute experience in similar sectors that will ensure good E&S risks
                     and impacts assessments and management. However, given the identified social risks and impacts, social risk remains
                     Substantial due to the scale and urban complexity of works, potential land acquisition/economic displacement, OHS
                     and community interface risks, and the need for robust enforcement and supervision across multiple sub-projects.
                     Continuous monitoring and adaptive management will be required throughout implementation.

                      ILBANK will be on-lending to municipalities and their affiliated utilities to implement the project activities. ILBANK has

                     C. Overview of Required Environmental and Social Risk Management Activities

                     C.1 What Borrower environmental and social analyses, instruments, plans and/or frameworks are planned or
                     required by implementation?
                     The project will be implemented under the IBRD-approved ILBANK ESMS. An Environmental and Social Commitment
                     Plan (ESCP), and Stakeholder Engagement Plan (SEP) have been prepared by ILBANK and reviewed and cleared by
                     World Bank. ILBANK has also prepared an Environmental and Social Management Framework (ESMF) describing its
                     ESMS, including procedures for cascading risk management to sub-borrowers and contractors. Contractors will be
                     required to prepare, obtain approval for, and implement C-ESMPs consistent with the approved ESMPs prior to
                     commencement of any civil works. The ESMS includes the process of cascading the E&S risk assessment and
                     management at the sub-borrower and contractor’s level and monitoring the compliance by ILBANK. All sub-projects
                     financed by the Bank will be subject to E&S appraisal procedures, screened against the ILBANK exclusion list, and E&S
                     documentation for sub-projects will be disclosed in accordance with the timelines provided in the ESCP.

                     ILBANK shall prepare and submit to the IBRD starting 30 days after the Project effectiveness, and bi-annually (six
                     months) thereafter, regular monitoring reports on environmental, social, health and safety (ESHS) performance of the
                     project, status of preparation and implementation of E&S documents required under the ESCP, stakeholder
                     engagement activities, and functioning of the grievance mechanism(s). The SEP shall be implemented after the loan
                     effective date, and sub-project level SEPs to be prepared and disclosed in accordance with the timelines outlined in the
                     ESCP. The Grievance Mechanism to be established no later than the loan effective date and maintained throughout the
                     project implementation. The GM will include a channel to receive and address grievances related to SEA/SH. GM for
                     project workers shall be established in line with the timelines stated in the ESCP. In case of permanent or temporary



Public Disclosure
                     land acquisition, sub-project level Resettlement Plans are to be prepared and submitted to the Bank for approval.
                     Approved RPs shall be implemented before commencement of any civil works for project activities that involve land
                     acquisition. Compensation as per the RP to be delivered to project affected persons prior to commencement of civil
                     works.

                     ILBANK shall maintain an E&S team under the International Relations Group of ILBANK to manage and coordinate the
                     entire Project with qualified and experienced personnel, resources to support management of ESHS risks. Present
                     staffing includes one ESMS Coordinator, four social specialists and five environmental and OHS specialists, as well as
                     individual E&S and OHS consultants (five social, five environmental and five OHS consultants). This team is responsible
                     for applying ESMS procedures, conducting E&S screening and risk classification, preparing Environmental and Social
                     Due Diligence (ESDD) reports, Environmental and Social Action Plans (ESAP), supervising the E&S performance of sub-
                     projects and reporting E&S results. Participating municipalities (municipalities and utilities) shall establish the Project
                     Implementation Units (PIUs) no later than 30 days after the signing of the relevant sub-loan agreements to carry out
                     the operational and administrative tasks to oversee implementation of the E&S instruments and monitoring progress.
                     PIUs shall allocate resources and recruit qualified staff for environmental, social and OHS. Based on E&S screenings
                     conducted for a handful of sub-projects, municipalities do have environment and social staff in place, however require
