                     C. Summary Description of Proposed Project Activities
                     Ghana has a road network of about 94,203 km, of which 27 percent paved and 73% unpaved. More than 50 percent of
                     the network, including many feeder and secondary roads, is in fair to impassable condition. Road density and coverage
                     are unevenly distributed: the north of the country remains poorly served, while the southern half has greater road
                     density supporting agricultural and livestock production, but often at a poor service level that increases travel distances
                     and costs to markets and limits opportunities. In fact, the population in the north is better connected to neighbor’s
                     countries and has some comparative productivity agricultural advantages. To address these challenges, the Government
                     of Ghana recently launched the Big Push Initiative 2025. The Program is aimed at reducing the investment gap in key
                     sectors and revitalizing aging, poorly maintained infrastructure. Among its priorities are feeder roads, which play a

                    Apr 14, 2026                                                                                                      Page 2 of 12

                     crucial role in linking rural areas to main transport corridors. Investing in feeder roads is essential to connect farms and
                     rural communities with markets, reduce transport costs and post-harvest losses, and improve access to services.
                     Strengthening these networks will boost agricultural productivity, competitiveness, and inclusive growth, while better
                     connecting isolated regions of the country. The proposed operation will support (i) the rehabilitation of selected feeder
                     roads linked to key agricultural value chains, (ii) the maintenance of the rehabilitated roads and road fund reform, and
                     (iii) project management and OHS enhancement. The proposed operation will be structured around 3 components:
                     Component 1: Feeder road rehabilitation Component 2: Road maintenance and support to road fund reform
                     Component 3: Project management Component 4: CERC


                     D. Environmental and Social Overview
                     D.1 Overview of Environmental and Social Project Settings
                     Proposed activities include rehabilitation of feeder roads, the construction of select major bridges, drains, culverts,
                     installation of fiber optics cable (FOC) , road maintenance and road fund reforms, The Project will also provide
                     dedicated support the strengthening of Ghana’s environmental, and social Health and safety institutional capacity and
                     system to improve regulatory compliance and development outcomes.
                     Exact locations and scopes of the rehabilitation and mantainance intervations are yet to be determined, howeverworks
                     will primarily target existing feeder roads both in rural and semi-urban areas.

                     Rural Ghana spans savannah and high forest ecosystems with significant biodiversity and natural resources. While
                     interventions will remain within existing road footprints, dispersion of sites along these ecosystems and habitats,
                     wetlands, streams, and farmland require careful planning and screening to avoid or minimize impacts.



Public Disclosure
                     Improved rural roads can reduce travel time, enhance safety, and facilitate market access for all but also for
                     disadvantaged groups such as women and persons with disabilities while reducing post-harvest losses. Social gains
                     include increased income, better access to services, and potential upward mobility. Risks include seasonal isolation
                     where roads are inadequate, disruption of traditional land use and local dynamics, potential displacement, and
                     tensions. Inclusive planning and community participation especially in markets where women are predominant users,
                     are essential to ensure culturally appropriate, equitable outcomes.
                     Socio-culturally, rural Ghana is deeply communal, traditional, and agrarian, heavily influenced by customary authority,
                     strong family ties, and religious beliefs. It is characterized by subsistence farming, gendered labor roles, rich oral
                     traditions, and, increasingly, challenges from youth out-migration and limited access to modern infrastructure
                     Construction-related risks (noise, dust, waste, traffic safety), resource use, community interactions, and potential
                     impacts on land, flora and fauna, wetlands, water bodies, cultural heritage, and vulnerable groups. Given geographic
                     dispersion, cumulative impacts may arise without robust coordination.
