I. PROJECT STATUS AND RATIONALE FOR RESTRUCTURING
  1. This restructuring seeks to adjust the Performance Based Conditions (PBC), their annual targets, the allocated
     amounts (no change to the total allocated PBC amount), as well as amend the project’s Results Framework to
     align it with the proposed PBC changes. The restructuring proposal builds on the Independent Verification Agent
     (IVA) assessment of PBC baselines, first project year results and reflects the Ministry of Finance and Economic
     Affairs (MoFEA) from February 12, 2025.
  2. The Malawi Water Sanitation Project -1 (MWSP-1) was approved by the Board of Directors on March 23, 2023, in
     the amount of US$145 million (IDA) and became effective on June 6, 2023.
  3. The overall implementation progress of the project is Satisfactory, and it is on track to achieve its development
     objectives. At the end of January 2025, the overall project disbursement stands at 12 percent with all major
     consultancy contracts and anti-cholera works contracts ongoing (the main works contracts await the detailed
     designs to be finalized in 2025).
  4. The project has four Components and their progress to date is as follows:
     a) Component 1 – Water Improvements (Total cost: US$75 million (IDA), of which US$5 million has been
     disbursed). Detailed design of Non-Revenue Water investments is ongoing.
     b) Component 2 – Priority Sanitation Improvements (Total cost: US$34 million (IDA), of which US$4 million has
     been disbursed). Detailed design of sanitation improvements is ongoing.
     c) Component 3 – Institutional Capacity Strengthening (Total cost: US$35 million (IDA), of which US$7 million has
     been disbursed). Good implementation progress so far.
     d) Component 4 – Technical Assistance and Project Management (Total cost: US$1.5, of which US$0.92 million
     has been disbursed).
  5. Performance of the implementing entities in terms of fiduciary, safeguards, and monitoring and evaluation are
     summarized as follows:
     a) Fiduciary Performance. The current financial management rating of the Project is Satisfactory, and there are no
     outstanding audits under the Project. All audit and financial management reports have been submitted on time
     and found to be acceptable to the World Bank. The procurement rating is at Moderately Satisfactory level.
     b) Safeguards Performance. The current environmental and social safeguards rating is Satisfactory. All required
     Environmental and Social Management Plans (ESMPs), received so far, have been cleared by the World Bank and
     publicly consulted and disclosed. The Grievance Redress Mechanism (GRM) log is submitted as part of the
     Quarterly Project Progress Report (QPPR).
  6. The rationale for the proposed changes of the PBCs is to update the indicators with the baseline assessment
     completed by the IVA (big differences with the baseline assessment during project preparation on some of the
     indicators), align their annual targes starting from the confirmed baselines, redistribute resource allocation
     between PBCs based on level of efforts, and amend the project’s Results Framework to align it with the PBC
     changes. This will allow to establish proper PBC indicators and enable full utilization of the allocated performance
     incentive resources under the project. The restructuring proposes to replace PBC#1: Non-Revenue Water (NRW)
     reduction indicator with a Reduction in Energy Consumption per cubic meter of water produced indicator as a
     more effective and context-appropriate operational efficiency indicator. Still, the NRW will be kept as a Project
     Development Objective (PDO) indicator in the Project Results Framework (PRF), since it is a good performance
     and efficiency indicator, and the project is aiming to achieve significant reduction of water losses, leading to
     reduction of consumed energy for water production.

II. DESCRIPTION OF PROPOSED CHANGES


    7. In line with the rationale provided in the previous sections and in response to the official request of the MoFEA
       dated February 12, 2025, this Restructuring Paper seeks approval to proceed with:

            PBC#1: Reduction in Energy Consumption, kWh/m3 of water produced.
         b) Amend annual targets for PBCs #2, #4 and #5.
         c) Redistribute resource allocations between PBCs, changes in PBCs #1, #2, #3, #4 and #5.
