1. The Republic of Peru (“the Borrower”) will implement the Water Security and Circularity Multi-phase
   Programmatic Approach (MPA) – Phase I: Wastewater Management and Circular Economy Project
   (the Project), with the involvement of the Ministry of Housing, Construction and Sanitation (MVCS, by
   its acronym in Spanish), in coordination with the National Urban Sanitation Program (PNSU, by its
   acronym in Spanish) and with the Safe Water Program for Lima and Callao (PASLC, by its acronym in
   Spanish), as set out in the Loan Agreement. The International Bank for Reconstruction and
   Development (“the Bank”) has agreed to provide financing for the MPA, as set forth in the Agreement.

2. The Borrower shall ensure that the Project is carried out in accordance with the Environmental and
   Social Standards (EAS) and this Environmental and Social Commitment Plan (ESCP), in a manner
   acceptable to the Bank. The ESCP is a part of the Agreement. Unless otherwise defined in this ESCP,
   capitalized terms used in this ESCP have the meanings ascribed to them in the Agreement.

3. Without limitation to the foregoing, this ESCP sets out material measures and actions that the
   Borrower shall carry out or cause to be carried out, including, as applicable, their respective
   timeframes; institutional, staffing, training, monitoring, and reporting arrangements; and grievance
   management. The ESCP also sets out the environmental and social (E&S) documents that shall be
   prepared or updated, consulted, disclosed and implemented under the Project, in accordance with
   the Bank ESF Environmental and Social Standards (ESSs), in form and substance acceptable to the
   Bank. Such E&S documents may be revised from time to time with prior written agreement by the
   Bank. As provided for under the referred Agreement, the Borrower shall ensure that there are
   sufficient funds available to cover the costs of implementing the ESCP.

4. As agreed by the Bank and the Borrower, this ESCP may be revised from time to time, if necessary, to
   reflect adaptive management of Project changes or unforeseen circumstances or in response to
   Project performance. In such circumstances, the Bank and the Borrower, agree to update the ESCP to
   reflect these changes through an exchange of letters signed between the Bank and the Borrower or
   the Coordinators of each Project Implementing Unit (PIU) established in the MVCS. The Borrower,
   shall promptly disclose the updated ESCP.

5. The subsection on "Indicators of Readiness for Implementation" below identifies the actions and
   measures to be monitored to assess Project readiness to begin implementation in accordance with
   this ESCP. Nevertheless, all actions and measures of this ESCP shall be implemented as set out in the
   "Timeframe" column below, regardless of whether they are listed in the referred subsection.

     The borrower shall comply with, and ensure compliance with, national labor legislation, in
     accordance with international labor treaties ratified by the Peruvian State on labor matters and
     this ESS 2. This includes measures to protect workers, the prohibition of forced labor and child
     labor, the prevention and management of Sexual Harassment (SH) and Sexual Exploitation and
     Abuse (SEA), Occupational Health and Safety (OHS) measures, a Code of Conduct applicable to
     Project Workers (including direct and contractor/subcontractor workers), and a specific grievance
     redress mechanism for Project workers, among other aspects.

     To this end, the following measures shall be verified and implemented:

       i.    Ensure that the Project workers (including "direct workers" assigned to the PIUs) receive
             the benefits and protections provided by law, and to which they would be entitled under
             their hiring modality or the regime that should apply to them, based on the actual or real
             nature of their relationship with their employing entity in the context of the services they
             provide.

      ii.    Provide project workers (especially "direct workers" and "contractor workers") with a
             written copy of their contracts (whether they are of a labor or even civil nature, such as
             "service providers");

      iii.   Provide "project workers" (especially "direct workers" and "contractor workers") with clear
             and understandable information and documentation about the terms and conditions of
             employment, including their contracting regime, their rights, information about rights
             related to working hours, wages or remuneration, overtime (as applicable), and the benefits
             to which they are entitled; as well as written notice of termination of employment with due
             notice and details of severance or service time compensation payments, if applicable.

      iv.    Implement occupational health and safety management measures (including personal
             protective equipment and emergency preparedness and response) for contractors. Health
             and safety management measures will be in accordance with EAS2 and the General
             Environmental, Health and Safety Guidelines (GMASS), and other relevant Good
             International Industry Practice (GIIP) and, as appropriate, GMASS and other industry-
             specific GIIP.

       v.    Implement measures, as appropriate, to, inter alia: i) prevent the use of all forms of forced
             labor and child labor; ii) enable direct workers and/or contractors to benefit from, inter alia,
             access to grievance and complaint mechanisms without fear of retaliation, and effective
             freedom to form and join direct worker and/or contractor organizations or alternative
