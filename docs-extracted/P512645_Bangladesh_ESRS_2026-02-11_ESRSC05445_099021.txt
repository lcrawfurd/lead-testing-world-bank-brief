                     C. Summary Description of Proposed Project Activities
                     The proposed US$300 million project aims to enhance foundational literacy and numeracy (FLN) for all children and
                     strengthen education system performance from pre-primary to grade 5. It will improve FLN and teaching practices
                     through a national rollout of the FLN package with teaching materials, teacher professional development, structured
                     pedagogy, and mentoring. To reduce inequities, it will support targeted learning acceleration programs, phased
                     expansion of quality pre-primary education in disadvantaged districts, and improved school infrastructure, especially
                     WASH facilities. The project will also strengthen education system performance by enhancing teacher and school

                     up.


                     D. Environmental and Social Overview
                     D.1 Overview of Environmental and Social Project Settings
                     The IPF component will finance consulting and non-consulting services to support the capacity development of relevant
                     counterparts and agencies in specific areas, including learning assessments, innovative pilots in AI in education, climate
                     change and air quality, strengthening data system management fiduciary and safeguard management and strengthen
                     coordination among various government entities. The activities of the TA will not be any location specific and will be
                     providing technical assistance to support knowledge management and capacity development activities.

                     D.2 Overview of Borrower’s Institutional Capacity for Managing Environmental and Social Risks and Impacts
                     The Department of Primary Education (DPE) within MoPME will serve as the executing agency and will have overall
                     responsibility for policy guidance and oversight for program implementation. An Inter-ministerial Program Steering
                     Committee (IPSC) will be established under MoPME to oversee the coordination, monitoring and implementation of
                     LEAP-BD. A Program Management Unit will be formed within DPE, headed by and consisting mainly of government
                     officers, for implementation, monitoring and supervision of activities.

                     MoPME and DPE have experience of working with IDA with several projects under PforR, ESF and safeguards (ASSET,

                     II. SCREENING OF POTENTIAL ENVIRONMENTAL AND SOCIAL RISKS AND IMPACTS

                     A. Environmental and Social Risk Classification (ESRC)                                                           Moderate


                     A.1 Environmental Risk Rating                                                                                    Moderate
                     The IPF will finance technical assistance, consulting and non-consulting services, and capacity development in areas
                     such as student learning assessments, data system management, AI pilots in education, and evaluation studies. No
                     civil works or activities with physical footprints are included. The main potential environmental risks relate to indirect
                     or downstream effects of studies and pilots, e.g., if digitalization strategies or AI pilots later inform infrastructure
                     investments or electronic waste handling. These risks are expected to be mitigated through standard good practices.
                     The environmental risk has been determined as Moderate and would be reassessed once more clarity on scope of
                     work is available during appraisal stage.

                     The IPF component will finance consulting and non-consulting services, including learning assessments, innovative
                     pilots in AI in education, climate change and air quality, strengthening data system management fiduciary and
                     safeguard management and strengthening coordination among various government entities. There will be no civil

                      strengthen capacity in areas such as learning assessments, AI pilots in education, climate change and air quality
                      initiatives, and data, fiduciary, and safeguard system management. This component will not support construction
                      activities, land acquisition, or resettlement, nor will it affect biodiversity, natural habitats, ecosystems, or cultural
                      heritage. However, the studies supported by the component might have some ES risk related to the downstream

                      ESS10 - Stakeholder Engagement and Information Disclosure                                                        Relevant

                      The key stakeholders will include officials at DPE and other implementing partners, teachers’ organization, persons
                      interested in education, NGOs working in primary education sectors, researchers/academics, curriculum developers,
                      students etc. The borrower will identify relevant stakeholders and engage with them during the project. Information
                      regarding the project design, various risks and impacts and intended outcome will be shared. The inputs from the
                      beneficiaries will be taken into cognizance during the project design. A grievance redress mechanism will be
                      implemented to address concerns and complaints about the project. The GRM will also be adapted to address SEA/SH
                      grievances using a survivor centric approach.

                      ESS2 - Labor and Working Conditions                                                                              Relevant

                      individual consultants as Direct Workers for various consultancy, research, studies, assessments and development of
                      academic curriculum who would qualify as project workers as per the ESS2. Given the types of project personnel and
                      risk profile the Labor Management Procedures (LMP) will be integrated as a part of ESCP incorporating aspects such
                      as applicability of labor laws including workplace harassment, non-discrimination and equal opportunity, grievance
