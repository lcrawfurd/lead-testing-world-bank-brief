                     C. Summary Description of Proposed Project Activities
                     The Project aims to improve livelihoods in Aksu district—particularly for vulnerable groups—by expanding sanitation
                     access, creating jobs, and strengthening climate resilience. With 80 percent of the population lacking adequate
                     sanitation, it will help reduce disease and pollution while generating employment through FSM and reuse-related
                     opportunities. Treated sludge will be promoted as fertilizer for non-food crops, cutting reliance on costly chemical
                     inputs and supporting climate adaptation. Community-led efforts will drive health, economic, and environmental
                     improvements, building a more resilient and empowered Aksu community. The Project includes three components: The
                     Project includes three components: Component 1: Developing Sanitation and Fecal Sludge Treatment Infrastructure will

                     Dissemination will finance overall project implementation, project monitoring and evaluation, and sharing of project
                     learnings and results.


                     D. Environmental and Social Overview
                     D.1 Overview of Environmental and Social Project Settings
                     The project will be implemented in Aksu district, situated in the northeastern part of the Kyrgyz Republic, within the
                     Issyk-Kul Region. To the west, the district borders Lake Issyk-Kul, the world's second-largest saline lake, which is
                     recognized as a Ramsar site due to its globally significant biodiversity. The district consists of the district center Aksu
                     (formerly: Teploklyuchenka) and and four Aiyl Aimaks encompassing 44 villages. As of 2024, the district had a
                     population of 68,421 of which 48.7 percent women. The majority (80 percent) reside in rural villages, and 20 percent
                     (14,153 people) live in the district center. Agriculture forms the backbone of the District’s economy and employs about
                     75 percent of the population. . The district’s infrastructure is underdeveloped, especially in the remote mountainous



Public Disclosure
                     areas, and limited access to clean water remains a major socioeconomic barrier, impacting public health and quality of
                     life. The situation regarding sanitation is even more alarming. In Teploklyuchenka, a wastewater treatment plant
                     (WWTP) and sewer network were built in the 1960s, but only 20 percent of the population are connected to the sewer
                     system. The remaining 80 percent of the population relies on onsite sanitation systems such as septic tanks and pit
                     latrines. The project would complement the proposed Water Supply and Sanitation Universal Access Program-1 Project
                     (WASUAP, P500620) with some investments aimed at increasing access to safely managed sanitation services at the
                     household level in Aksu district.

                     D.2 Overview of Borrower’s Institutional Capacity for Managing Environmental and Social Risks and Impacts
                     The proposed grant-financed activity will be implemented by the State Institution for Drinking Water and Wastewater
                     Disposal (SIDWSWD) under the Ministry of Water Resources, Agriculture, and Processing Industry (MWRAPI) of the
                     Kyrgyz Republic. The existing Project Implementation Unit (PIU) of the SIDWSWD, responsible for the implementation of
                     the ongoing World Bank-financed Climate Resilient Water Services Project (CRWSP, P173734) will manage the proposed
                     WASUAP and grant-financed activity. The SIDWSWD has gained some experience with the Environmental and Social
                     Framework (ESF) through managing the Bank-funded project. The environmental and social performance of the CRWSP
                     was rated satisfactory as per the last Implementation Status and Results (ISR) report. The existing PIU of the SIDWSWD
                     will be expanded and further strengthened to manage the proposed WASUAP and the JSDF grant-financed project. The
                     PIU will be restructured into a Project Coordination Unit (PCU) at the central level based in Bishkek, with a dedicated
                     team of specialists, including an environmental specialist, a social development specialist, an institutional development
                     specialist, an M&E specialist, and a communications specialist. At the district level, a technical team will be established
                     to support project implementation, which will include an environmental and social officer. The technical teams will work
                     closely with the PCU at the SIDWSWD and be responsible for day-to-day project oversight, including environmental and

                     II. SCREENING OF POTENTIAL ENVIRONMENTAL AND SOCIAL RISKS AND IMPACTS

                     A. Environmental and Social Risk Classification (ESRC)                                                          Substantial


                     A.1 Environmental Risk Rating                                                                                   Substantial
                     The proposed project will finance a wastewater and faecal sludge treatment plant designed to treat and safely
                     dispose of human waste collected from sewered and non-sewered sanitation systems and thus is expected to have
                     overall environmental and social benefits. However, while they provide essential sanitation services, the construction
                     and operations these facilities can pose significant environmental risks and impacts that must be carefully considered
                     to ensure safe operations. The potentially significant environmental risks resulting from the proposed project include:
                     (i) Soil contamination; (ii) Surface Water Pollution; (iii) Ground Water and Soil contamination; (iv) Air Pollution Faecal
                     sludge (v) Odor Nuisance;. (vi) Greenhouse Gas Emissions; and (Vii) Resource Depletion,as the processing plants
                     require water, energy, and chemicals. These proposed activities are small in size and geographical spread. The
                     envisaged impacts are site specific and can be mitigated by routine measure including the choice of technology,
                     building designs and implementation of site specific ESF instruments such as ESIA/ESMP and national building codes.
                     On-site sewerage infrastructure, including septic tanks and pipe connections, entails a variety of risks that must be
                     mitigated. These risks which could materialize during construction and operation include bad location, poorly
                     designed, constructed, or maintained septic systems which can lead to the seepage of untreated wastewater into the

                     exposure to harmful substances, physical injuries, and hazardous working conditions. During operation, there are also
                     OHS related risks due to exposure of workers and communities to pathogens and parasites. To mitigate these risks,
                     the client will prepare ESMF and site specific ESMPs that will be consistent with the risk mitigation hierarchy of the
                     ESF and GIIP.

                     A.2 Social Risk Rating
                     The Social Risk is rated as Moderate based on the information about the project to date. The project will finance the
                     construction of a wastewater and fecal sludge treatment facility located in the district center of Aksu, capable of
                     safely processing waste from septic tanks and pit latrines from households in the surrounding rural area. Additionally,
                     local residents, particularly vulnerable groups (such as women and the unemployed), will be trained and employed to
                     participate in fecal sludge management (including collection, transport, treatment, and end use/reuse). The Project is
                     expected to have positive social impacts, such as improved hygiene and sanitation standards, and will create
                     healthier, more resilient, and economically empowered communities. The potential exclusion risks of beneficiaries
                     from the Project activities will be managed through participatory monitoring and evaluation, public awareness, and
                     stakeholder engagement under Component 3. However, the Project may adversely impact vulnerable groups due to
                     new costs and user fees for sludge collection and transportation. These financial burdens need to be carefully
                     assessed and mitigated during project implementation to ensure that they do not disproportionately affect
                     vulnerable populations, drawing on the approaches used in active water supply projects to support low-income
                     families. Regarding potential land acquisition and resettlement impacts, as well as workers' and community health

                     B. Relevance of Standards and Policies at Concept Stage

                     B.1 Relevance of Environmental and Social Standards

                      ESS1 - Assessment and Management of Environmental and Social Risks and Impacts                                 Relevant

                      The construction and operation of the facility can pose environmental risks and impacts including: (i) soil
                      contamination, water pollution, odor, emission, wastes and OHS related impacts. The engineering designs are not
                      available, and sites are not known at this point. The recipient will prepare an ESMF that set out the principles,
                      procedures, steps and timelines to prepare site specific ESF instruments. The ESMF shall have Exclusion List depicting
                      ineligible activities and screen out locations that can impact biodiversity. The recipient shall prepare site specific
                      ESMPs with annexes on Septic Tanks Code of Practice, IFCs Environmental, Health, and Safety Guidelines for Water
                      and Sanitation, and WBG 2022 publication on Wastewater Treatment and Reuse- A Guide to Help Small Towns Select
                      Appropriate Options. Potential social risks include land acquisition, resettlement of vulnerable groups and labor
                      issues. The ESMF, SEP, a LMP, and RPF will be prepared and disclosed prior to appraisal.

                      ESS10 - Stakeholder Engagement and Information Disclosure                                                      Relevant

                      Key stakeholders are those benefiting from the Project, including the municipalities and households in Aksu district.

                      ESS2 - Labor and Working Conditions                                                                            Relevant

                      The project will include direct and contracted workers. The construction, installation and operation of septic tanks
                      pose significant OHS risks to workers. These risks include exposure to harmful substances, manual handling
                      ergonomic risks, slip, trip, fall hazards, restricted movement, physical injuries, and hazardous working conditions.
                      Mitigating OHS risks will include the provision of PPE, training, safe working practices, emergency preparedness, and
                      effective site management. These potential risks and mitigation measures will be in the ESMF, ESMP and other ESF
                      due diligence instruments. No significant risks related to labor influx, child and forced labor are expected. The SEA/SH
                      risk is assessed as moderate. The ESMF will include sections of the Bank's EHSG (e.g., EHS checklists and codes of
                      conduct). The PIU will prepare and disclose LMP to manage labor-related risks. Site-specific ESMPs will incorporate
                      LMP requirements, Code of Conduct, SEA/SH prevention, and workers’ GRM.
