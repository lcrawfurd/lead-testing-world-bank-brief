I. PROJECT STATUS AND RATIONALE FOR RESTRUCTURING


A. Program status
1. The Burkina Faso Water Supply and Sanitation Program - P164345 (Programme d’Approvisionnement en Eau et
   d’Assainissement, PAEA) aims to improve access to water supply and sanitation services in targeted areas of the
   country. The Program was approved in June 2018 and became effective in December 2018. The IDA financing of US$300
   million, combining a US$250 million Credit and a US$50 million Grant, contributes to a Program totaling approximately
   US$365 million (including US$65 million representing GoBF and beneficiaries’ contribution), under the Government’s
   National programs for Water Supply and Sanitation, and Integrated Water Resources Management (Programme National
   pour la Gestion integrée des Ressources en Eau, PN-GIRE). The PAEA is implemented by the Ministry in charge of water
   and by the national urban water utility (Office National de l’Eau et de l’Assainissement, ONEA). These entities have
   established fully staffed implementation units.

2. The Program's progress towards the Development Objective (PDO) and Implementation Progress (IP) are both rated
   Moderately Satisfactory. Technical, Disbursement Linked Indicators (DLI), and Monitoring and Evaluation System ratings
   are Satisfactory. Fiduciary Systems have been rated Moderately Satisfactory since 2019 due to delays in procurement
   and management issues. The management issues are mostly related to urban water supply activities implemented by
   the national water utility, ONEA, which face budget overruns and delays in the procurement process.

3. Program disbursement reached 92.4 percent two months before the planned closing date of June 2025. An approval of
   the proposed restructuring will help to smoothly prepare additional financing for the Program.

4. As of February 2025, the Program provided drinking water and sanitation facilities to 1,591,749, out of the 2,507,960
   targeted by June 2025. The four key result areas of the Program (Water Supply, Sanitation, Water Resources and Human
   Capital) have reached varying levels: The water supply and sanitation results area have reached respectively 70.18
   percent and 51.5 percent of their targets. The water resource knowledge area has also shown substantial progress,
   including 30 out of 50 productive boreholes achieved (60 percents of the target). Regarding the human capital result
   area, the Program has already surpassed its objectives with 136 graduates (of which 88 percents securing full-time jobs
   as civil servants at the Ministry of Water). The detailed results are summarized as follows :
   (a) Improved access to water supply (RA#1). As of February 2025, the Program provided improved water sources to
       1,128,389 people (70.18 percents of the target). This includes 766,569 urban residents (100.4 percent of the target),
       361,820 rural residents (43 percent of the target) and an overall number of 43,750 IDPs. These results were achieved
       through over 120,000 new household connections, more than 550 new functional standpipes, and about 700 new
       boreholes equipped with handpumps in rural and urban areas. Overall, the water production capacity increased by
       23,500 cubic meters per day (81 percent of the 2023 revised target). The remaining 19 percent are delayed due to
       issues in urban water supply center contracts.
   (b) Improved Access to sanitation (RA#2). The Program has provided sanitation access to 463,360 people, which is 51.48
       percent of the 2023 revised target. This includes 341,690 urban residents (59 percent of the target) and 121,670 rural
       residents (36.9 percent of the target) provided with improved household latrines, including 13,390 IPDs. Community
       latrine achievement in schools and health care centers remains low, increasing from 94 facilities in June 2024 to 290 in
       February 2025, still far from the target of 4,080. Financial challenges faced by construction companies caused delays,
       but a contract amendment for intermediate payments has accelerated progress.

   (c) Improved access to reliable information on Water Resources (RA#3) shows some progress but corresponding
       infrastructure remains delayed. The December 2023 restructuring has significantly reduced the activities of this result
       area due to procurement delays and security concerns in some sites. Despite these challenges, the Program has made
       significant progress with productive boreholes in the hard rock aquifer. Six out of fifty boreholes have been verified by
       the Independent Verification Agency (IVA), and twenty-four additional boreholes will be submitted for verification by
       the end of FY25. Deep boreholes construction up to 800 meters is underway to better understand the groundwater
       resources. However, the completion of these works is scheduled for Q3 FY26, beyond the current closing date of the
       Program.
   (d) Human Capital strengthening (RA#4) fully achieved or surpassed its targets. A total of 186 individuals registered in
       technical training programs, with 124 of them (115 percents of the revised 2023 target) graduating and all being
       integrated as civil servants (115 percents of the revised 2023 target). Additionally, 3675 people benefited from short
       training sessions, far exceeding the initial target of 3504. The program also provided technical and training programs to
       27 women (80 percents of the target). Furthermore, 11 individuals graduated from specialized training programs (100
       percents of the revised 2023 target), and 3 from doctoral programs, with one completed (33 percents of the revised
       2023 target).

5. Supporting resilience in a changing context. The PAEA has played an important role in Burkina Faso in contributing to
   improved access to water and sanitation services and promoting hygiene practices in both urban and rural areas. These
   efforts have been particularly crucial in contributing to cover the IDPs need for water and sanitation service delivery. The
   Program has provided water supply and sanitation services to 57,140 IDPs so far.

6. Security. The results described above were obtained despite a fragile implementation period impacted by a deteriorating
   security environment affecting the Sahel region and Burkina Faso in particular. Various security incidents slowed down
   implementation starting in 2019, with particularly challenging periods from 2021 to date, including theft of equipment
   and the kidnapping of workers1. The client, with the Bank’s support, addresses these challenges by integrating flexible
   measures and alternative options including the selection of new sites when initial ones become inaccessible

7. Independent Verification of the Program: The International Institute of Water and Environmental Engineering (2iE)
   remains the Program’s Independent Verification Agent (IVA). The eleventh campaign of verification of the Disbursement
   Linked Indicators (DLIs) achievement has been held in December 2024 and the twelfth one is being implemented for
   completion in May 2025.

8. Environmental and social (E&S). At this stage of implementation, the Program is not encountering significant
   environmental and social issues. According to the latest ISR in March 2025, the E&S rating remained Moderately
   Satisfactory. Measures are being taken to suspend work during the rainy season to prevent accidents in open pits like
   those that occurred in June and July 20222. The GRM exists and is operational with the support provided by the Regional

9. Fiduciary aspects. At the time of the proposed restructuring, there was no overdue audit, and the country was not subject
   to any ongoing suspension of disbursements. However, urban activities implemented by ONEA have been facing significant
   delays, due to budget overruns and delays in signing contracts addenda. The fiduciary audit commissioned by the Bank
   revealed an overrun of EURO 41 million on the allocated budget for the drinking water component, due to contract
   mismanagement and governance issues within ONEA. A technical audit will be done as a follow-up action to identify
   capacity gaps and define corrective measures as appropriate.

B. Rationale for Restructuring
10. The restructuring of the Burkina Faso Water Supply and Sanitation Program (P164345) is required to extend the Program
    closing date to prepare an additional financing while streamlining the matrix of DLI to ensure that resources are allocated
    efficiently for the remaining activities.
   (a) Extension of Program Closing Date: The Program is currently scheduled to close on June 30, 2025. The GoBF requested
       in its letter dated November 11, 2024, a twenty-four-month extension to implement the ongoing activities in the parent
       Program and new activities from additional financing. The AF still being under preparation, the current extension will
       be limited to twelve months to achieve the AF approval.
   (b) Reallocation Between DLIs and the Result Framework adjustment : The current allocation of funds and activities are
       based on the 2023 restructuring. After two years of implementation, a new reallocation is necessary to better align the
       Program's resources with Government priorities, needs and achievements. The proposed reallocation will ensure that
       the most advanced and critical activities receive adequate funding and support, enhancing the Program's overall
       impact. Detailed information of DLI cancellation and reallocations is summarized in Table 2. DLI3 on the adoption of
       urban water is canceled due to implementation issues in the current political and social context. DLI4 and DLI5 on
       community latrines construction and maintenance are respectively decreased and canceled. The corresponding
       amounts are reallocated toward the three following DLIs : the increase of water production capacity (DLI2); the number
       of people provided with access to an improved water source (DLR 1) and the strengthening of human capital (DLR7).
       The reallocation will be reflected in the implementation schedule and the Results Framework. It will positively impact
       on the disbursement estimate to ensure that resources are allocated efficiently for the remaining activities.

A. Summary of proposed changes
11. This restructuring paper seeks the approval of the Division Director to undertake the following changes: (i) extend the
    Program closing date; (ii) proceed to changes in the Matrix of DLI to perform reallocations between DLIs/DLRs; and (iii)

    amend the results framework, the disbursement estimates and the implementation schedule to reflect the changes in
    the Matrix of DLIs.

B. Change in closing date
12. The Program credit and grant closing date will be extended by twelve (12) months, from the current June 30, 2025, to
    June 30, 2026, in response to the GoBF’s request in its letter dated November 11, 2024. The Financing Agreements (Grant
    No. D330-BF and Credit No. 6255-BF) will be amended to reflect this change.

C. Reallocation and/or Change on DLI and Results Framework adjustment
13. The intermediate indicators’ targets within the Program’s Results Framework have been updated to accurately reflect the
    actual implementation of activities since the last restructuring in December 2023. The different changes are described as
    follows:

14. The improved access to water supply results area (RA#1) has achieved significant progress in urban areas. As a result,
    adjustments will be made to increase the number of beneficiaries in urban areas by +266,644 and decrease those in rural
    areas by -76,180, leading to a net global increase of 190,464 and an updated target of 1,798,424. The water production
    capacity constructed under the Program will also be increased from 28,902.28 cubic meters per day to 33,553.50 cubic
    meters per day to align with the expected outcomes. The validation and adoption of a New Urban Water Tariff Structure
    (DLR.3.4 and DLR.3.5) will be canceled due to implementation issues in the current political and social context. However,
    policy dialogue for tariff structure adjustment will continue. The total amount of EUR 5,786,798 allocated to these 2 DLRs
    will be reallocated to DLR 1.1, resulting in an increase of 90,137 additional people having access to improved water sources
    in urban areas. The related DLIs 1.1, 1.2, and 2.1 and 2.2 (split DLI) will be updated accordingly to reflect these changes.

15. The improved access to sanitation results area (RA#2) covers household and community sanitation facilities.
    Implementation has been slow, particularly in rural areas. The restructuring will reduce the target for household latrines
    by 25.5 percents, affecting 100,000 beneficiaries in urban areas and 130,000 in rural areas. The number of community
    cabins in rural areas will also be reduced from 4,080 to 2,594, a decrease of 36.4 percents. Contracts for rural areas have
    now been awarded, and amendments signed to address the cash flow issues raised by the contractors. The pace of work
    has improved, but completing 4,080 cabins would take too long (beyond FY27). The related DLIs 4.1, 4.2, 5.1, 5.2, 5.3 will
    be updated accordingly, reallocating funds to improved access to water supply results area (RA#1). The incoming additional
    financing will address the reduction in the number of people benefiting from sanitation facilities through four fecal sludge
    treatment plants expected to increase the number of people with access to safely managed sanitation by 960,000.

16. Improved access to reliable information on Water resources results area (RA#3). The 2023 restructuring has substantially
    reduced the activities of this RA due to insecurity in the initial exploration and several unsuccessful procurement processes
    that delayed associated work. Only achievable DLIs, such as seismic campaign and productive boreholes, were maintained.
    The funds canceled were reallocated to Water Supply and Human Capital Results Areas. As of February 2025, productive
    boreholes in the hard rock aquifer have significantly increased whereas the seismic campaigns did not progress. The
    proposed restructuring will reduce the number of positive boreholes from 50 to 30 and will cancel the number of seismic
    campaigns. The related DLIs 6.1 and 6.2 will be updated accordingly, and funds reallocated to increase the water
    production capacity, the number of people with access to water supply and to strengthen the human capital in the water
    sector.

17. The Human Capital strengthening results area (RA#4) has been showing good results since Program inception and the
    2023 restructuring. It is readjusted to (i) consider the number of beneficiaries who successfully completed the technical
