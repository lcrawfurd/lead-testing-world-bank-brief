1 Introduction

1-1 Background of the Project

1-1-1 Project Objectives

This project aims to reduce carbon emissions from the transportation and water sectors,
enhance the carbon sequestration capacity of local ecosystems, improve the accessibility
of national wetland parks and green spaces, and provide sustainable economic activity
opportunities related to environmental restoration to mitigate climate change, enhance
climate adaptation and resilience, and improve urban livability by improving ecosystems
and implementing nature-based solutions. Under a professional and expert team's
guidance, the project will establish and improve the carbon emission monitoring and
accounting system in Guiyang and format an innovative demonstration model for green
and low-carbon development in Guiyang.

1-1-2 Construction Content and Activities

The project content includes two parts: one is hardware, such as infrastructure
construction, and the other is software, such as low-carbon planning system research,
project management, and capacity building. The project construction content is divided
into three sub-projects: low-carbon planning system and study, Aha Lake low-carbon
and nature-oriented city demonstration area construction, project management, and
capacity building. The composition of the sub-projects and the specific construction
content are shown in Table 1-1. The project construction period is 5 years (2026-2030),
and the project's total investment is about RMB 2.473 billion, of which RMB 1.8 billion
is from the World Bank Financed and RMB 673 million is from domestic matching
funds. The project construction location map is shown in Figure 1-1.
(1) Sub-project 1: Promoting low-carbon urban transport for livable cities
This sub-project mainly reduces carbon emissions, improves travel convenience and
environmental quality, and creates sustainable urban transportation through intelligent
management, green infrastructure, and policy support. The construction content includes
constructing a slow-moving system, a public transportation system and charging service

                                           1
facilities, and improving the urban transportation road network. The specific
construction content includes:
①Newly built main line, radial lines, and connecting lines of the greenway around the
lake: The main line is 18.2km long and 7m wide, and the three radial lines are 4.5km long.
The total length of the greenway connection line is 25.3km, connecting the Xiaowanhe
Greenway, rail stations and other areas in Yunyan District to form a slow-moving network
covering the entire area of Aha Lake.
②Construction of cycling stations around the Aha lake. The content of this sub project is
to construct 17 cycling stations, including toilets, safety and emergency response,
environmental sanitation, rest, bicycle rental, barrier free facilities, tourism reception,
first aid, etc.
③Greening and Landscape Project around the Lake. This sub-project mainly involves
the restoration of green plants and landscapes along the greenway. The total area of the
green plant landscape restoration project is 118000 square meters, with 94 landscapes
belts.
④Supporting services and construction of municipal facilities around the lake. Construct
supporting water supply, drainage, strong electricity, telecommunications, and lighting
facilities along the lake greenway; Supporting facilities such as science education,
environmental sanitation, rest and recreation, and activities for the elderly and children;
Upgrade and renovate the water supply facilities in the wetland park.
⑤Construction of public transportation system and charging service facilities. New
public transportation facilities and charging service facilities will be built, and existing
roads will be upgraded to improve the traffic conditions and efficiency of roads and buses.
⑥Improvement of urban transportation network. Constructing or upgrading key
connections around Aha Lake to the main urban area, reducing congestion to the main
urban area of Nanmingyun, thereby improving travel efficiency while reducing
greenhouse gas emissions.
(2) Sub-project 2: Urban solutions to alleviate, adapt, and promote biodiversity
development
This sub-project mainly aims to protect and strengthen urban ecosystems and

                                             2
biodiversity, enhance the ecological quality of the city, and promote the prosperity of
biodiversity. The construction content includes: protect and strengthen urban ecosystems
and biodiversity; Improve water quality, prevent floods, and enhance ecosystem
resilience; improve the public facilities and infrastructure of Lake Aha.The construction
content includes:
①Wetland Park Biodiversity Monitoring and Protection Project. Implement 5-year
monitoring and protection of major biological groups such as plants, birds, mammals,
amphibians, and reptiles within the Aha Lake National Wetland Park to prevent invasive
species.
②Construction of wetland natural education and popular science education system.
Build a multi-level science and education system within the Aha Lake National Wetland
Park, and create a "borderless natural museum".
③The Aha Lake Bird Habitat and Watershed Ecological Zone Restoration Project.
Restoration of river water ecology and implementation of river shoreline restoration and
protection within the Aha Lake National Wetland Park.
④Construction of sewage treatment facilities. Construct sewage interception ditches and
small-scale water treatment and pollution control facilities around Lake Aha to improve
its water quality.
(3) Sub-project 3: Innovative methods & systems and enhanced capabilities
This sub-project mainly supports the research on carbon sequestration and its
methodology, as well as the development and construction of smart platforms and
applications, and the research on climate-smart and nature-friendly urban spatial
planning and urban green low-carbon transportation, laying the foundation for building
Guiyang into a national, regional and global urban carbon emission monitoring and
emission reduction good practice city. The sub-project will also support the payment for
meeting the World Bank's ESF (ESF), Procurement and Financial Management
Requirements, and the daily expenses incurred in Monitoring and Evaluation (M&E).
①Climate smart and nature friendly urban spatial planning. This project aims to promote
the construction of low-carbon cities in the Aha Lake area and Guiyang city through
ecological management research and low-carbon city design, providing feasible paths for

                                            3
low-carbon city construction at both theoretical and practical levels.
②Research on carbon sequestration methods and policy support for carbon markets.
Based on the work of carbon sink projects, strictly follow the development requirements
of voluntary greenhouse gas emission reduction projects, prepare design documents for
voluntary greenhouse gas emission reduction projects, and form a methodology for
applying for voluntary greenhouse gas emission reduction projects to the state.
③Research on urban green and low-carbon transportation. Research on the green travel
needs and service improvement strategies of special groups (disabled and elderly) in
Guiyang City, study the policy measures and monitoring system for collaborative
governance of traffic congestion and carbon reduction in Gui'an of Guiyang city, promote
the inclusiveness and fairness of special groups, and enhance the sustainability of green
city development in Guiyang.
④ Capacity building. Provide training to all parties involved in this project to enhance
their decision-making, implementation, technical proficiency, and management and
operational capabilities in green and low-carbon city planning.

1-2 Objectives of Social Impact Assessment

The social impact assessment is an objective assessment of the social impact caused
by the project based on full consultation with various stakeholders such as the
affected population, enterprises, and communities. These impacts include direct and
indirect adverse impacts brought by the project and direct and indirect positive
impacts. The assessment focuses on who will be affected by the project construction,
its effects on them, and how they will react. Through the evaluation, we can grasp the
potential social risks and social conflicts in the project implementation process to
adopt appropriate social management tools and measures to avoid or reduce social
risks to a minimum.
Following the requirements of the World Bank's environmental and social policy
framework (ESF), this project has prepared the "Social Impact Assessment Report
(SIA)", "Resettlement Action Plan (RAP)" "Ethnic Minority Development Plan
(EMDP)" and "Environmental Impact Assessment Report (EIA)" etc. This report aims
to avoid or mitigate the social risks and impacts of the project and, at the same time,
maximize the positive social impact of the project. The “Social Management Plan”
and "Labor Management Procedure" are included in this report as annexes. The
specific goals to be achieved by the social impact assessment include the following
aspects.

1-2-1 Screening Potential Social Risks Faced by Project

By conducting a social impact assessment, project planners and decision-makers can
understand the potential social impacts and risks during project implementation in
advance to take feasible policy measures to avoid and eliminate these adverse effects.
If adverse effects cannot be avoided, project planners should take technical and
financial measures to compensate the affected groups so that the project construction
can gain the understanding and support of local communities and residents, which is
essential to ensure the smooth implementation of the project.



                                          10
1-2-2 Identifying the Needs and Interests of Stakeholders

Conducting a social impact assessment helps identify all relevant stakeholders of the
project. Through focus interviews, questionnaires, and other methods, various
stakeholders can obtain project information on time and participate in the project
implementation process. Project planners and decision-makers can understand the
interests of multiple stakeholders, especially the unique needs and demands of
vulnerable groups such as low-income people, women, children, the elderly, and
disabled people, as well as ethnic minority groups to formulate appropriate policies
and measures to ensure better that these groups can obtain development opportunities
and benefits from the project, help them restore their livelihoods, protect their
legitimate rights and interests, promote women's development and gender equality,
and promote the development of ethnic minority areas.

1-2-3 Evaluation of Social Impact and Mitigation Measures

By conducting social impact assessments of projects, project planners avoid making
decisions solely based on economic benefits and are able to plan and implement
projects from a long-term and broad perspective. In conducting social impact
assessments, communication and understanding with stakeholders are enhanced.
Various stakeholders can more fully understand the socioeconomic benefits of the
project. The project can also gain the support of affected communities and broadly
groups and play a better role in local social development.

1-2-4 Optimizing Project Design and Implementation

Through social impact assessment, project owners can establish appropriate
organizational networks and institutional arrangements in the stages of project
selection, design, implementation, monitoring, and evaluation, strengthen public
participation, establish information disclosure mechanisms, widely consult various
stakeholders for opinions and suggestions, and incorporate their constructive
suggestions into project design to optimize project design.

1-4 Contents of Social Impact Assessment

Social impact assessment analyzes, supervises, and manages the social consequences
caused by project construction, including direct and indirect social impacts and
positive and negative ones. The specific contents and procedures of social impact
                                           14
assessment are as follows.

1-4-1 Baseline Analysis of the Project-affected Area

Baseline analysis of the natural resource environment, social and economic
development status, and historical and cultural traditions of the project-affected area is
conducted through historical documents and statistical data, and social policies and
institutional arrangements in the project-affected area are analyzed. The analysis
summarizes the current status, characteristics, and social and economic development
trends in the project-affected area from a macro perspective. This enables project
planners to have a deeper understanding of the natural and social environment of the
project area, understand the natural resources on which residents depend for survival
and the way and status of natural resources such as land they traditionally occupy,
analyze the population and economic development status and trends, and grasp the
overall economic development level, social and cultural characteristics and existing
social network relationship status of the project-affected area.

1-4-2 Identification and Analysis of Stakeholders

The project's stakeholders include all kinds of groups and organizations that may be
affected by the project construction and can also affect the implementation of the
project. They include groups or organizations that benefit directly or indirectly and
groups or organizations whose interests are directly or indirectly damaged. Based on
identifying the stakeholders, the social impact assessment team will listen to their
voices and understand their needs and interests through various means, such as public
consultation. The assessment experts will focus on interviewing vulnerable groups
such as women, and poor people, and ethnic minorities to gain an in-depth
understanding of the unique needs faced by vulnerable groups and help them cope
with the changes brought about by the project construction. Since the local people are
more familiar with the regional situation and have more local knowledge, they can put
forward more targeted opinions and feasible suggestions. Through consultation with
stakeholders, the views and recommendations of locally affected people,
organizations, and communities on project planning and implementation can be
                                            15
collected, which can optimize the project design and ensure a more efficient and
smoother implementation of the project.

1-4-3 Public Participation and Consultation

Public participation can absorb knowledge from all aspects more widely and enrich
and improve the project implementation plan. Throughout project preparation and
implementation, the project implementation agency will conduct prior, free, and full
consultations with various stakeholders and establish channels and procedures for
appeals and complaints from affected groups. In particular, participation mechanisms
and channels for vulnerable groups and ethnic minorities should be established. After
diagnosing various potential conflicts of interest that may be encountered during
project construction, the project implementation agency can further understand the
needs and interests of these stakeholders, clarify whether the project construction has
received sufficient support from local communities and residents, and analyze and
understand the reasons for support or opposition from various stakeholders. Through
the extensive participation of multiple stakeholders, social conflicts and risks can be
prevented and resolved so that the project can be implemented smoothly, and the
benefits of the project can be better utilized.

1-4-4 Project Social Risk Screening and Social Impact Analysis

This report mainly analyzes and evaluates the project's impact on local society from
positive and negative aspects. On the one hand, the project construction requires the
expropriation of land and the demolition of houses. During the project's construction,
the local farmland irrigation facilities, rural roads, and natural ecological environment
may be damaged. These will bring negative impact to the production and life of local
residents. Moreover, different groups may have conflicts of interest and opposing
attitudes towards the project construction. The social impact assessment will focus on
analyzing the various potential social risks brought by the project, especially the
possible adverse effects on ethnic minorities and vulnerable groups such as the elderly,
people with disabilities, the poor, single-parent families, and farmers whose land has
been expropriated. On the other hand, the project brings various direct or indirect
                                             16
positive impacts to multiple fields of local social life, mainly including improvements
in people's health, cultural level, concept progress, gender equality, and working
conditions.

1-4-5 Social Impact Mitigation Measures and Action Plans

Based on the evaluation of the social impact of the project and the interests of
stakeholders, the Project Management Office (PMO) and local government will
formulate social impact mitigation measures and action plans and establish social
management institutions for the project. These project institutions, together with the
government social management institutions, will jointly manage the project's potential
social risks, ensure the project's smooth implementation, and give full play to the
project's positive impact.

The Buyi people have many traditional festivals. In addition to the New Year, Dragon
Boat Festival, Mid-Autumn Festival, etc., which are the same as those of the Han
people, the "March 3", "April 8", "June 6" and other festivals have their unique
characteristics. On “March 3”, young men and women of the Buyi go up the mountain
to sing songs. “April 8” is a festival to commemorate the oxen. Every household
makes "Niu Wang Ba" or five-color glutinous rice, first offering sacrifices to the
ancestors, then calling the oxen's soul, and letting the oxen rest for a day, showing
people's love and reward for the oxen. On "June 6", every household kills chickens
and makes rice dumplings. In some places, pigs and cattle are slaughtered to celebrate
the festival and worship ancestors; women carry baskets filled with rice dumplings to
visit relatives and friends; the men in charge gather in the village, drink, and discuss
significant events in the village; young men and women gather in groups in the
wilderness or the village to sing songs, date (Langshao in Buyi language), throw
flower bags and other activities. In some places, "June 6" is called the Song Festival.
Thousands of men and women, old and young, gather together to sing and dance and
celebrate the Song Festival (Figure 2-2).
In the project-affected area, although the ethnic minorities have their unique customs
and habits in terms of clothing, diet, housing, marriage, etiquette, funerals, etc., and
also have some festivals with ethnic characteristics, all ethnic groups use the same
writing system, and intermarriage is also widespread. The population of each ethnic
group lives in a situation of "large mixed residence and small concentrated
settlement". All ethnic groups live in equality and harmony without conflicts between
ethnic groups.

3-2-1 Minority Development Policies

——All ethnic groups in the People's Republic of China are equal. The state protects
the legitimate rights and interests of all ethnic minorities and maintains and develops
equal, united, and mutually supportive relations among all ethnic groups.
Discrimination and oppression against any ethnic group are prohibited. Acts that
undermine ethnic unity and create ethnic divisions are prohibited (Article 4 of the
Constitution of the People's Republic of China).
——The state helps minority areas accelerate economic and cultural development in
accordance with the characteristics and needs of each minority. Areas, where ethnic
minorities live in concentrated communities, implement regional autonomy and
establish autonomous organs to exercise autonomous rights. All ethnic autonomous
areas are inseparable parts of the People's Republic of China. All ethnic groups have
the freedom to use and develop their languages and scripts and have the freedom to
maintain or reform their customs and habits (Article 4 of the Constitution of the
People's Republic of China).
——When enterprises and institutions in ethnic autonomous areas recruit personnel
by national regulations, they shall prioritize ethnic minorities. They may recruit from
the ethnic minority population in rural and pastoral areas (Article 23 of the Regional
Ethnic Autonomy Law of the People's Republic of China).
——The autonomous organs of ethnic autonomous areas organize and support
relevant units and departments to collect, organize, translate, and publish books on
ethnic history and culture, protect ethnic scenic spots, precious cultural relics, and
other important historical and cultural heritage, and inherit and develop excellent
ethnic traditional culture (Article 38 of Regional Ethnic Autonomy Law of the
People's Republic of China).
——The state and higher-level people's governments shall increase their financial,
financial, material, technical, and human resources support to impoverished areas in
ethnic autonomous regions to help the poor population escape poverty as soon as
                                          31
possible and achieve a moderately prosperous society (Article 69 of Regional Ethnic
Autonomy Law of the People's Republic of China).
——When the state develops resources and carries out construction in ethnic
autonomous areas, it should take into account the interests of the ethnic autonomous
regions, make arrangements that are conducive to the economic development of the
ethnic autonomous areas, and take into account the production and life of local ethnic
minorities. The state shall take measures to provide specific interest compensation to
ethnic autonomous regions that export natural resources (Article 65 of the Regional
Ethnic Autonomy Law of the People's Republic of China).
——If ethnic autonomous regions contribute to the country's ecological balance and
environmental protection, the state will provide specific benefits compensation
(Article 66 of Regional Ethnic Autonomy Law of the People's Republic of China).
——The higher-level people's government shall, based on the actual conditions of
ethnic autonomous areas, prioritize arranging infrastructure construction projects in
ethnic autonomous regions. According to national regulations, if the national
infrastructure projects such as transportation, energy, water conservancy, education,
health, culture, sports, radio, and television arranged by the state in ethnic
autonomous areas require ethnic autonomous areas to bear matching funds, the
proportion of matching funds shall be appropriately reduced; if the national poverty
alleviation key counties and financially difficult counties in ethnic autonomous areas
are genuinely unable to afford them, matching funds shall be exempted (Article 14 of
Certain Provisions for Implementation Regional Ethnic Autonomy Law of the
People's Republic of China in Guizhou Province).
——The higher state organs shall establish an ecological compensation mechanism
and provide reasonable compensation to ethnic autonomous regions that have made
contributions to the country's ecological construction and environmental protection
through measures such as fiscal transfer payments and project support (Article 18 of
Certain Provisions for Implementation Regional Ethnic Autonomy Law of the
People's Republic of China in Guizhou Province).

3-2-2 Protection of Women's Rights

——Equality between men and women is the basic national policy of the state.
Women enjoy equal rights with men in all political, economic, cultural, social, and
family aspects (Article 66 of Law on Protection of Women's Rights and Interests of
the People's Republic of China).
——When planning and constructing infrastructure, people's governments at all
levels should take into account the unique needs of women and equip public facilities
such as public toilets and baby care rooms that meet the needs of women (Article 34
of Law on Protection of Women's Rights and Interests of the People's Republic of
China).
——The state guarantees that women enjoy equal labor and social security rights with
men (Article 41 of Law on Protection of Women's Rights and Interests of the People's
Republic of China).
——Equal pay for equal work for men and women. Women enjoy equal rights to men
regarding welfare benefits (Article 45 of Law on Protection of Women's Rights and
Interests of the People's Republic of China).
——Women enjoy equal rights with men in terms of confirmation of membership in
rural collective economic organizations, land contracting, and management,
distribution   of   collective   economic   organization   income,   land   acquisition
compensation and resettlement or requisition compensation, and use of homesteads.
The agreement on acquisition compensation and resettlement or requisition
compensation should include women who enjoy relevant rights and interests and
record the content of their rights and interests (Article 55 of Law on Protection of
Women's Rights and Interests of the People's Republic of China).
——Women enjoy equal employment rights with men. When hiring employees,
except for jobs or positions that are not suitable for women as stipulated by the state,
women may not be refused employment, or the hiring standards for women may not
be raised based on their gender (Article 13 of Labor Law of the People's Republic of
China).

                                            33
——Employers shall not reduce the wages of female employees, dismiss them, or
terminate their labor or employment contracts due to pregnancy, childbirth, or
breastfeeding (Article 5 of Special Provisions on Labor Protection for Female
Employees).
——If female employees cannot adapt to their original work during pregnancy,
employers should reduce their workload or arrange other adaptable work based on the
certificate of medical institutions. Employers shall not extend working hours or
arrange night shifts for pregnant female employees for more than 7 months. They
shall arrange some rest time during working hours (Article 6 of Special Provisions on
Labor Protection for Female Employees).
——Employers shall not extend working hours or arrange night shifts for female
employees who are breastfeeding infants under 1 year old (Article 9 of Special
Provisions on Labor Protection for Female Employees).

3-2-3 Protection of Workers' Rights

——Workers shall not be discriminated against in employment due to differences in
nationality, race, gender, or religious beliefs (Article 12 of Labor Law of the People's
Republic of China).
——Employers are prohibited from hiring minors under 16 (Article 15 of the Labor
Law of the People's Republic of China).
——The state implements a working hour system in which workers' daily working
hours do not exceed eight hours and the average weekly working hours do not exceed
44 hours (Article 36 of Labor Law of the People's Republic of China).
——Employers shall ensure that workers have at least one day off per week (Article
38 of the Labor Law of the People's Republic of China).
——Employers may extend working hours due to production and operation needs
after consultation with trade unions and workers, but generally not more than one
hour per day; if working hours need to be extended due to special reasons, the
extended working hours shall not exceed three hours per day under the condition of
ensuring the health of workers. Still, they shall not exceed thirty-six hours per month

                                           34
(Article 41 of the Labor Law of the People's Republic of China).
——Wages shall be paid to workers in cash monthly. Workers' wages shall not be
deducted or delayed without reason (Article 50 of the Labor Law of the People's
Republic of China).
——Employers must provide workers with labor safety and health conditions that
meet national regulations and necessary labor protection supplies, and workers
engaged in work with occupational hazards should undergo regular health
examinations (Article 54 of Labor Law of the People's Republic of China).

3-2-4 Protection of Land-expropriated Farmers' Rights and Interests

——Land acquisition shall be compensated fairly and reasonably to ensure that the
original living standard of the farmers whose land is acquired is not reduced and their
long-term livelihood is guaranteed. When acquiring land, land compensation fees,
resettlement subsidies, and compensation for rural villagers' houses, other ground
attachments, and young crops, etc., shall be paid in full and on time following the law,
and social security expenses for the farmers whose land is acquired shall be arranged.
The standards for land compensation fees and resettlement subsidies for agricultural
land acquisition shall be determined by provinces, autonomous regions, and
municipalities directly under the Central Government through the formulation and
publication of district comprehensive land prices. The formulation of district
comprehensive land prices shall comprehensively consider factors such as the original
use of the land, land resource conditions, land output value, land location, land supply
and demand, population, and economic and social development level. It shall be
adjusted or re-announced at least once every three years (Article 48 of the Land
Administration Law of the People's Republic of China).
——The standards for compensation for acquiring other land, ground attachments,
young crops, etc., other than agricultural land, shall be formulated by provinces,
autonomous regions, and municipalities directly under the Central Government. For
the houses of rural villagers, compensation should be paid first and then relocated,
and living conditions should be improved. The wishes of rural villagers should be

                                           35
respected, and fair and reasonable compensation should be given by re-arranging
homesteads for building houses, providing resettlement houses, or monetary
compensation. The relocation and temporary resettlement expenses caused by the
expropriation should be compensated to protect the right of rural villagers to housing
and their legitimate housing property rights (Article 48 of Land Administration Law
of the People's Republic of China).
——Land-expropriated farmers who are within the legal working age and can work
and are willing to work and are unemployed can register for unemployment at public
employment service agencies and receive an "Employment and Entrepreneurship
Certificate". If found challenging, the unemployed can enjoy employment support
policies such as public welfare job placement (Guiyang Municipal People's
Government's Opinions on Employment and Social Security for Land-expropriated
Farmers).
——Land project owners should prioritize the employment of land-expropriated
farmers who meet the employment conditions. They can enjoy essential pension and
medical insurance subsidies if they employ land-expropriated farmers in the district
(city, county) where the project is located. Subsidy funds are drawn from the district
(city, county) employment special funds, and the specific subsidy standards are
implemented following the regulations of the Provincial Department of Human
Resources and Social Security and the Provincial Department of Finance. (Guiyang
Municipal People's Government's Opinions on Employment and Social Security for
Land-expropriated Farmers).
——Land-expropriated farmers who transfer to rural employment can enjoy
corresponding training subsidies if they participate in vocational skills training; those
who start their businesses can enjoy preferential policies such as small guaranteed
loans. (Guiyang Municipal People's Government's Opinions on Employment and
Social Security for Land-expropriated Farmers).

3-2-5 Protection of Migrant Workers' Rights and Interests

——Migrant workers have the right to receive their wages on time and in full. No unit

                                           36
or individual may withhold wages for migrant workers (Article 3 of Regulations on
Guaranteeing the Payment of Wages to Migrant Workers).
——Employers shall implement the real-name management system for the
employment of migrant workers and shall agree with the recruited migrant workers in
writing or stipulate the wage payment standards, payment time, payment methods,
etc., through legally formulated rules and regulations (Article 6 of Regulations on
Guaranteeing the Payment of Wages to Migrant Workers).
——Any unit or individual has the right to report to the human resources and social
security administrative department or other relevant departments for withholding
wages for migrant workers. The human resources and social security administrative
department and other relevant departments shall publicly disclose reporting and
complaint telephone numbers, websites, and other channels and accept reports and
complaints on the withholding of wages for migrant workers following the law.
Implement a first inquiry responsibility system for handling reports and complaints,
that means the first person is responsible for recording and resolving complaints. If
the department accepts the report, it shall handle it promptly following the law; if it
does not, it shall be transferred to the relevant department on time. The appropriate
department shall handle the report promptly following the law and inform the reporter
or complainant of the handling results (Article 10 of Regulations on Guaranteeing the
Payment of Wages to Migrant Workers).
——The construction unit shall have the funds required for construction. If there is no
arrangement of funds to meet the construction needs, the construction project shall not
be started; if a construction permit is required according to law, the relevant industry
construction department shall not issue a construction permit (Article 23 of
Regulations on Guaranteeing the Payment of Wages to Migrant Workers).
——The general contractor shall open a unique account for migrant workers' wages
following relevant regulations, which shall be used specifically to pay the salaries of
migrant workers in the construction project (Article 26 of Regulations on
Guaranteeing the Payment of Wages to Migrant Workers).
——The general contractor or subcontractor shall enter into a labor contract with the
                                           37
recruited migrant workers and register the employment in real name following the law.
The qualified industry shall register and manage the employment in real name through
the corresponding management service information platform. Personnel who have not
signed a labor contract with the general contractor or subcontractor and registered the
employment in real name shall not enter the project site for construction. The general
contractor shall assign a labor and wages manager to the project department to
supervise and manage the subcontractor's labor, understand the construction site's
labor, attendance, and wage payment, and review the wage payment table for migrant
workers compiled by the subcontractor. The subcontractor shall cooperate (Article 28
with the Regulations on Guaranteeing the Payment of Wages to Migrant Workers).

3-3 Environmental and Social Framework of the World Bank

World Bank Environmental and Social Framework (ESF) became effective on
October 1, 2018. ESF integrates the existing environmental and social policies of the
World Bank, forming a complete set of laws, regulations, principles, and procedures
for mitigating the environmental and social impacts of projects. ESF ensures that its
loan projects fully consider ecological protection and social responsibilities while
promoting economic growth. The formulation and implementation of this framework
reflects the World Bank's commitment to sustainable development goals and its
concern for environmental protection and social welfare on a global scale.
⚫   Standard 1: Assessment and management of environmental and social risks and
impacts. It is required to conduct an Environmental and Social Assessment (ESA) on
the project, manage its potential environmental and social risks, and obtain
environmental and social effects consistent with the Environmental and Social
Standards.
⚫   Standard 2: Labor and working conditions. The project must introduce
comprehensive protection standards for labor and working conditions to protect
workers' rights and interests.
⚫   Standard 3: Resource efficiency and pollution prevention and management. The
project must take effective measures to improve resource efficiency and prevent
                                          38
pollution, reduce environmental pollution, protect the ecological environment, and
improve people's quality of life.
⚫   Standard 4: Community health and safety. The project is required to formulate
community health and safety measures to protect the health and safety of community
residents.
⚫   Standard 5: Land acquisition, land use restrictions, and involuntary resettlement.
It is required that the project minimize land acquisition and mitigate the unavoidable
adverse social and economic impacts caused by land acquisition or land use
restrictions: timely compensate asset losses based on replacement costs; strive to
assist resettled persons in restoring their livelihoods and living standards to the level
before relocation or before the implementation of the project.
⚫   Standard 6: Biodiversity conservation and sustainable management of biological
and natural resources. The project is required to actively support and promote
biodiversity conservation and sustainable management of biological and natural
resources and contribute to promoting global sustainable development.
⚫   Standard 7: Indigenous Peoples. Emphasize active participation and consultation
with indigenous communities to ensure that the project implementation considers their
needs and wishes. The project implementation must also pay attention to the
protection and inheritance of indigenous cultural heritage, including supporting the
protection of indigenous languages, the inheritance of traditional knowledge, and the
protection of cultural heritage.
⚫   Standard 8: Cultural Heritage. If the construction project involves cultural
heritage, the EIA must include cultural heritage impact assessment content, such as
chance find procedures.
⚫   Standard 9: Financial Intermediaries. Financial intermediaries must cooperate
with the World Bank's requirements and follow the guidelines of Environmental and
Social Frameworks.
⚫   Standard 10: Stakeholder Participation and Information Disclosure. This standard
requires clarifying the responsibilities of stakeholder participation throughout the
project cycle, disclosing information in a timely manner, and allowing the project to
                                           39
benefit more people, especially the vulnerable groups.

The whole process of project preparation, construction, and final acceptance cannot

4-1 Definition and Identification of Stakeholders

4-1-1 Definition of Stakeholders

The "stakeholders" of this project refer to the following individuals and groups.
(1) Project-affected Parties
Individuals and organizations affected by the project are defined as “Project-affected
parties”. They mainly refer to stakeholders who may be affected due to the actual
impact or potential risk caused by the project on their physical environment, health,
safety, cultural practices, well-being, or livelihood. These stakeholders may be
individuals, groups, or local communities. Among the Project-affected parties, the
demands of vulnerable groups should be given special attention. The vulnerable
groups are social groups that cannot guarantee basic living standards for themselves
and their family members or maintain their legal rights by relying on their capabilities
or strength and need support and help from governments and society. The vulnerable
groups mainly include women, poor people, scattered ethnic minorities, disabled
person, elderly people living alone, etc. Due to their limited income and ability, the
vulnerable groups often find it challenging to cope with the changes in their
                                           42
production and life brought about by project construction. Moreover, they lack
influence on public policies, and their demands are more likely to be ignored.
Therefore, PMO and local government must take appropriate measures to ensure that
the rights and interests of vulnerable groups are fully protected in the project.
(2) Other Interested Parties
Individuals and organizations concerned about or can influence the project are defined
as "other interested parties". They mainly refer to individuals, groups, or organizations
interested in the project. These stakeholders may include regulators, government
officials, the private sector, scientific groups, academics, trade unions, women's
organizations, other civil society organizations, and cultural groups. Stakeholders may
be beneficiaries, risk bearers, or even project victims.

4-1-2 Identification of Stakeholders

To identify project stakeholders, it is necessary to collect information about them,
clarify their relationship with the project, identify which are key stakeholders who are
crucial to the project, which are decision-makers who provide resources or resolve
disputes, which are groups that directly affect the project, which are indirect
influencers, and which are observers. The PMO needs to fully understand the needs
and demands of all stakeholders in order to implement management in a targeted
manner. The stakeholders of this project are divided into two categories:
(1) Primary stakeholders. Groups and individuals with a legal, contractual
relationship with the project. Including owners, contractors and suppliers, design
agencies, engineering supervision, consulting agencies, and the project-affected
families, enterprises, and organizations.
(2) Other stakeholders. Groups and individuals with no formal contractual
relationship with the project but who are affected by or can influence it, including
government departments for project approval, news media, NGOs, and local

Public participation and consultation can provide local knowledge for project planners
and decision-makers and help them understand the various potential social impacts

5 Project Social Risk and Impact Analysis

The World Bank-financed Guiyang Urban Green Development Project can improve
the ecological environment and living environment of the Aha Lake area, promote
local green development, improve the quality of life of local people, and push the
entire city towards a green and low-carbon development path. In project planning, the
project owner will not only focus on the economic benefits and financial sustainability
of the project itself but also pay attention to the benefits and effects of the project on
the social development of the entire region from a long-term and broad perspective,
and evaluate the social benefits and potential risks brought by the project.

5-1 Project Social Risk Rating and Social Impact Screening

Some sub-projects belong to technical assistance research, management platform
construction, and institutional capacity-building projects. The project itself and
downstream activities have no environmental and social risks. However, some
activities require LA, HD and infrastructure construction, which impacts the
surrounding ecological environment and the production and life of community
residents. In November 2024, the PMO organized an EIA team and a SIA team to
screen the main potential environmental and social risks of the project.
Based on the project risk screening results, the integrated risk level of the project is
“Substantial”. The specific environmental and social risk screening results are shown
in Table 5-1.
The social impacts of the project construction are summarized as follows:
(1) The project requires the permanent land acquisition (LA) of 1,662 mu and house
demolition (HD) of 18,111 square meters. The LA impacts five sub-district offices
(townships), 11 neighborhood committees (villages), 1,697 households, and 6,387
people. The HD affects three sub-district offices (townships), 6 villages, 59
households, leading to the relocation of 245 people, posing “substantial” social risk.
Additionally, the temporary occupation of land during construction may disrupt the

production activities and daily lives of some residents.
(2) The construction of project roads and greenways requires temporary labor,
including local workers and migrant workers, which may lead to potential risks such
as extended working hours, substandard working conditions, and inadequate living
environments.
(3) The project will affect some ethnic minority villages that are located near cities，
the vast majority of the labor force has transferred to urban employment, and the labor
force who stay in the local area to cultivate land is also part-time labor, with 80% of
their income coming from non-agricultural sectors. The project land acquisition may
negative impacts on the local community and pose potential social risks. However, the
PMO and the local governments have substantial experience in project management
and strong capacity. Therefore, the overall social risk of the project is determined to
be ”substantial”.

5-2 Main Social Impacts and Management Measures of the Project

After investigation, interviews, and analysis, the SIA team believes that the project
will bring significant benefits to local economic and social development. However, it
also faces some potential social risks. These risks can be avoided and controlled
through proper mitigation measures. The project has formulated Social Management
Plan and proposed countermeasures to avoid and control various potential social risks
and increase social efficiency (Appendix 2).

5-2-1 Impact and Management Measures of Land Acquisition

Based on the construction items and contents of the FSR of the project, the
preliminary estimate for this project is that a total of 1662 mu of land will be
permanently requisitioned, including 269 mu of state-owned land and 1393 mu of
collective land, without involving basic farmland. 1,697 households and 6,387 people
are affected by LA, including 3,330 males and 3,057 females. The affected ethnic
minority population is 4,728, accounting for 74.03% (as shown in Table 5-2)
Since the LA for the project, particularly the greenway around the lake, follows a
linear pattern and involves 11 villages, the impact on each village and household is
minimal. Furthermore, survey results indicate that the acquired land in these villages
primarily consists of forest land and unused land, with the LA impact rate not
exceeded 10% (as shown in Table 5-3). Due to the project area being located at the
urban-rural junction, it has a high population density and low per capita land area.
Although the project is a linear-type project and the amount of land acquisition is
relatively small, a large number of households and people are affected. Some of the
land to be acquired consists of unused land owned collectively by village groups,
without specific contracted households. Once such unused land from a group is
expropriated, all residents of that group are counted as households and individuals
affected by the land acquisition.
Although the construction of the project is beneficial for local development and
improvement of the living environment, it is inevitable that some land will be
requisitioned. After some villagers' cultivated land is requisitioned, they may lose
some means of production, which will inevitably have adverse effects on their

                                          63
production. During interviews with affected villagers by the SIA team, many villagers
expressed their willingness to have their land requisitioned if the government timely
and fully pays the land compensation fees in accordance with the land compensation
standards and ensures information transparency (see Interview Case 1). When the SIA
team asked them about their attitude towards the project construction, the vast
majority of residents expressed support and would not obstruct the construction of the
project. The risk of LA for the project is deemed “substantial”, but it remains
manageable. The PMO has developed a RAP and implemented management measures
to mitigate potential risks and enhance the project's benefits (as shown in Table 5-4).



                                     Interview Case 1
  "I wanted to run a farmhouse before, but Aha Lake used to be a water source, and the
  government did not allow us to run a farmhouse. I heard that Aha Lake is no longer a
  water source for Guiyang City, and the greenway is planned to be built. I believe that
  the project construction will bring us new development opportunities and more small
  businesses. I am a person who can adapt to new changes. If the land acquisition
  compensation standard is reasonable and the compensation can be paid in time, it is not
  a problem for my land to be expropriated. After all, these lands are river beaches and are
  not the main source of income. Of course, I also worry that the land acquisition
  compensation may not be paid in time, the construction may be delayed too long, and
  even become an unfinished project."
  ——Jin Cuilan (female, 47 years old, Buyi ethnic group), a villager of Jinshan Village, Jinzhu
  Sub-district Office, Economic and Technological Development Zone, interviewed on November 29,
  2024


During the implementation of the Sewage Upgrading and Reconstruction Project，
some land needs to be temporarily occupied. During the construction of the greenway
around the lake, the construction unit may also need to temporarily occupy some land
to establish work sheds and material stacking sites. Due to the current feasibility study
stage of the project, the location, type, and quantity of temporary land occupation for
project construction cannot be determined yet. The temporary land occupation of the
project does not occupy arable land and forest land, mainly occupying unused land
and construction land, and has no adverse impact on the livelihoods of the affected
population. On the contrary, temporary land occupation also adds new income
(temporary land occupation fees) for landowners, and local residents are happy to
have their unused land temporarily occupied by the project.
This project will take the following measures to ensure the restoration of temporary
                                                 64
land use. (1) Temporary occupation of arable land is prohibited and must not affect
the livelihoods of local residents. (2) Temporary land occupiers should pay the
temporary land occupation fees in a timely manner according to the standards, and
also deposit the land reclamation fees in a dedicated account in full. (3) The
temporary land use period generally does not exceed two years. Land users shall
complete land reclamation within one year from the expiration of the temporary land
use period, restoring it to its original state.

5-2-5 Labor Management Risks and Mitigation Measures

During the project preparation stage, the primary stakeholders are government
approval and management departments, design institutions, technical assistance units,
and consulting institutions. These institutions are part of the formal sectors, where
employees benefit from comprehensive social security, comply with regulated
working hours and working conditions, and have their labor rights fully protected. As
a result, labor management in these sectors faces fewer risks.
During the project implementation phase, contractors and equipment suppliers are
expected to hire around 2200 workers during peak construction periods, with
approximately 1000 temporary workers. The majority of these temporary laborers will
be migrant workers or local workers, most of whom are in informal employment.
These workers generally have limited awareness of protecting individual rights and
their ability to safeguard legitimate rights and interests is insufficient. This project is
anticipated to employ 500 non-local laborers.
There are many rental properties available locally, so contractors typically do not
build their own construction camps. As a result, the environment and health risks of
the labor force are relatively low. According to a survey of existing local construction
sites, the government's management of temporary labor such as migrant workers hired
by contractors, has become increasingly standardized. The government has
established a mechanism and system for the regular payment of migrant workers'
wages. However, there are still weakness in managing labor working hours and
working conditions for temporary workers. The potential risks of labor management
are shown in Table 5-9. The PMO has developed LMP for the project, included as an
appendix to this report. The LMP incorporates the labor management requirements for
the project into overall Social Management Plan (SMP).

5-3 Positive Impact of the Project

Some of the project's positive impacts on local social and economic development are
direct and can be achieved in the short term. Some are indirect and can only manifest
after a long period of time.
1. The project can improve residents' travel conditions, facilitate their medical
treatment, schooling, shopping, etc., and improve their quality of life. After the
project's completion, residents will have faster and more convenient travel routes,
saving them a lot of time and money and improving their quality of life.
2. The project has created conditions for developing the cultural tourism industry and
                                                        78
can provide residents with a new source of income. Many rural areas originally had
tourism resources that could not be produced. The beautiful natural scenery and
unique ethnic customs of the local area can attract tourists, thereby creating many new
sources of income for residents, and local farmers can also quickly move towards the
road to wealth.
3. Greatly improve the living environment of surrounding rural residents and enhance
their sense of happiness. The project promotes biodiversity protection by repairing the
habitats of birds and other animals in the Aha Lake Wetland, which can attract more
citizens to visit the park. The construction of greenways, stations, and landscapes can
provide an excellent place for leisure for the residents in the surrounding areas and
other areas of Guiyang city, directly improving the residents' sense of happiness.

member to accept women's complaints and appeals.
6. Establish a communication and participation mechanism with ethnic minorities.
The project area is an area where ethnic minorities are distributed. The project's
resettlement scheme should align with the cultural habits of local PAPs and the wishes
of ethnic minorities and obtain their consent and understanding. The local ethnic
minority villages implement village-self-governance system. The village committee,

mediating civil disputes, assisting in maintaining social order, and reflecting the
opinions, demands, and suggestions of villagers to the people's government. The
chairman, vice chairman, and members of the village committee are directly elected

directors, and committee members in the affected villages are from ethnic minorities.
Each village committee has at least one female member, and village directors such as

occupation, family background, religious beliefs, education level, financial status, or
length of residence. These county and township people's congress representatives are

negotiations. In addition, the PMO also respects the traditional leaders of ethnic

Municipal Government. Any grievances related to this project that the PMO has the
authority to handle will be transferred to the PMO for processing. Grievances that can
only be handled by official institutions of Guiyang Municipal Government will also
be conveyed to the PMO regarding the grievances handling procedures and results,
ensuring that the PMO can record all grievances related to the project.

(2) Labor dispute resolution mechanism and procedures
There are two channels for enterprise labor appeals related to the project: one is the
internal labor appeal channel and mechanism of the enterprise; The second is the
external labor appeal channels and mechanisms for enterprises. Labors can choose
either mechanism for appeal, or appeal through both channels simultaneously.
① Internal appeal procedure. If employees feel that they have been treated unfairly,
they should first appeal to the company's human resources department, labor union,
and leaders. The human resources department is responsible for registering,
investigating, verifying the appeal, and proposing corresponding solutions.
The enterprise establishes a labor dispute mediation committee, which is composed of
representatives from employees, employers, and labor unions. There must be female
representatives among the representatives, and the director of the labor dispute
mediation committee shall be a union representative. The labor dispute mediation
committee is responsible for mediating labor disputes and making decisions on
solutions. The human resources department should pay attention to the protection of
personal privacy rights and promptly provide feedback on the handling results to the
appellant. Enterprise labor unions should play a role in safeguarding the legitimate
rights and interests of employees.
                                          93
If the labor force refuses to accept the decision of the enterprise labor dispute
mediation committee, they may apply for mediation, arbitration, or litigation through
labor appeal channels outside the enterprise in accordance with the law.
② External appeal procedures. If there is a labor dispute between the enterprise and
the employee related to the project, and the parties cannot resolve it through labor
mediation by the enterprise, they can appeal through the following three channels.
Labor mediation. The appellant may apply for mediation to a grassroots people's
mediation organization established in accordance with the law. If a mediation
agreement is reached on the payment of overdue labor remuneration, work-related
injury medical expenses, economic compensation or indemnity, and the employer fails
to perform within the agreed period, the employee may apply to the people's court for
a payment order in accordance with the law by presenting the mediation agreement.
The people's court shall issue a payment order in accordance with the law. After
reaching a mediation agreement, if one party fails to perform the mediation agreement
within the agreed period, the other party may apply for arbitration in accordance with
the law. Those who are dissatisfied with the arbitration award may file a lawsuit with
the people's court.
Labor arbitration. After a labor dispute arises, if mediation fails and one party
requests arbitration, they may apply to the labor dispute arbitration committee for
arbitration. Either party may also directly apply for arbitration to the labor dispute
arbitration committee after the labor dispute arises. The labor arbitration award shall
be made in accordance with the opinion of the majority of arbitrators, and the
dissenting opinions of the minority arbitrators shall be recorded in the transcript. The
labor dispute case ruled by the arbitration tribunal shall be concluded within 45 days
from the date of acceptance of the arbitration application by the labor dispute

7-1-2 Permanent Appeal Channels and Procedures in Guiyang City

The people's governments of Guizhou Province and Guiyang City have established a
permanent appeal platform and mechanism. This is an important way for the
government to understand social and public opinion, accept public supervision, and
also an important channel for the public to appeal. These permanent appeal channels
mainly include the following categories:
（1）Guiyang 12345 Government Service Platform
The 12345 government service platform in Guiyang City is established by the
Municipal People's Government. The platform accepts inquiries, complaints, and
requests for help from citizens 24 hours a day. Citizens can consult, complain and ask
for help through 12345 hotline, mayor's mailbox, mobile phone SMS, 12345 official
website of government affairs service center, "Guiyang 12345" official WeChat
official account and other ways. The service platform has established a
comprehensive service process, with the city level platform responsible for hotline
connection, problem resolution, tracking and supervision, and account closure and
cancellation; The governments of each district (city, county) are responsible for the
overall coordination and handling of issues raised by enterprises and the public in
their respective jurisdictions, and report the relevant problem handling situation to the
municipal platform as required, forming an efficient service model of "handling
complaints immediately". This comprehensive government service platform can be
used for consultation, appeals, and suggestions from various stakeholders in project
planning and implementation.
（2）Guiyang Municipal Bureau of Letters and Calls

petition matters. This institution receives petition materials from citizens, legal

handles them on their own according to specific circumstances.
（ 3 ） Government departments such as the Safety Supervision Bureau, Labor

labor laws, such as forced labor, failure to pay wages in a timely manner, or labor
disputes with the enterprise, they can directly report and complain to the Guiyang
Labor Supervision Brigade. The labor supervision brigade will promptly accept,
investigate, and provide handling suggestions to urge employers and workers to

departments responsible for ecological environment protection will conduct

of environmental pollution accidents.

8-1-1 Social Management Agency Network

There are two major categories of institutions for project social management.
The first category includes permanent establishment (government institutions), such
as the Human Resources and Social Security Bureau, the Ethnic and Religious Affairs
Commission, the Culture and Tourism Bureau, etc. Their daily responsibilities include
social management and social development.
The second category includes project social management institutions, some temporary
institutions specially set up for the project. After the project is completed, the
institutions will no longer exist. Some permanent project social management
institutions arrange personnel to specialize in project social risk management during a
specific period.
These institutions form an organizational network for social risk management, jointly
manage potential risks of the project and ensure the smooth implementation of the
project (as shown in Figure 8-1).

8-1-2 Capacity of Social Management Agency

The capacity of a social management institution and its staff is a key factor
determining the effectiveness of social risk management. The PMO will allocate

8-2-1 Resettlement of PAPs

The PAPs will be compensated for the loss of property in accordance with the
standards of the RAP. In addition, the PMO and local government will also take the
following livelihood restoration measures to ensure that the income of the PAPs is
restored or even improved.
(1) Continuously optimize the project design plan, increase the connection line
between greenways and surrounding villages, avoid or reduce involuntary
resettlement as much as possible, and better leverage the spillover effects of the
project on local economic and social development.
(2) Utilize the cultural tourism development opportunities brought by project
construction, vigorously develop ethnic cultural creative products and tourism goods,
and prioritize providing sales booths for local ethnic minority traditional craft
products. Support the development of housing rental and living services for
surrounding villagers, restore their livelihoods and increase the income level of PAPs.
(3) The local government actively supports the development of agricultural
cooperatives, improves the level of agricultural industrialization, enhances farmers'
negotiation ability in the market, and creates characteristic agricultural product bases.
(4) Provide project-related employment opportunities. During the implementation of
the project, more than 200 temporary jobs related to the project can be provided.
After the project is completed, it can also provide more than 55 job opportunities
such as cleaning and security. The local government will prioritize providing
employment opportunities to affected ethnic minorities, women, and the vulnerable
groups, allowing them to share the benefits of the project and increase their income.

8-2-2 Labor Management

During the preparation and implementation of this project, all participating enterprises
are required to strictly comply with national labor laws and project labor management
procedures (As shown in Appendix 3), safeguard the legitimate rights and interests of
labor, and improve the working environment and living conditions of labor.
(1) Establish a labor employment registration system. All contractors involved in the
                                          101
construction of this project shall sign labor contracts with the employed labor force.
All types of workers recruited by contractors shall be registered for labor employment.
The basic information and employment information of contractors must be entered
into the Guizhou Province Labor Employment Information System for online filing.
(2) Equip a project safety management specialist. The safety management specialist is
responsible for ensuring the safety of work and residential areas. Contractors must
provide safe working conditions and necessary labor protection equipment for
workers, allocate sufficient equipment maintenance personnel, regularly inspect and
maintain equipment and workplaces, timely replace damaged equipment and safety
facilities, and ensure workplace safety.
(3) Set up a dedicated account for migrant workers' wages (or labor fees). This project
implements a separate management system for labor costs and other project payments.
The contractors should break down the labor costs in the project price and open a
special account for migrant workers' wages (or labor fees) at the bank where the
project is located, specifically for paying migrant workers' wages.
(4) Set up a dedicated account for migrant workers' wages (or labor fees). This project
implements a separate management system for labor costs and other project payments.
The contractor should separate labor costs from project payments and open a special
account for migrant workers' wages (or labor fees) at the bank where the project is
located, specifically for paying migrant workers' wages.
(5) Establish a labor appeal mechanism. Any employee who encounters labor disputes
or unfair treatment can appeal through these appeal mechanisms. Contractors need to
set up appeal and rights protection information signs at the construction site.
(6) Establish a labor training system. During the implementation of the project, a
series of labor education and training activities will be carried out. One is to carry out
legal knowledge training to enhance workers' legal awareness and rights protection
awareness; Secondly, carry out specialized training on preventing and stopping sexual
harassment in the workplace; The third is to carry out safety production training.
Employees must undergo training and pass the evaluation before being allowed to
work.

(7) Contractor and supplier management. Contractors and suppliers who have
business dealings with the project should follow the LMP of this project. Prohibit the
use of child labor, pay wages and subsidies in a timely and sufficient manner, prohibit
forced labor, prevent sexual harassment and violence, implement special protection
policies for female workers, prevent employment discrimination, and strengthen the
management of the relationship between labor and local communities. The relevant
requirements for contractor labor management should be included in the bidding
documents.

monitoring.
IMO is also responsible for preparing external monitoring reports and submitting
them to the project office and the World Bank. The frequency and cycle of the reports
are as follows.
(1) During the implementation of the project, both internal and external monitoring
agencies should submit an annual monitoring report to the World Bank. The annual
monitoring report should be submitted before June 30th each year and can be included
as part of the resettlement monitoring report.
(2) In the project completion report, a dedicated chapter should be arranged to
systematically review the implementation of the social management plan, summarize
experiences and lessons learned.

1 Overview of Labor Use Related to the Project

1.1 Objectives of Labor Management Procedure

To treat the labors of the project fairly, provide them with safe and healthy working
conditions during the project construction, establish good labor-manager relationships,
and improve the development benefits of the project, the Project Management Office
(PMO) has formulated "Labor Management Procedure"(LMP) to achieve the
following objectives:
(1) To promote safety and health at work.
(2) To promote the fair treatment, nondiscrimination and equal opportunity of project
workers.
(3) To protect project workers, including vulnerable workers such as women, persons
with disabilities, children (of working age 16 to 18) and migrant workers（The rural
labors employed by the contractors, whose registered residence is in the countryside,
but mainly engaged in non-agricultural production, has strong mobility and belongs to
migrant workers), contracted workers and primary supply workers, as appropriate.
(4) To prevent the use of all forms of forced labor and child labor.
(5) To support workers in safeguarding their rights and interests in a way that is
consistent with national laws.
(6) To provide project workers with accessible means to raise workplace concerns.

1.2 Types of Project Labor

The project primarily relies on contractors for construction, consulting services and
equipment supply, with no primary suppliers identified for the project. Based on the
definition in ESS2 and with reference to the project overview, interviews with key
company personnel, and review of relevant documents, the types of workers involved
in the project include direct workers and contracted workers. At peak periods, the
labor force is estimated to exceed 3,000 workers (Table 1-1).
(1) Direct workers. People employed or engaged directly by the Borrower (including

the project proponent and the project implementing agencies) to work specifically in
relation to the project. The workers directly employed or hired for this project mainly
include workers of the project implementation agency.
(2) Contracted workers. People employed or engaged through third parties to perform
work related to core project functions, including consulting services, construction or
equipment suppliers, regardless of location. The labors employed by the contractors
during the construction period of this project are classified as contracted workers.

relationship with their employers, high wages, good working conditions, and

to protect their rights and interests.

2 Potential Risk Screening of Labor Management

Based on the construction content of the Project Engineering Feasibility Study Report,
The SIA team screened and evaluated the labor management risks in the future
implementation of the project. In general, the risk of engaging in hazardous work and
occupational health in the project is relatively low, and the possibility of using child
labor and underage labor is slight. However, the project has potential risks such as
long working hours, failure to pay workers' wages in a timely and complete manner,
imperfect labor and production safety conditions, poor living environment, failure to
implement special protection measures for female employees, and sexual harassment.
The comprehensive assessment of labor management risks is at a high level.
Contracted workers are the key group for labor risk management.

2.1 Risks of Health and Safety of workers during construction phase

According to the Project Feasibility Study Report, this project will involve around
2,200 workers during the peak time for constructing connection road activities etc.
which will present health and safety risks to the workers. For example, when
constructing roads and sewage interception projects, workers will use heavy
machinery, exposure to the dust, or work in confined spaces. There are potential
safety concerns and occupational diseases to the workers, therefore the risk level is
considered “moderate”. It is necessary to strictly abide by operating procedures and
EHS management systems to ensure workers' safety and physical health. Therefore, a
EHS plan focused on managing workers’ health and safety in line with EHS
guidelines has been prepared as part of this LMP.

2.2 Risks of Using Child Labor

The Chinese government has formulated a series of laws and policies to protect the
rights and interests of workers and crack down on the illegal phenomena of forced
labor and the use of child labor. The Labor Law of the People's Republic of China

                                          116
stipulates that employers are prohibited from recruiting child laborers under 16. The
labor inspection department has always strictly investigated and dealt with the illegal
use of child labor. Judging from the current environment in Guiyang, it is improbable
that contractors and suppliers will use child labor during the implementation of the
project. The Guiyang Municipal Government Labor Inspection Agency will work
with PMO to establish a reporting and investigation system for labor violations.
Sufficient law enforcement personnel will be deployed to strictly prohibit the use of
child labor in Guiyang, primarily in organizations involved in project construction. In
addition, the labor inspection department in the area where the equipment supplier is
located also strictly supervises the use of child labor. Therefore, the possibility of
using child labor in the project is minimal.

2.3 Risk of Forced Labor

Forced labor refers to the employer's failure to provide labor protection or working
conditions, making it impossible for workers to perform normal labor. Or the
employer uses violence, threats, or illegal restrictions on personal freedom to force the
worker to work. The project will not accept any work or service from any individual
under threat of force or punishment and will not employ any trafficked persons.
The Labor Law of the People's Republic of China stipulates that workers have the
right to equal employment and choice of occupation, the right to obtain labor
remuneration, the right to rest and vacation, the right to obtain labor safety and health
protection, the right to receive vocational skills training, the right to enjoy social
insurance and welfare, the right to request labor dispute settlement and other labor
rights stipulated by law. PMO and the Guiyang Labor Supervision Department have
set up a special complaint hotline to strengthen the implementation and supervision of
labor protection laws and severely crack down on forced labor. Judging from the
current labor environment, the possibility of forced labor in the project is minimal.

2.4 Risks of Hiring Temporary Labor

Civil engineering contractors may hire temporary workers to complete low-skilled

work such as earthwork excavation. At peak times, the number of workers may reach
2,000, of which about 40% are from outsiders. They are mainly migrant workers or

Poor working conditions and inadequate labor protection; (5) Female temporary

3.2 Summary of the Laws and Policies Related to Labor Management

——Workers have the right to equal employment and choice of occupation, the right
to obtain labor remuneration, the right to rest and vacation, the right to labor safety
and health protection, the right to receive vocational skills training, the right to enjoy
social insurance and welfare, the right to submit labor dispute resolution and other
labor rights prescribed by law (Labor Law of the People's Republic of China, Article
3).
                                                               120
——Workers shall not be discriminated against in employment due to differences in
nationality, race, gender, or religious beliefs (Labor Law of the People’s Republic of
China, Article 12).
——Women enjoy equal employment rights with men. When hiring employees,
except for jobs or positions that are not suitable for women as stipulated by the state,
women cannot be refused employment or the hiring standards for women cannot be
raised on the grounds of gender (Labor Law of the People's Republic of China, Article
13).
——Employers are prohibited from hiring minors under 16 (Labor Law of the
People’s Republic of China, Article 15).
—— If an employer is on the verge of bankruptcy and is undergoing statutory
reorganization or if production and operation conditions are in serious difficulty and it
is necessary to reduce staff, it shall explain the situation to the trade union or all
employees 30 days in advance, listen to the opinions of the trade union or employees,
and report to the labor administrative department before reducing staff. Suppose an
employer reduces staff by the provisions of this article and recruits new staff within
six months. In that case, it shall prioritize the laid-off staff (Labor Law of the People's
Republic of China, Article 27).
——If a worker is in any of the following circumstances, the employer shall not
terminate the labor contract by the provisions of Articles 26 and 27 of this Law:
(1) Having suffered from an occupational disease or work-related injury and being
confirmed to have lost or partially lost the ability to work;
(2) Being ill or injured and within the prescribed medical treatment period;
(3) Female employees who are pregnant, in labor, or breastfeeding;
(4) Other circumstances prescribed by laws and administrative regulations (Labor
Law of the People's Republic of China, Article 29).
——When an enterprise, institution, or social organization disciplines an employee
and the trade union deems it inappropriate, the trade union has the right to express its
opinion. When an employer unilaterally terminates an employee's labor contract, it
shall notify the trade union of the reasons in advance. When the trade union deems
                                            121
that the employer has violated laws, regulations, and relevant contracts and requires a
re-examination of the matter, the employer shall study the trade union's opinions and
notify the trade union of the results of the handling in writing. Suppose an employee
believes that the employer has violated his or her labor rights and interests and applies
for labor dispute arbitration or files a lawsuit with the People's Court. In that case, the
trade union shall provide support and assistance (Article 22 of the Trade Union Law of
the People's Republic of China).
——If an enterprise, institution, or social organization violates the provisions of labor
laws and regulations and infringes upon the labor rights and interests of employees in
the following circumstances, the trade union shall represent the employees in
negotiations with the enterprise, institution, or social organization and require the
enterprise, institution or social organization to take measures to correct the violation;
the enterprise, institution or social organization shall study and deal with the violation
and make a reply to the trade union; if the enterprise, institution or social organization
refuses to correct the violation, the trade union may request the local people's
government to deal with the violation by the law :
(1) Withholding or delaying the payment of wages to employees;
(2) Failure to provide labor safety and sanitation conditions;
(3) arbitrarily extending working hours;
(4) Violating the special rights and interests of female employees and underage
workers;
(5) Other acts seriously infringe upon employees' labor rights and interests (Trade
Union Law of the People's Republic of China, Article 23).
——If a trade union finds that an enterprise gives illegal commands or forces workers
to work in dangerous conditions, or if it discovers obvious major accident hazards and
occupational hazards during production, it has the right to propose solutions. The
enterprise should promptly study and respond. Suppose a situation endangers the life
and safety of employees. In that case, the trade union can suggest that the enterprise
evacuate the employees from the dangerous site. The enterprise must make a timely
decision on how to deal with the situation (Article 25 of the Trade Union Law of the
                                            122
People's Republic of China).
——Employers shall not reduce the wages of female employees, dismiss them, or
terminate their labor or employment contracts due to their pregnancy, childbirth, or
breastfeeding (Article 5 of the Special Provisions on Labor Protection for Female
Employees ).
——If a female employee cannot adapt to the original work during pregnancy, the
employer shall reduce the workload or arrange other work that she can adapt to based
on the medical institution's certificate. For female employees over 7 months pregnant,
the employer shall not extend the working hours or arrange night shifts and shall
arrange a certain amount of rest during working hours. Pregnant female employees
shall receive prenatal examinations during working hours, and the time required shall
be included in the working hours (Article 6 of the " Special Provisions on Labor
Protection for Female Employees ").
——Employers shall not extend their working hours or arrange night shifts for female
employees who are breastfeeding infants under one year old (Article 9 of the Special
Provisions on Labor Protection for Female Employees).
——In the workplace, employers should prevent and stop sexual harassment of
female employees (Article 11 of the Special Provisions on Labor Protection of Female
Employees).
——After a labor dispute occurs, the parties may apply for mediation to their unit's
labor dispute mediation committee; if mediation fails and one party requires
arbitration, they may apply for arbitration to the labor dispute arbitration committee.
One party may also apply directly to the labor dispute arbitration committee for
arbitration. If the party is dissatisfied with the arbitration award, it may file a lawsuit
with the People's Court (Labor Law of the People's Republic of China, Article 79).
——Safety facilities for new, rebuilt, and expanded projects of production and
business units must be designed, constructed, put into production, and used
simultaneously as the main project. Investment in safety facilities should be included
in the construction project's budget (Article 31 of the Work Safety Law of the People's
Republic of China).

——Production and operation units shall set up obvious safety warning signs in
production and operation sites and relevant facilities and equipment with major risk
factors (Article 35 of the "Safety Production Law of the People's Republic of China").
——Production and operation sites and employee dormitories should be equipped
with exits and evacuation passages that meet emergency evacuation requirements, are
marked, and are kept unobstructed. It is prohibited to occupy, lock, or block the exits
and evacuation passages of production and operation sites or employee dormitories
(Article 35 of the Work Safety Law of the People's Republic of China).

occupational health, labor protection, labor disputes, and prohibiting forced labor

In addition, regarding labor protection of female employees and GBV, the Special

5 Complaints Mechanism

There are two channels for labor complaints related to this project: the labor
complaint channel and mechanism within an enterprise and the labor complaint
channel and mechanism outside an enterprise. The labor can choose either a
mechanism to file a complaint or a complaint through both channels simultaneously.

5.1 Internal Complaint Mechanism

This project requires all relevant enterprises to establish a labor dispute complaint
mechanism. This provision is essential for employees to express dissatisfaction and
solve labor problems. It is conducive to resolving labor disputes in a non-litigation
manner within enterprises, improving employee satisfaction, and establishing
harmonious labor relations. Employees who encounter a labor dispute or unreasonable
treatment can file a complaint through the enterprise's internal complaint channel. The
specific procedures are as follows:
(1) Employees can first file a complaint with the internal human resources department,
labor union, and company leaders. The human resources department is responsible for
registering, investigating, verifying the complaint, and proposing appropriate
solutions.
(2) A labor dispute mediation committee shall be established within enterprises. The
labor dispute mediation committee shall comprise employees, employers, and union
representatives. There must be at least one female representative among the
representatives, and the labor dispute mediation committee director shall be a union
representative. The labor dispute mediation committee is responsible for mediating
labor disputes and deciding solutions. The human resources department should pay
attention to protecting personal privacy rights and promptly give feedback on the
handling results to the complainant. The enterprise union should play a role in
safeguarding employees' legitimate rights and interests.
(3) If the complainant does not accept the solution decision, they may apply for
mediation, arbitration, or file a lawsuit through external complaint procedures by the
                                          136
law.

5.2 External Complaint Mechanism

Guiyang has established a permanent labor dispute complaint mechanism. Suppose a
labor dispute occurs in a project-related enterprise in addition to using the complaint
mechanism established by the project. In that case, it can also use the enterprise's
external complaint mechanism, apply for mediation arbitration, file a lawsuit by the
law, or resolve the dispute through negotiation. The specific procedures are as
follows:
5.2.1 Mediation
When a labor dispute occurs between a project-related enterprise and its employees,
the parties may apply for mediation at a mediation organization outside the enterprise.
These mediation organizations include (1) grassroots mediation organizations
established by the law and (2) organizations established in towns and sub-districts
with labor dispute mediation functions. Suppose a mediation agreement is reached on
the payment of overdue labor remuneration, work-related medical expenses,
economic compensation, or damages, and the employer fails to perform within the
agreed period. In that case, the employee may apply to the People's Court for a
payment order by the law by presenting the mediation agreement. The People's Court
shall issue a payment order by the law. After reaching a mediation agreement, if one
party fails to perform the contract within the agreed period, the other party may apply
for arbitration per the law. If the party is dissatisfied with the arbitration award, they
may file a lawsuit with the courts.
5.2.2 Labor Arbitration
After a labor dispute occurs, if mediation fails and one party requires arbitration, the
party may apply for arbitration to the Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee. One
party may also apply for arbitration directly to the Labor Dispute Arbitration
Committee after a labor dispute occurs. The applicant shall submit a written
arbitration application. Within 5 days from the date of receipt of the arbitration
application, if the Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee deems that the conditions for
                                           137
acceptance are met, it shall accept the application and notify the applicant; after the
Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee agrees with the arbitration application, it shall
serve a copy of the arbitration application to the respondent within 5 days. After
receiving the copy of the arbitration application, the respondent shall submit a reply to
the Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee within 10 days. After receiving the reply,
the Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee shall serve a copy of the reply to the
applicant within 5 days. If the respondent fails to submit a reply, it will not affect the
arbitration procedure.
The Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee adopts the arbitration tribunal system to
adjudicate labor dispute cases. The arbitration tribunal is composed of 3 arbitrators,
with a chief arbitrator. A single arbitrator can arbitrate simple labor dispute cases. The
arbitration tribunal shall notify both parties of the date and location of the hearing 5
days before the hearing. If a party has a legitimate reason, it may request a
postponement of the hearing 3 days before the hearing. Whether to postpone the
hearing shall be decided by the Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee.
Arbitration results shall be based on the opinions of the majority of arbitrators, and
the dissenting views of the minority of arbitrators shall be recorded. When the
arbitration tribunal cannot form a majority opinion, the arbitration result shall be
made following the chief arbitrator’s opinion. The arbitration tribunal shall adjudicate
labor dispute cases within 45 days from the date the labor dispute arbitration
committee accepts the application. Suppose the case is complicated and needs to be
postponed. In that case, it may be delayed with the approval of the director of the
Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee, and the parties shall be notified in writing.
However, the extension period shall not exceed 15 days.
5.2.3 Litigation
Suppose the two parties to a labor dispute are dissatisfied with the labor arbitration
award. In that case, they may file a lawsuit with the People's Court within 15 days
from receipt of the arbitration result. If one party does not file a lawsuit or perform the
arbitration result within the statutory period, the other party may apply to the court for
compulsory execution. If the labor arbitration fails to make an arbitration result within
                                            138
the prescribed time limit, the parties may file a lawsuit regarding the labor dispute
with the court.

3. Equip with Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
     Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) provides additional protection to workers
exposed to workplace hazards in conjunction with other facility controls and safety
systems. PPE is considered to be a last resort that is above and beyond the other
facility controls and provides the worker with an extra level of personal protection.
When alternative technologies, work plans or procedures cannot eliminate, or
sufficiently reduce, a hazard or exposure, active use of PPE will be accepted.
     (1) Identification and provision of appropriate PPE that offers adequate

protection to the worker, co-workers, and occasional visitors, without incurring
unnecessary inconvenience to the individual.
     (2) Proper maintenance of PPE, including cleaning when dirty and replacement

when damaged or worn out. Proper use of PPE should be part of the recurrent training
programs for employees
     (3) Selection of PPE should be based on the hazard and risk ranking and selected

according to criteria on performance and testing established.

4. Maintain Community Health and Safety
     During the construction period of the project, the increase in transportation
vehicles and pedestrian flow poses a threat to the health and safety of the community.
The PMO will take the following measures to strengthen traffic safety management,
minimize traffic accidents, and prevent disease transmission.
4.1 Traffic Safety Management
Adoption of best transport safety practices across all aspects of project operations
with the goal of preventing traffic accidents and minimizing injuries suffered by
project personnel and the public. Measures should include:
(1) Emphasizing safety aspects among drivers; Improving driving skills and requiring

licensing of drivers; Adopting limits for trip duration and arranging driver rosters to
avoid overtiredness; Avoiding dangerous routes and times of day to reduce the risk of
accidents; Use of speed control devices (governors) on trucks, and remote monitoring
of driver actions.
                                          145
(2) Regular maintenance of vehicles and use of manufacturer approved parts to

minimize potentially serious accidents caused by equipment malfunction or premature
failure.
(3) Minimizing pedestrian interaction with construction vehicles.

(4) Collaboration with local communities and responsible authorities to improve

signage, visibility and overall safety of roads, particularly along stretches located near
schools or other locations where children may be present. Collaborating with local
communities on education about traffic and pedestrian safety (e.g. school education
campaigns).
(5) Coordination with emergency responders to ensure that appropriate first aid is

provided in the event of accidents;
(6) Using locally sourced materials, whenever possible, to minimize transport

distances;
(7) Locating associated facilities such as worker camps close to project sites and

arranging worker bus transport to minimizing external traffic.
(8) Employing safe traffic control measures, including road signs and flag persons to

warn of dangerous conditions。
4.2 Preventing Communicable Diseases
     To prevent communicable diseases such as sexually-transmitted diseases(STDs)
caused by personnel movement during the construction phase of the project. PMO
will take various corrective measures typically involve a combination of behavioral
and environmental modifications.
     (1) Providing surveillance and active screening and treatment of workers.

Conducting immunization programs for workers in local communities to improve
health and guard against infection.
     (2) Undertaking   health awareness and education initiatives, encouraging
individuals to take protective measures such as using condoms to protect others from
infection.

4.3 Control noise and Vibration
     During construction and decommissioning activities, noise and vibration may be
caused by the operation of pile drivers, earth moving and excavation equipment,
concrete mixers, cranes and the transportation of equipment, materials and people.
Some recommended noise reduction and control strategies to consider in areas close
to community areas include:
(1) Planning activities in consultation with local communities so that activities with

the greatest potential to generate noise are planned during periods of the day that will
result in least disturbance.

5. Strengthen Occupational Health and Safety Training
     (1) Provisions should be made to provide OHS orientation training to all new
employees to ensure they are apprised of the basic site rules of work at / on the site
and of personal protection and preventing injury to fellow employees. Training should
consist of basic hazard awareness, site specific hazards, safe work practices, and
emergency procedures for fire, evacuation, and natural disaster, as appropriate. Any
site-specific hazard or color coding in use should be thoroughly reviewed as part of
orientation training.
     (2)The employer should ensure that workers and contractors, prior to
commencement of new assignments, have received adequate training and information
enabling them to understand work hazards and to protect their health from hazardous
ambient factors that may be present. The training should adequately cover:
Knowledge of materials, equipment, and tools; Known hazards in the operations and
how they are controlled; Potential risks to health; Precautions to prevent exposure;
Hygiene requirements; Wearing and use of protective equipment and clothing;
Appropriate response to operation extremes, incidents and accidents
     (3)Workers with rescue and first-aid duties should receive dedicated training so
as not to inadvertently aggravate exposures and health hazards to themselves or their
coworkers. Training would include the risks of becoming infected with blood-borne
pathogens through contact with bodily fluids and tissue.
     (4)Through appropriate contract specifications and monitoring, the employer
should ensure that service providers, as well as contracted and subcontracted labor,
are trained adequately before assignments begin.
