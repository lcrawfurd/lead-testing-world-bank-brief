I. PROGRAM STATUS AND RATIONALE FOR RESTRUCTURING

A. Program status
1. The Rural Water Supply Universal Access Program (“AQUA-VIE Program”), P164186, is the backbone of the national
   program of the Government of Benin (GoB) to provide universal access to water supply services and to professionalize
   service delivery in the rural areas of the country. The Program is implemented by the Rural Water Supply Agency
   (Agence Nationale d’Approvisionnement en Eau Potable en Milieu Rural). The Program was approved in May 2018 and
   became effective in September 2018. It is structured around two Result Areas : (i) Firstly, the Program supports
   investments in piped rural water supply systems to rapidly expand access to support the GoB achieve its ambitious
   targets for the sector ; (ii) Secondly, the Program strengthens service delivery arrangements through comprehensive
   reforms of the rural water supply sector, based on delegation of service delivery to private operators through regional
   performance-based affermage contracts with the aim to improve the quality and sustainability of the expanded water
   supply systems.
2. The total cost of the Program is estimated at US$240 million. Its financing is made up of an IDA Program-for-Results
   (PforR) instrument amounting US$220 million. The Program is part of a broader national program of expenditures to
   achieve universal access to rural water services whose total amount was estimated at US$560 million in 2018. The IDA
   PforR has disbursed so far US$68 million representing 33 percent of the total financing, in line with disbursement
   forecasts. The overall risk of the Program is currently rated Moderate.
3. Progress towards the achievement of Program Development Objective (PDO) and Implementation Progress have been
   rated Satisfactory since the approval of the Program despite the COVID-19 crisis, both in terms of implementation of
   the investments in piped rural water supply systems (Result Area 1) and in terms of recruitment of the regional private
   operators (Result Area 2):
             The construction of the first batch of the multi-village rural water supply systems making up the AQUA-VIE
              Program is in some cases completed and in others is at an advanced stage of execution. The negotiations for
              the award of the second batch of multi-village rural water supply systems are on-going at the time of this
              restructuring. Until now the COVID-19 crisis has notably not generated a noticeable delay on the execution of
              the investment program.
             The recruitment process of the private operators under regional performance-based affermage contracts is
              also well advanced despite the complexity of this PPP transaction and despite the ongoing pandemic. Offers
              from all the pre-qualified firms have been submitted, the technical offers have been evaluated and results of
              the technical evaluation have been vetted and published. At the time of this restructuring the financial offers
              have been evaluated and are in the process of being vetted. It is estimated that the recruitment process of
              the three regional contracts will be completed by the end of March 2021.
             The GoB is also finalizing the definition of a users’ tariff policy in order to ensure affordable access by the rural
              population to water supply services provided by the regional private operators as well as the sustainable
              financing of rural water supply services.
4. There is no substantial fiduciary, environmental and social issues to report at this stage. At the time of this
   restructuring, there is no audit overdue and neither the loan, nor the country, is subject to an ongoing suspension of
   disbursements.

B. Rationale for Restructuring
5. The rationale for the proposed restructuring is underpinned by:
    (i) The decision of the GoB to:
            a. Cluster the rural areas of the country in three zones to award the performance-based affermage contracts,
               in lieu of six zones as envisaged during the appraisal of the Program. This change to the Program’s design
               allows achieving economies of scale to the benefit of users’ tariff.
            b. Promote the construction of multi-village piped rural water supply systems in lieu of piped rural water
               supply systems supplying only a set of localities of a single village as envisaged during the appraisal of the
               Program. The environmental & social risks and impacts of those multi-village piped rural water supply
               systems are systematically screened, assessed and mitigated in accordance with the same instruments of
               the Program’s environmental & social system.
    (ii) The need to update the formulation of a Disbursement Linked Indicator (DLI) and of two indicators of the
         Program’s Result Framework in order to reflect the two decisions of the GoB mentioned above. In addition, one
         of the Intermediate Indicator is not relevant anymore, as agreed between the the GoB and the IDA.
    (iii) The need to update two actions of the Program Action Plan (PAP) in order to reflect an adjustment to the
          frequency of the missions for internal audit / control and changes made to the arrangements for land management
          under the Program, as agreed between the GoB and the IDA.
6. The World Bank received a letter from the Ministry of Economy and Finance (MINFIN) dated February 8th, 2021
   requesting the changes to the Program’s DLI, Result Framework and PAP mentioned in paragraph 5.
7. Section II of this Restructuring Paper describes the proposed changes in more detail. The Financing Agreement, Project
   Agreement, Subsidiary Agreement as well as the Project’s Operational Manual will be amended to reflect the agreed
   changes.
