                         ENVIRONMENTAL AND SOCIAL COMMITMENT PLAN

1. The Republic of Tunisia (the Borrower) will implement the Potable Water Security and Resilience
   Project (P514340) (the Project), with the involvement of the Ministry of Agriculture, Hydraulic
   Resources and Fisheries (MARHP), the National potable water utility /Société Nationale d’Exploitation
   et de Distribution des Eaux (SONEDE), as set out in the Loan Agreement (the Agreement). The
   International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (the Bank) has agreed to provide financing
   for the Project, which is co-financed by the Arab Fund for Economic and Social Development (AFESD),
   as set out in the Agreement.

2. The Borrower shall ensure that the Project is carried out in accordance with the Environmental and
   Social Standards (ESSs) and this Environmental and Social Commitment Plan (ESCP), in a manner
   acceptable to the Bank. The ESCP is a part of the Agreement. Unless otherwise defined in this ESCP,
   capitalized terms used in this ESCP have the meanings ascribed to them in the Agreement.

3. Without limitation to the foregoing, this ESCP sets out material measures and actions that the
   Borrower shall carry out or cause to be carried out, including, as applicable, their respective
   timeframes; institutional, staffing, training, monitoring and reporting arrangements; and grievance
   management. The ESCP also sets out the environmental and social (E&S) documents that shall be
   prepared or updated, consulted, disclosed and implemented under the Project, consistent with the
   Environmental and Social Standards (ESSs), in form and substance acceptable to the Bank. Said E&S
   documents may be revised from time to time with prior written agreement by the [Bank/Association].
   As provided for under the referred Agreement[s], the Borrower shall ensure that there are sufficient
   funds available to cover the costs of implementing the ESCP.

4. As agreed by the Bank and the Borrower, this ESCP will be revised from time to time, if necessary, to
   reflect adaptive management of Project changes or unforeseen circumstances or in response to
   Project performance. In such circumstances, the Bank and the Borrower agree to update the ESCP to
   reflect these changes through an exchange of letters signed between the Bank and the Borrower’s
   Representative specified in the Agreement or Director General of SONEDE, as applicable. The
   Borrower shall promptly disclose the updated ESCP.

5. The subsection on “Indicators for Implementation Readiness” below identifies the actions and
   measures to be monitored to assess Project readiness to begin implementation in accordance with
   this ESCP. Nevertheless, all actions and measures in this ESCP shall be implemented as set out in the
   “Timeframe” column below irrespective of whether they are listed in the referred subsection.

4.3   SEA AND SH RISKS

      Develop and implement a SEA/SH Action Plan to assess and manage the risks of SEA/SH          Prepare the SEA/SH Action Plan          SONEDE
      related to the Project. This Action Plan will be annexed to the ESMF, and its mitigation     before project effectiveness and

      Based on the World Bank’s SEA/SH risk screening tool, the Project has been evaluated as
      having a substantial risk level. Accordingly, a comprehensive SEA/SH Action Plan will be
      prepared and implemented to address and mitigate these risks.

      The Action Plan will specify targeted measures to identify, prevent, and respond to SEA/SH
      risks associated with Project activities. These measures may be incorporated within the
      Labor Management Procedures (LMP) and/or the ESMPs, and will include:
           o Awareness-raising initiatives for project workers and local communities
           o Establishment of confidential GM
           o Specialized training for project personnel
           o Implementation of codes of conduct
           o Clear protocols for reporting and managing SEA/SH incidents

      The SEA/SH Action Plan will be implemented following the same schedule as the ESMPs
      and will be updated as needed to address emerging risks throughout Project
      implementation.
