                     Proposed Development Objective

                     The Development Objective of the Series of Projects (SoP) is to strengthen the shared management of selected Pacific
                     Island oceanic and coastal fisheries, and the critical habitats upon which they depend. The Development Objective of
                     this Project is to improve access to economic opportunities for fishers and fish workers, increase climate-resilient
                     domestic supply of fish products, and strengthen national capacity for fisheries management.

                     themes and activities will evolve around: - Development of enabling infrastructure for efficient dockside inspections,

                     D. Environmental and Social Overview
                     D.1 Overview of Environmental and Social Project Settings
                     Vanuatu is a small island developing state in the South Pacific where fisheries are central to livelihoods, food security,
                     and economic activity. The Project is national in scope with key physical investments concentrated in Luganville (Espiritu
                     Santo) and Port Vila (Efate), complemented by nationwide technical assistance. The Project supports tuna industry
                     domestication, small- and medium-scale fisheries value chains, and fisheries governance strengthening.
                     The principal physical investments include: (i) coastal reclamation and construction of a multipurpose jetty, moorings,
                     boatyard pen, gantry, and administrative facilities at the Vanuatu Fisheries Department (VFD) compound in Luganville;
                     (ii) a Fisheries Technology and Commercialization Centre (HACCP-compliant) within the same compound; (iii) upgrades
                     to the Luganville Provincial Fish Market; and (iv) construction of a new VFD Headquarters in Port Vila. The reclamation,
                     marine infrastructure and the technology and commercialization center investments at Luganville are located in



Public Disclosure
                     predominantly modified coastal and urban environments, with long-standing port, wharf, and commercial land uses,
                     but within a broader marine setting of high ecological value (the Palekula–Turtle Bay Special, Unique Marine Area
                     (SUMA) identified under the Marine and Coastal Biodiversity Management in Pacific Island Countries (MACBIO) Project).
                     The broader area supports ecologically important coastal and marine ecosystems (seagrass, mangroves, coral
                     communities) and species of conservation concern, including dugongs, marine turtles, migratory birds, and sharks.
                     Increased vessel movements underwater noise, turbidity, sedimentation, and long-term changes to coastal processes
                     present risks that require careful assessment.

                     Vanuatu is highly vulnerable to natural hazards and climate change, including cyclones, earthquakes, volcanic eruptions,
                     storm surges, flooding, and sea-level rise. These risks are particularly relevant for coastal reclamation, jetty
                     infrastructure, and buildings on reclaimed land, with potential for liquefaction, overtopping, erosion, and service
                     disruption if not properly addressed through climate-resilient design.

                     modified and natural coastal habitats — as well as sediment disturbance and discharge that could affect water quality

                      ESS7 is relevant to the Project only in cases where Project activities affect Indigenous Peoples’ customary land or
                      marine resources, including where activities require access to, occupation of, or result in restriction of access to land,
                      foreshore or seabed areas that are customarily owned, used or occupied by Indigenous Peoples, as defined under
                      ESS7. ESS7 does not apply to Project activities that do not affect Indigenous Peoples’ own customary land or marine
                      resource use. Project activities, particularly those involving coastal reclamation, marine infrastructure and associated
                      facilities, may in certain locations overlap with customary land or marine areas used by Indigenous Peoples. Where
                      such impacts occur, potential ESS7 risks are expected to be moderate and site specific, and may include disputes or
                      delays related to access to customary land or marine areas, impacts on customary resource use arising from physical
                      occupation or restriction of access, and risks of social conflict if engagement is not conducted in a culturally
                      appropriate manner or does not allow sufficient time for customary decision making. To manage these risks, the
                      Project will apply a screening process to identify whether Indigenous Peoples’ customary land or marine resources
                      may be affected. Where screening confirms that such impacts are likely, the Project will apply ESS7 requirements,
                      including Free, Prior and Informed Consent (FPIC), in a culturally appropriate manner. FPIC will be required only for
                      activities that may result in temporary or permanent loss of access to Indigenous Peoples’ customary land or marine
                      resources or involve physical occupation or restriction of use of such areas. FPIC processes will be undertaken,
