                     C. Summary Description of Proposed Project Activities

                               Paraiba Water Security Project 2 (northeast Mpa Phase I) (P511557)



                     The Project will constitute the Phase 1 of the Multiphase Programmatic Approach (MPA) ‘Integrated Water Security
                     Program for the Northeast of Brazil’. Most of the region’s nine states face similar constraints, including limited water
                     availability, recurrent droughts and floods, fragmented institutions, and underdeveloped infrastructure and
                     management tools— further shaped by transboundary watersheds and aquifers, as well as shared hydraulic
                     infrastructure. These commonalities present a valuable opportunity for horizontal learning and collaboration, enabling
                     states to exchange knowledge and build on each other’s innovations and good practices. Several states have already
                     developed effective solutions that are highly relevant and replicable across the region. The MPA will facilitate structured
                     knowledge exchange and coordination across the states, helping reduce negative externalities and enhance the
                     collective impact of water security investments. About 7.3 percent of the Northeast Region’s population lives in the
                     State of Paraiba; within Paraiba, 77.6 are urban residents and 22.4 percent live in rural areas (IBGE, 2022). The state's
                     water security challenges and socioeconomic landscape present a great opportunity to test and refine the Program's
                     strategies. Phase 1 of the MPA comprises activities prioritized by the State Government aimed at improving water
                     security across Paraíba, strengthening key sector water institutions such as the State Water Resources Management
                     Executive Agency (Agência Executiva de Gestão das Águas, AESA), the State Water and Sanitation Company (Companhia
                     de Água e Esgotos da Paraíba, CAGEPA), and a newly created Executive Management Unit for Rural Sanitation (Gerencia
                     Executiva de Saneamento Rural, GESR), and constructing essential infrastructure such as a wastewater treatment plant
                     (WWTP), the sectorization of the water supply distribution system to reduce non-revenue water (NRW), and the
                     construction of a water transmission main for the Gramame system, all serving the RMJP. The Project builds on the
                     experience of the on-going Paraiba Improving Water Resources Management and Services Provision Project (or Paraiba
                     Water Security Project, PWSP, P165683) which was approved in 2019 and is currently under implementation. This
                     project includes actions on WRM, security of dams, the construction of water mains, the sectorization of the water

                     supply network of RMJP, wastewater collection infrastructure for RMJP, and institutional strengthening. The Project will
                     include the following components: Component 1. Integrated Water Resources Management. This component aims to
                     contribute to proactive WRM in the state by strengthening the state WRM institution (AESA) and consolidating key
                     management instruments related to water quantity, quality, and drought resilience. It will focus on expanding surface
                     and groundwater monitoring, fostering dialogue on the costs and financial sustainability of the water management
                     system, improving water licenses and charges, and establishing water quality standards through a participatory process
                     with society, along with the necessary actions to achieve those standards. The component will also focus on increasing
                     the state’s capacity for drought management through increased monitoring and warning mechanisms for
                     implementation of adequate measures, and the proposition of a state water reuse policy. Component 2. Resilient Water
                     Services. This component is designed to advance the modernization and expansion of water supply and sanitation
                     services, supporting the state water utility (CAGEPA), the state water services regulatory agency (ARPB), and a recently
                     created unit that will lead the development of a strategy and plan to support rural water supply and sanitation in the
                     state (GESR). It encompasses the implementation of water infrastructure investments that will enhance the quality of
                     life for residents, improve environmental outcomes, and promote economic growth within the state, particularly in the
                     RMJP region. Component 3. Project Management. This component will support the Project Management Unit (Unidade
                     de Gerenciamento do Projeto, UGP) in strengthening its technical and administrative capacity. Activities will include the
                     hiring of consultants and the provision of independent audit services to ensure effective project implementation,
                     monitoring, and compliance with fiduciary and safeguard requirements.


                     D. Environmental and Social Overview
                     D.1 Overview of Environmental and Social Project Settings

                     Located in the Brazilian Northeast Region, the Paraiba State is the Brazilian state with the lowest water availability, faces
                     major challenges in human water supply, made worse by frequent droughts and climate variability. Events like the
                     severe rationing of 1999-2000, when reservoirs reached historic lows, highlight these issues.
                     The State covers a total area of 56,467.242 km² and is administratively divided into 223 municipalities. Geographically, it
                     can be separated into four distinct zones, each with unique physiographic characteristics: the Mata (Coastal), the
                     Agreste, the Borborema, and the Sertão Zones. The Zona da Mata lies adjacent to the Atlantic coast, stretching several
                     kilometers inland. This region is characterized by a humid tropical climate, abundant rainfall, and diverse biotic features.
                     Its vegetation includes localized areas of mangroves, restinga, and the Atlantic Forest. The Zona da Mata encompasses
                     the João Pessoa Metropolitan Region (JPMR), which includes 12 municipalities such as João Pessoa (the state capital),
                     Cabedelo, Bayeux, Conde, Lucena, Mamanguape, Rio Tinto, and Santa Rita. The JPMR is home to a population of
                     approximately 1.4 million people.

                     João Pessoa, with 15.5% real estate growth in 12 years, attracts tourists and investors but struggles with public
                     infrastructure, especially sanitation. Rapidly growing neighborhoods have overloaded water systems, causing pressure
                     drops, mainly in summer. To meet rising demand, strict operation, investment in system expansion, loss reduction, and
                     automation are essential to support real estate and local development. The project interventions will include the
                     expansion of the Baixo Paraíba Wastewater Treatment Plant (WWTP), the implementation of the treated water pipeline
                     from the Gramame system, and the creation of water supply sectors in the JPMR. All the civil work supported by the
                     project will take place in urban areas or anthropized, peri-urban areas.

                     D.2 Overview of Borrower’s Institutional Capacity for Managing Environmental and Social Risks and Impacts
                     Brazil has advanced environmental, social (including Indigenous Peoples rights), and labor laws. The National Sanitation
                     Law (Federal Law No. 14,026/2020) sets national guidelines for water supply, sewage treatment, urban cleaning, waste
                     management, and environmental protection. Brazil’s laws on access to information and privacy are robust, including the
                     Access to Information Law (Law 12,527/2011), which guarantees public access to information and grievance
                     mechanisms.
                     The National Environment Council (CONAMA) is a key regulatory body, developing standards for environmental
                     protection. CONAMA Resolution 430/2011 regulates liquid effluent discharge into water bodies.
                     The Borrower has a proven track record with World Bank projects (P165683, P147158, P511645) in water management
                     and agriculture, consistently complying with social and environmental criteria. UGP staff are regularly trained on ESF
                     requirements to ensure effective stakeholder engagement and mitigation of impacts.
                     To coordinate the Project in its different stages, a Project Management Unit (PMU) reporting directly to SEIRHMA will
                     be established. The PMU at State Secretariat for Infrastructure, Water Resources and Environment (SEIRHMA) will
                     consist of technical coordination staff, representing SEIRHMA, Paraiba State Water Resources Management Executive
                     Agency (AESA) and Water and Sewerage Company of Paraiba (CAGEPA).

                     A.1 Environmental Risk Rating                                                                                Moderate
                     This proposed project builds on a successful previous operation, aiming to strengthen integrated water resources
                     management, improve water service reliability, and enhance the efficiency of water and wastewater services in the
                     JPMR. The project will provide technical support and invest in water and sanitation infrastructure, including sewage
                     system upgrades in João Pessoa, Cabedelo, and Conde, to reduce spills and improve treatment, benefiting local
                     ecosystems. Component 1 offers technical assistance and small monitoring projects with minimal environmental risk.
                     Component 2 focuses on sanitation, including a 36 km water main within the RMJP reticulation system, expansion of
                     the Baixo Paraíba WWTP, optimization of João Pessoa’s metro water supply, and rural water studies. Component 3
                     addresses project management and emphasizes social and environmental compliance. All work will take place in
                     urban or peri-urban areas, with no major construction in sensitive zones or significant threats to biodiversity. Civil
                     works range from monitoring networks to moderately complex WWTP and pipeline construction (about 18 months).
                     Impacts are expected to be local and manageable, though the WWTP may directly affect the Paraiba River
                     mangrove—currently degraded by effluent—which could improve with the project but may also alter local use
                     patterns. Design and procurement for the WWTP expansion will include strict environmental management, complying
                     with national regulations, mangrove protection, dam safety, and legal limits for treated effluent and sludge
                     management. The RMJP water system is supplied by 3 existing dams – related dams. The Project will not fund
                     interventions on the existing dams. It will support the preparation of the dam safety plans of the three related dams.
                                                                                                                                  Moderate
                     A.2 Social Risk Rating
                     The Project, as Phase 1 of the Integrated Water Security Program for Northeast Brazil, aims to improve water supply
                     and sanitation in João Pessoa’s Metropolitan Region and enhance water security in Paraíba. It builds on the ongoing



Public Disclosure
                     Paraiba Project (P165683). Moderate social impacts are expected under Component 2, mainly due to physical and
                     economic displacement. The WWTP site, though state-owned, is occupied by families and an informal mineral
                     extraction operation. These impacts are addressed in the approved Resettlement Action Plan (RAP), which covers 49
                     PAPs/areas including households with residents, land occupants, economic activities, workers and service providers.
                     Cadaster, consultation and pre-negotiation have already been conducted with PAPs. RAP’s implementation will begin
                     with fencing off the WWTP site and will conclude with compensation for those affected within the next 12 months.
                     The team will assess the status of PAPs during preparation. Risks related to community health and safety during
                     construction and operation of the WWTP will be managed via the C-ESMPs. Major labor influx is not expected; risks
                     will be managed through worker codes of conduct, worker training and community sensitization training, accessible
                     grievance mechanisms, and contractor management clauses and community health and safety management
                     measures. A SEP will ensure community consultation and address concerns, especially given local socioeconomic
                     challenges. Other Component 2 activities, like reducing water losses in RJMP, will have minimal E&S impacts and use
                     non-destructive methods. Rural sanitation work will focus on studies for the legal structuring of the sector, while
                     Component 1 will support key management systems for water resources, improved licensing, and participatory water
                     quality standards. Component 3 will strengthen the E&S risk management capacity.

                      The JPMR interventions are expected to benefit local ecosystems by reducing raw wastewater spillovers and
                      minimizing treated effluent discharges into the Paraiba River estuary and mangroves. These improvements will
                      contribute to public health, environmental sustainability, and economic development in the region. Engineering
                      studies for the Gramame adductor system are in early stages, whereas improvements to João Pessoa’s wastewater
                      services are advanced, with preliminary engineering completed, an environmental license (LP 3061/2022) issued, and
                      the E&S Assessment and Management Plan approved by the Bank RAS. Project sites are urban or peri-urban, and
                      mitigation measures will address direct impacts such as pollution, WWTP effluent discharge standards, construction-
                      related noise, dust, waste, and disruptions to local traffic and infrastructure. These measures will be detailed in the
                      project’s Operation Manual and ESF instruments and will comply with EHSGs.

                      ESS10 - Stakeholder Engagement and Information Disclosure                                                      Relevant

                      ESS10 is relevant. The borrower will utilize good practices and lessons learned from the previous operation. Likely
                      stakeholders include local communities near project sites, residents of the João Pessoa Metropolitan Region, relevant
                      government agencies (including AESA, ARPB, CAGEPA, and SEIRH), and potentially civil society organizations (CSOs)
                      working on water and sanitation issues in Paraíba. Vulnerable groups may include low-income households, and
                      people affected by physical and economic displacement. The SEP will define stakeholder engagement and
                      information sharing throughout each project phase. It will ensure accessible formats, meaningful consultation,
                      feedback channels, and special measures for vulnerable groups. An advanced draft SEP will be prepared and disclosed
                      by Appraisal.

                      ESS4 - Community Health and Safety                                                                           Relevant

                      This standard is relevant. Component 2 includes expanding and upgrading wastewater treatment in the RMJP and
