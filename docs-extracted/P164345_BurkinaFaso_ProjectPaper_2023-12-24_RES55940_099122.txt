I. PROGRAM STATUS AND RATIONALE FOR RESTRUCTURING



A. Program status

1. The Burkina Faso Water Supply and Sanitation Program - P164345 (Programme d’Approvisionnement en Eau et
   d’Assainissement, PAEA) aims to improve access to water supply and sanitation services in targeted areas of the
   country. The Program was approved in June 2018 and became effective in December 2018. The IDA financing of
   US$300 million, combining a US$250 million Credit and a US$50 million Grant, contributes to a Program totaling
   approximatively US$365 million (including US$65 million representing GoBF and beneficiaries’ contribution), under
   the Government’s National programs for Water Supply and Sanitation, and Integrated Water Resources
   Management (Programme National pour la Gestion des Ressources en Eau, PN-GIRE). The PAEA is implemented by
   the Ministry in charge of water and by the national urban water utility (Office National de l’Eau et de
   l’Assainissement, ONEA). These entities have established fully staffed implementation units.

2. Implementation progress (IP) and project ratings. Currently, the project’s overall progress towards achievement
   of the Program Development Objective (PDO) and overall IP are both rated Moderately Satisfactory. The Program
   has reached more than 1,220,000 beneficiaries and is projected to reach more than 1,760,000 beneficiaries in the
   first quarter of 2024. The Program Technical, Disbursement Linked Indicators (DLI) and the Monitoring and
   Evaluation System ratings are deemed Satisfactory. The Fiduciary Systems have been rated as 'Moderately
   Satisfactory' since the Implementation Status Report #2 in 2019, due to delays in achieving results for the first year.
   This is particularly the case for activities linked to water resources management. Until now, national bottlenecks in
   procurement (specifically processing time) have prevented an improvement in the rating. As of November 2023,
   one month before the initial planned closing date of December 31, 2023, the Program has disbursed US$196.98
   million, representing 71.2 percent of the total. An additional US$7.6 million is expected to be disbursed in early
   January 2024, which would bring the disbursement rate to 74 percent. If the proposed restructuring is approved,
   based on the progress already achieved, a new disbursement request could be processed by the end of January
   2024. This would be equivalent to US$26 million, and the cumulative disbursement ratio would then exceed 83%.

 3. Results achieved to date. A month from the original project closing date, the implementation of the four key result
    areas of the PAEA (Water Supply, Sanitation, Water Resources Knowledge, and Human Capital) has been impactful.
    As of November 2023, 829,660 people (of which at least 3,900 identified Internally Displaced Persons - IDPs) were
    provided with access to improved water (72 percent of the initial target – RA#1) and 406,800 people (of which at
    least 13,000 IDPs) benefited from access to improved sanitation facilities (30 percent of the target – RA#2). The
    Program has also developed an additional water production capacity of 23,565 m3/day in both urban and rural
    areas, which surpasses the initial target of 17,544 m3/day. On the water sector reform, the government is working
    on the application of a new urban tariff structure based on the urban tariff study completed under the program. In
    rural areas, the Tariff Reduction Decree was adopted in October 2019, to provide more equity between rural and
    urban tariffs1. Progress by results area (RA) is summarized as follows.

 4. Improved access to water supply (RA#1). To date, 829,660 people were provided with access to an improved water
    source under the Program, of which 552,050 in urban areas and 307,610 in rural areas.

5. Improved Access to sanitation (RA#2). To date, 406,800 people were provided with access to improved sanitation
   facilities under the Program, of which, 312,290 in urban areas and 94,510 in rural areas. Less than 100 cabins have
   been constructed (and results achieved are only in urban areas). After a first unfruitful procurement process,
   contracts for rural areas have now been awarded, and the works will be launched in early January 2024.

6. Improved access to reliable information on Water Resources (RA#3) shows some progress but corresponding
   infrastructure remains delayed. Several studies have been completed and contribute to this RA, namely the
   optimization of the hydrometric and water quality networks, bathymetric studies carried out for 62 reservoirs
   (dams) to estimate sedimentation levels that could inform measures to be taken regarding water resource
   prevention and safety plan, and seismic prospecting transects (195 km) to characterize of the aquifers of the
   Western sedimentary zone. Other studies and explorations are ongoing. However, the construction of deep
   boreholes is still delayed due to several factors such as (i) the technical complexity of the activities which required
   more preparation and procurement time than initially planned, (ii) the degradation of the security context,
   necessitating changes in the location of exploration areas, and (iii) the receipt of unsatisfactory bids twice, in July
   2022 and July 2023.

7. Human Capital strengthening (RA#4) has shown tangible results, as follows: (i) 174 graduates and undergraduates
   registered in technical training programs (62 % of the target), of which 122 have successfully completed their
   program, and all but one have been integrated as civil servants; and (ii) 3,599 people benefited from short training
   (far beyond the initial target of 1334). The program has also provided technical and training programs to 27 women
   (80% of the target); in addition, 29 people are currently engaged in specialized training programs and 7 in doctoral
   programs.

8. Results certification. To certify the above-mentioned results, the International Institute of Water and
   Environmental Engineering (2iE) has been recruited as the Program’s Independent Verification Agent (IVA). The
   first campaign of verification of the Disbursement Linked Indicators (DLIs) achievement has been held in April-May
   2020 and the eighth one has been completed on November 16, 2023.

9. The remaining contracts to be signed within the program are : (i) the contract for the National Water Information
   System to be awarded to a new contractor following the termination of the previous one, (ii) the contracts for deep
   boreholes construction, (iii) possible contracts or amendements to existing contracts for works completion due to
   the failure of previous contractors for latrines and small water network systems in rural areas.

10. Supporting resilience in a changing context. The PAEA has played an important role in Burkina Faso in contributing
    to improved access to water and sanitation services and promoting hygiene practices in both urban and rural areas.
    These efforts have been particularly crucial in preventing and responding to the threat posed by COVID-19.
    Furthermore, the funding provided to ONEA has helped the utility to address the impact of various social measures
    taken by the government, such as providing free water at standpipes and the first 8m3 of the home connection

     monthly bill free of charge. This emergency liquidity support has been essential in ensuring that the utility is able
     to continue providing uninterrupted service to urban and peri-urban areas despite potential cash shortages.

 11. Security. The results described above were obtained despite a fragile implementation period impacted by a
     deteriorating security environment affecting the Sahel region and the Burkina Faso in particular. Various security
     incidents slowed down implementation starting in 2019, with particularly challenging periods in 2021 and 2022,
     including theft of equipment and the kidnapping of workers2. The client with the Bank support, adjusted to these
     events and integrated flexible measures and alternative options including the selection of new sites. Following the
     Country Manager initiative, recommendations to support implementation of selected World Bank projects
     (including PAEA) were released in June 2023 by the Ministries in charge of defense and security. These
     recommendations (which relate to security status and specific field arrangements to each location and in close
     collaboration with the Defense and Security Departments) would help accelerate activity implementation under
     the Program when works resume in rural areas.

 12. Environmental and social (E&S) and fiduciary aspects of implementation. At this stage of implementation, the
     Program is not facing substantial fiduciary, environmental and social issues. Following fatalities linked to activities
     implemented under the program in June and July 2022, works were suspended, an audit of the E&S management
     system was carried out, and corrective measures identified. These measures were implemented satisfactorily,
     allowing works to resume in March 2023 in urban areas and in early December 2023 in rural areas. Consequently,
     at the end of the December mission support, the E&S rating has been revised from MU to MS. As recommended by
     the Environmental and Social Incident Response Tool kit (ESIRT), a first notification memo and an after-action memo
     have been submitted and cleared by the Bank Management. The Bank team will ensure that the Program
     implements residual measures such as (i) closing the remaining pits (numbering 513) as soon as accessible regarding
     field security conditions, (ii) taking out all-risk insurance for all companies in charge of the works, and (iii)
     implementing the required measures throughout the works. The World Bank Task Team also confirms that at the
     time of this proposed restructuring there is no overdue audit and neither the loan, nor the country, is subject to an
     ongoing suspension of disbursements.

13. Mid-term review. The Program mid-term review was completed in October 2022 with the following main
    conclusions : (i) the Water Supply (RA#1) and Human Capital (RA#4) results areas are on track; (ii) the Sanitation
    results area (RA#2) needs to be closely monitored due to implementation delays and E&S concerns; (iii) improved
    access to reliable information on Water Resources result area (RA#3) would be achieved between 12 to 18 months
    after the current Program's closing date.



B. Rationale for Restructuring

14. The proposed restructuring is required to incorporate the recommendations agreed in the MTR, that are aimed at
    improving project implementation before closing, and to allow ample time to prepare an AF requested by the
    government to scale up the program. In this sense, the following changes are proposed under this restructuring: (i)
    extend Program’s closing date, (ii) expand its geographical scope, and (iii) amend results framework and DLIs matrix
    in the financing agreements. The rationale for each of these modifications is presented below.

15. Extension of Program Closing date: The Program became effective on December 19, 2018. However, the
    deterioration of the security context had a substantial impact on the pace of Program implementation in both rural
    and urban areas (e.g., redesigning and repositioning of the Kaya water treatment plant from the edge of Lake Dem,
    subject of terrorist threats, to a site inside the city). The January and September 2022 coups d'état had
    repercussions on the implementation of the Program due to (i) changes of government with impacts in the
    organization of the Ministry in charge of water over the last two years, which moved from the Ministry of Water
    and Sanitation to the Ministry of Water Energy, Environment, Water and Sanitation, then to the Ministry of
    Environment, Water and Sanitation, with four Ministers, and four Managing Directors of the National Utility
    (ONEA); (ii) delays in processing invoices with Treasury. The challenging political context described above as well as
    the need to consolidate the good results achieved so far, support the GoBF's request for an additional 18-month
    extension, i.e., June 30, 2025. The proposed extension would also allow processing for the Additional Financing
    (AF), requested by GoBF. The AF will scale up the Program results, with a particular focus on water supply and
    sanitation provision to IDPs and strengthening services and infrastructure resilience.
        The World Bank
        Burkina Faso Water Supply and Sanitation Program (P164345)


16. Amendment of results framework and DLIs matrix: Based on the results of the MTR and the status of
    implementation of the Program, changes are proposed to the indicator targets to better reflect Program
    performance. Given achievements under the Water Supply RA#1 and the lag in roll out of community cabins and
    household facilities under Sanitation RA#2, the associated indicator targets will be increased and reduced,
    respectively. Moreover, DLIs and results linked to Water Resources activities that cannot be completed within the
    Program timeline will be deleted. The proposed changes will finally capture the efforts and good results in
    strengthening Human Capital. Updating the intermediate targets of the Results Framework and the Program's
    Disbursement Estimates are necessary steps to reflect actual implementation of the Program's activities since the
    first restructuring, and the revised Program Result Areas with the new closing date.

17. The World Bank has received four correspondences from the Ministry of Economy, Finance and Prospective. The
    first correspondence, dated November 22, 2022, requested a restructuring of the program to expand the
    geographic scope, and amend the results framework and DLIs matrix in the financing agreements. After this, a
    second request was sent on July 28, 2023, requesting an additional financing of US$100 million. These requests
    were further specified in the third and fourth correspondences, dated October 23, 2023, and December 19, 2023,
    the latest included an 18-month extension.



II. DESCRIPTION OF PROPOSED CHANGES

A. Summary of proposed changes

18. This restructuring paper seeks the approval of the Country Director to undertake the following changes: (i) extend
    the program closing date; (ii) modify the geographical scope of the Program activities; (iii) amend the results
    framework to reflect ---; (iv) proceed to reallocations between DLIs; and (v) modify the disbursement estimates and
    implementation schedule. The Credit and the Grant Agreements will be amended to reflect relevant changes.

B. Change in Loan closing date

19. The Program loan closing date will be extended by one year and half, from the current December 31, 2023, to June
    30, 2025, as requested by GoBF in its letter dated December 19, 2023. A longer period is needed to allow the
    Program to be scaled up through the upcoming AF, and to secure the funding. The Financing Agreement (D330-BF
    and Cr 6255-BF) will be amended to reflect this change.

    and Part 2 (Sanitation), which are now similar, will include a total of 23 centers, both to be covered by water and
    sanitation services which will be implemented on a demand-driven basis. The four rural targeted areas are not
    changed yet; however should the security situation deteriorate, the Program activities could be shifted to new

D. Change in Results Framework

21. The intermediate targets of the indicators of the Program’s Results Framework are updated in order to reflect the
    actual implementation of the Program’s activities since the last restructuring in April 2020. Similarly, the
    intermediate targets of the indicators are also updated to reflect the revised projected implementation of the
    Result Areas of the Program onwards.

22. The improved access to water supply results area (RA#1), which has shown good achievements, will see an increase
    in the number of beneficiaries, adding 449,365, till a total of 1,607,960. The Water production capacity constructed
    under the Program is also increased from 17,544 m3/day to 28,904 m3/day to capture the already achieved results
    (23,565 m3/day) and the anticipated results. The related DLIs are 1.1, 1.2, and 2.1 and 2.2 (split DLI).

23. The improved access sanitation results area (RA#2), which is lagging, is reduced by 33%, from 1,350,000 to 900,000
    beneficiaries. To that end, the number of community cabins will be reduced from 11,250 to 4,820. To date, less than
    100 cabins have been constructed (and results achieved are only in urban areas). After a first unfruitful procurement
    process, contracts for rural areas have now been awarded, and the works will be launched in December 2023. The
    related DLIs are 4.1, 4.2, 5.1, 5.2, 5.3.

24. Improved access to reliable information on Water resources results area (RA#3). The Water Resources Management
    (WRM) ASA conducted in 2017 informed Program Concept on the initial areas to explore for groundwater knowledge
    improvement (e.g., mainly the North and West border regions with Mali, and the South-East border region with
    Benin). Because of insecurity and accessibility issues in these areas during the first two years of implementation,
    Program areas were constantly under review during the period. After a first unfruitful procurement process in 2022,
    a second attempt launched in July 2023 is also likely to be partially unfruitful (diagraphy or drilling). As a result, the
    associated works would not be completed by mid-2025. In the absence of an extension, this would require the
    transfer of these activities to another project (i.e., Burkina Faso Water Security Project, P179094). Besides the
    difficulty to award contracts for diagraphy and drilling, exploration campaigns will also be limited due to the visibility
    of heavy equipment that may attract terrorists' attention. Given that no disbursements had yet been made under
    this results area, only DLIs that are likely to be achieved before the end of 2024 are maintained (DLIs 6.1.1 seismic
    campaign and 6.2 Productive boreholes). The others would be removed (DLIs 6.1.2, 6.1.3, and 6.1.4). Given the size
    of the exploration program, this will only result in a partial cancellation. The cancelled amounts would be reallocated
    between the Water Supply Results Areas and Human Capital.

25. The Human Capital strengthening results area (RA#4) has been showing good results since Program inception. It is
    readjusted (i) to consider the opposition of the Ministry in charge of Local Government for the recruitment and
    training of municipal staff in charge of civil engineering matters (water included) (ii) to take into account graduation
    date of key programs including PhD and the number of graduates integrated as civil servants , (iii) leverage the
    positive outcome of short-term training (which exceeded targets) (iv) introduce certification programs (which are
    neither graduate nor short-term programs but are still very relevant for capacity building in environmental and social
    aspects, fiduciary aspects About point (iv) above, the certification programs will contribute to the joint
    Governance/CMU initiative for reaching a critical mass of national professionals. So, introducing a new DLI could
    better capture all the efforts made to improve people skills in project management. The related DLIs are: 7.1.1, 7.1.2,
    7.2.1,7.2.2, 7.2.3 (new DLI), 7.3.1, 7.3.2, 7.4, 7.5.
