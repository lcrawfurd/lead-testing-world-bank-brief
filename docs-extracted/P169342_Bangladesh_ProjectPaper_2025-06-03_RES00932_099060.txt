I. PROJECT STATUS AND RATIONALE FOR RESTRUCTURING
1. Background: The Bangladesh Rural WASH for Human Capital Development Project was approved by the World Bank
   Board on September 25, 2020, and became effective on May 10, 2021.The total cost of the Project is US$550.5
   million. The Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB) and World Bank are jointly financing US$400 million in equal
   shares, and the Government of Bangladesh is providing US$150.5 million in counterpart funding which is being
   facilitated by the Palli Karma-Sahayak Foundation (PKSF). The Project is executed by two implementing agencies:
   Department of Public Health Engineering (DPHE) and the PKSF, with the objective to: (i) improve access to ‘safely
   managed’ water supply and sanitation in selected areas of rural Bangladesh; and (ii) strengthen sector institutional
   capacity for water and sanitation. PKSF activities are executed on the ground by Partner Organizations (PO) in charge
   of supporting the poor and non-poor households to access micro-credit to install toilets for introducing safely
   managed sanitation facilities, improve water services, and WASH market development.

2. Project status. Progress towards the Project Development Objective is moderately satisfactory, and Implementation
   Progress is also currently rated moderately satisfactory.


3. Upon the request of the Government of Bangladesh (GoB), the proposed restructuring involves the complete
   withdrawal of the counterpart contribution from the PKSF.


4. Implementation progress

    Component 1: Investments in water supply
    Development of large-piped water schemes by DPHE experienced a delayed start due to land related issues. However,
    the tenders for all the 54 schemes under 22 packages have now been awarded, and implementation is underway,
    physical progress is 11.25 percent as of April 30, 2025. All schemes are expected to be completed by October 2026.
    These schemes serve 85,050 beneficiaries or 14 percent of the target.


   DPHE has made considerable progress with the development of small-piped water schemes. Physical progress is
   eighty-three percent as of April 30, 2025. However, 418 schemes of the total 3,271 schemes are not expected to be
   complete by October 2025. A total of 1,287 schemes (including 228 schemes under Component 5 on COVID-19
   emergency response) have been commissioned and are providing water to 203,346 beneficiaries of the targeted
   516,285 beneficiaries.

   Micro-finance institutions’ loans for water supply (PKSF): As of March 31, 2025, 93,387 water points in households,
   against an overall target of 120,000 (78 percent) have been installed through MFI loans amounting to US$26.96
   million.

    The feasibility study for climate-risk hotspots in coastal areas (DPHE) is ongoing and scheduled for completion in July
    2025.

    Component 2: Investments in sanitation and hygiene:
    Public toilets and toilets in community health clinics: DPHE is responsible for constructing public toilets in areas with
    high pedestrian traffic, including markets, bus stations, and community health clinics. Out of 852 public toilets, 740

    the Project is completed. Both mobile and web applications are operational at present.

    Component 4: Project implementation and management: Both DPHE and PKSF have set up their Project
    Management Units (PMU), headed by the respective Project Director and Project Coordinator and supported by
    deputed and recruited staff, and are functioning smoothly. In addition, the respective Project Steering Committees
    have been formed and are holding meetings.

    Component 5: COVID-19 emergency response: This component provided WASH services where needed in the
    COVID-19 pandemic hotspots. Construction of 829 hand washing stations (out of 882) has been completed, and the
    remaining stations are under construction.

5. Financial Management: The project consistently submitted Interim Unaudited Financial Reports (IUFRs), withdrawal
   applications, and other related disclosures in a timely manner. A desk review indicates that all recent submissions up
   to December 2024 for both PIUs were acceptable, with a few exceptions from the Financial Management perspective
   regarding reporting requirements and financial disclosure. Both PMUs have timely submitted the project audit report
   for the fiscal year 2023-2024, with the auditor expressing an unqualified opinion on the project financial statements.
   DPHE is actively implementing measures to address the observations noted in the audit report.

6. Rationale for Restructuring: In the first two years, PKSF activities experienced field level challenges, due to Covid-19
   pandemic and start-up issues. This impacted the generation of WASH revolving funds, the main source of counterpart
   funding. As a result, the percentage of counterpart funding was revised during the first restructuring that was
   approved in April 2024, as described further in Section II below. That restructuring also included the expansion of
   geographical locations from 78 to 182 Upazila (subdistricts) to enable the PKSF to reach more beneficiaries through
   their POs. Considering expansion of geographical areas, the PKSF was expected to increase the disbursement of their
   loans to additional households. However, they managed to disburse only 441,170 sanitation loans as of March 2025
   against the target of one million of which 500,000 loans were to be disbursed using AIIB and IDA funds and 500,000
   loans were from counterpart funding. Given the imminent project closure in October 2025, PKSF is unable to generate
   sufficient funds from loan repayments to re-invest as counterpart funding within the short remaining timeframe.
   Consequently, the withdrawal of counterpart funding will allow the approved funding from IDA and AIIB to be utilized
   effectively, enabling PKSF to achieve the targeted beneficiaries stated in the result framework using IDA and AIIB
   funding. The beneficiaries in the result framework of the PAD were counted solely based on the investment from AIIB

II. DESCRIPTION OF PROPOSED CHANGES

7. The total financing for PKSF is US$328.94 million, which includes the IDA and AIIB contribution of US$184.48 million
   and counterpart funding of US$144.46 million. Out of US$328.94 million, the PKSF planned to provide US$235.4
   million as WASH loans to one million households of which US$135.32 million will be provided by the Government of
   Bangladesh as counterpart funding. According to the revised Financing Agreement for the subcomponent 1.3 (MFI
   loan for households for water facility improvement) approved as per the first restructuring, the financing ratios for
   years 1–2 were: 25 percent each by IDA and the AIIB, and 50 percent by the PKSF; for year 3: 37.50 percent each by
   IDA and the AIIB and 25 percent by PKSF; for year 4: 20 percent each by IDA and the AIIB and 60 percent by PKSF; for
   year 5: 11.50 percent each by IDA and the AIIB and 77 percent by PKSF. Similarly, for subcomponent 2.2.a, which
   involves MFI loans to households for installing sanitation and hygiene facilities, the financing ratios were as follows:
   40.5 percent each by IDA and the AIIB, and 19.0 percent by the PKSF in years 1-2; 37.50 percent each by IDA and the
   AIIB and 25 percent by PKSF in year 3; 20 percent each by IDA and the AIIB and 60 percent by PKSF in year 4; and
   10.75 percent each by IDA and the AIIB and 78.50 percent by PKSF in year 5.

8. Proposed Changes: Removal of counterpart funding and revisions to the disbursement percentage of AIIB and IDA
   funding for MFI Loans: The Government of Bangladesh, via an ERD letter dated March 16, 2025, has officially
   requested to remove the counterpart funding condition of the recipient for financing of MFI loans under Part 1.3 and
   Part 2.2 (a) of the Project. In the same letter, the Government has also requested the Association to remove this
   condition retroactivity from July 1, 2024, which is not feasible due to disbursements already made for year 4 as per
   the financing agreement. Under IDA share, going forward, revised financing percentage for year 5 will be at 50% for
   MFI loans and will be effective from the date of countersignature of the amendment letter. The AIIB co-lender
   agreement is also in the process of being amended to finance the remaining 50% for MFI loans for year 5.
