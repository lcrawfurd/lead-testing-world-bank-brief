                     Proposed Development Objective

                     The Development Objective of the Series of Projects (SoP) is to strengthen the shared management of selected Pacific
                     Island oceanic and coastal fisheries, and the critical habitats upon which they depend. The Development Objective of
                     this project is to improve access to economic opportunities for fishers and fish workers, increase climate-resilient
                     domestic supply of fish products, and strengthen national capacity and regional collaboration for fisheries management.

                     C. Summary Description of Proposed Project Activities
                     The project will support three priority investment areas: i) tuna industry domestication, ii) small- and medium-size
                     fisheries and aquaculture value chain development, and iii) fisheries governance and institutions' strengthening. Project
                     themes and activities will evolve around: - Development of enabling infrastructure for efficient dockside inspections,
                     offloading, and transshipment in Luganville, Espiritu Santo. - Work force professionalization through vocational training
                     for fishers and fish industry workers. - Technology incubation to foster hands-on training, innovation support and

                     enterprise development in seafood products development. - Food security through value chain development of small
                     and medium scale fisheries, in particular through investments in cold chain systems and fish markets. - Strengthening of
                     local fisher groups in Santo, to partake in sector management and development, including development of satellite
                     distribution systems and inter-island fish trading. - Aquaculture development through upgrading hatcheries in Luganville
                     and providing seed supply for development small-scale and semi-commercial commercial aquaculture in response to
                     high protein demand. - Institutional strengthening including construction of VFD headquarter offices. - Monitoring,
                     Control, Surveillance and Enforcement, including improved regional cooperation. The project will be implemented by
                     the Vanuatu Fisheries Department under the Ministry of Fisheries, Oceans, and Maritime Affairs (MFOMA).


                     D. Environmental and Social Overview
                     D.1 Overview of Environmental and Social Project Settings
                     Vanuatu is a small island nation located in the South Pacific with fisheries being pivotal to the nation’s economy and
                     food security, especially for communities heavily dependent on coastal and oceanic fisheries. Vanuatu’s oceanic
                     fisheries rely heavily on foreign vessels operating under licensing agreements, with limited domestic processing. Coastal
                     fisheries are integral to subsistence livelihoods and local economies, with an estimated 50 percent of households
                     participating in fishing activities.

                     The project aims to strengthen national capacity and regional collaboration for fisheries. The institutional strengthening
                     and capacity building, as well as specific investment activities will focus on strengthening the domestic value chain for
                     paving the way to enhance regional integration.
                     The project interventions will focus on strengthening the domestic value chain for fishing stocks managed through



Public Disclosure
                     Western and Central Pacific Ocean (WCPO) fishery which is recognized globally for its governance and institutions to
                     manage fish resources at sustainable levels. Nevertheless, there is risk from climate change impacts (include warming
                     waters and shifting currents) which could alter fish distribution and changes in the stocks available for sustainable
                     fishing. Monitoring and management controls in place at the national, sub-regional (PNA) and regional (WCPO) levels
                     represent an effective framework to manage sustainable fishing levels. WCPO purse seine fisheries has obtained
                     certification in accordance with MSC Fisheries Standard which is the only globally recognized wild catch seafood
                     certificate that conform to the ISEAL Code of Good Practice for Setting Social and Environmental Standards.

                     Country is vulnerable to climate change which transcends to impacts on project infrastructure particularly from the
                     extreme weather events such as cyclones, extreme precipitation, flooding, sea-level rise, coastal erosion, soil
                     salinization.

                     D.2 Overview of Borrower’s Institutional Capacity for Managing Environmental and Social Risks and Impacts
                     Vanuatu Fishery Department (VFD), as the department responsible for fisheries, within the Ministry of Fisheries, Oceans
                     and Maritime Affairs (MFOMA) will lead implementation. Project Support Team (PST) will be established within VFD to
                     assist with project management. VFD has no prior experience implementing a World Bank funded project. MFOMA was
                     established in October 2024 and is in the process of setting up its institutional and operational arrangements. As such, a
                     project preparation advance (PPA) is planned to advance establishment of the PST and key technical and feasibility
                     studies. The PST will include at least a project coordinator, a financial management officer, a procurement officer and an
                     environmental and social risk officer, supported by technical consultants as required.

                     II. SCREENING OF POTENTIAL ENVIRONMENTAL AND SOCIAL RISKS AND IMPACTS

                     A. Environmental and Social Risk Classification (ESRC)                                                           Substantial


                     A.1 Environmental Risk Rating                                                                                    Substantial
                     The project involves technical assistance and physical investment activities, posing substantial environmental risks.
                     Land reclamation for component 1 infrastructure may impact coastal dynamics, marine habitats, and water quality.
                     Jetty construction could alter coastal processes, increase turbidity, and cause water contamination from accidental
                     spills. Changes in water circulation due to the jetty might affect water quality, reducing oxygen levels or increasing
                     nutrients. Building on reclaimed land risks ground instability and soil liquefaction. The seafood technology incubator
                     may intensively use water, exacerbating risks due to limited availability, and generate significant wastewater with
                     high pollution, impacting aquatic environments. Component 2 activities risk unsustainable fish stock harvesting in
                     fisheries value chains. IBAT data review identified IUCN CR and EN species in the project area, necessitating
                     biodiversity impact assessments. Contextual risks include Vanuatu's FCV context, vulnerability to natural hazards like



Public Disclosure
                     floods, cyclones, and storm surges, with climate change expected to increase infrastructure vulnerability. Vanuatu's
                     environmental regulations have gaps compared to ESS requirements, with inconsistent enforcement, especially
                     locally. The Vanuatu Department of Environmental Protection and Conservation (DEPC) administers environmental
                     laws but struggles with full implementation and enforcement. Moreover, the Implementing Agency has not prior
                     experience implementing World Bank funded projects.
                                                                                                                                      Substantial
                     A.2 Social Risk Rating
                     The proposed project is rated Substantial due to potential adverse social impacts related to land acquisition,
                     livelihoods, labor influx, and vulnerable communities. It involves infrastructure development, including a
                     multipurpose jetty, onshore facilities, fish markets, and a new VFD headquarters, alongside expanding fisheries and
                     aquaculture activities. Key social risks include: (ESS5) Infrastructure construction may require new land use,
                     potentially leading to displacement and impacting livelihoods, especially for informal users or those with customary
                     tenure. Access to resources and traditional fishing grounds needs careful assessment. Impacts on Livelihoods and
                     Access to Resources (ESS5): Changes in fishing practices and market dynamics could affect small-scale fishers,
                     aquaculture farmers, and communities reliant on marine resources. The transition may exclude certain groups from
                     new opportunities or traditional access. Labor Influx and Associated Risks (ESS2, ESS4): Construction and operation of
                     facilities may lead to labor influx, straining local services, increasing competition for resources, and potentially causing
                     social tensions. Risks include communicable diseases and Sexual Exploitation, Abuse, and Harassment (SEA/SH).
                     Impacts on Vulnerable Groups (ESS1, ESS7): Diverse communities may be vulnerable due to reliance on subsistence
                     activities, customary land tenure, or limited access to information. Gender inequalities need addressing to prevent
                     exacerbating vulnerabilities or creating new ones. Community Health and Safety (ESS4): Construction activities and

                     B.1 Relevance of Environmental and Social Standards

                      ESS1 - Assessment and Management of Environmental and Social Risks and Impacts                                  Relevant

                      The project is likely to have substantial environmental and moderate social risks, due to from its support to multiple
                      physical activities (as described in section A.1), and downstream activities under TA. feasibility study/engineering
                      design for a large-scale seafood processing hub, coupled with the design of a fishmeal plant. Physical activities will
                      have substantial environmental and social impacts in terms. Potential impacts are related to of land use, noise/dust
                      nuisance, water pollution and construction waste management, traffic impact, community disturbance, construction
                      related risks such as health and safety risks during construction stage, as well as waste and pollution discharge,
                      worker health and safety risks, coastal ecological impacts, risks on biosecurity and exclusion of marginalized peoples
                      from project benefits etc. during the operation. The preparation phase will be used to assess these risks and develop
                      appropriate instruments including the ESCP, SEP and LMP.

                      ESS10 - Stakeholder Engagement and Information Disclosure                                                       Relevant

                      ESS10 is relevant. The project is anticipated to involve a wide range of stakeholders including local communities
                      directly impacted by fishing activities, fisherfolks, vulnerable groups such as women, elderly, people with disabilities
                      and poor and IP fishing communities, fish vendors, retailers, market operators and suppliers as well as members of
                      fish cooperatives and associations, businesses owners and relevant government agencies. The project will comply

                      ESS2 - Labor and Working Conditions                                                                             Relevant

                      Potential labor risks include occupational health and safety (OHS) hazards associated with construction activities and
                      fishing operations, as well as issues related to terms and conditions of employment for contracted staff. A
                      comprehensive Labor Management Procedures (LMP) will be prepared by the Borrower to address these risks and
                      ensure compliance with ESS2. The project aims to create jobs for approximately 500 people. The LMP will outline the
                      labor requirements, associated risks, and necessary procedures to manage labor issues effectively. Key measures with
                      include enforcing OHS standards for onshore and at sea, preventing child and forced labor, and establishing a
                      Grievance Redress Mechanism (GRM) to address worker concerns. An LMP will address how the project can enforce
                      national labor laws and ESS2 requirements, ensuring safe and healthy working conditions, fair wages, transparent
                      terms of employment in addition to codes of conduct and other measures within the SEA-SH AP.

                      ESS3 - Resource Efficiency and Pollution Prevention and Management                                                 Relevant

                      The project will have resource efficiency and pollution discharge implications during construction and operation
                      stages, especially the operation of office buildings, and seafood product development facility which is likely to water
                      resource intensive. Significant amount of wastewater with high pollution load is also likely to be generated from
                      seafood incubator facility and from jetty operations. Lack of adequate treatment will impact aquatic and marine
                      environments. Construction phase will generate construction and demolition waste; waste from seafood incubator
                      operations includes: (a) putrescible by-product waste from inedible fish parts; (b) process rejects; (c) chemical waste;
                      and (d) sludge from wastewater treatment plant. Inadequate waste management may lead to chemical and organic
                      wastes entering and polluting soil and water resources. End of life disposal risks of the E-waste. The resources
                      efficiency and pollution management will be addressed through the E&S Instruments.

                      ESS4 - Community Health and Safety                                                                                 Relevant

                      The project will impact community health and safety during construction and operation phases. Risks include
                      construction hazards, increased traffic, labor influx, and emergency preparedness needs. Construction activities may
                      cause noise, dust, and hazardous material spills, while increased traffic poses safety risks. Labor influx could strain
                      services and increase social conflict risks. Emergency preparedness is crucial due to Vanuatu's vulnerability to natural
                      disasters and potential project-related accidents. Hazardous materials management involves handling fuels and
                      chemicals, with risks of spills affecting communities. Universal access principles will ensure infrastructure accessibility
                      for all, including people with disabilities. These risks will be assessed and managed through various studies and plans,
                      including the E&S Scoping/Initial Assessment study, SEP, LMP, SEA/SH AP, and later through project ESIA and

                      ESS5 - Land Acquisition, Restrictions on Land Use and Involuntary Resettlement                                     Relevant

                      Involuntary land acquisition is excluded from the project scope. Certain components may restrict land use, potentially
                      causing physical or economic displacement. Construction activities, such as land reclamation and building a
                      multipurpose jetty and new facilities, may require land leasing or affect formal and informal land users. While the
                      VFD headquarters is planned on government-owned land, the status of other sites is uncertain. Aquaculture
                      development and a seafood hub may restrict access to traditional fishing grounds, affecting livelihoods. The ESCP will
                      require the application of ESS5, including i) land screening for all project sites to identify risks of physical or economic
                      displacement, regardless of title status and ii) preparation and implementation of Resettlement Plans (RP) where
                      required, consistent with ESS5 provisions.

                      ESS7 - Indigenous Peoples/Sub-Saharan African Historically Underserved Traditional                               Relevant
                      Local Communities

                      ESS7 is relevant. At the country level, Vanuatu meets the ESS7 IP criteria; the country has distinct social and cultural
                      groups, 98% of lands are held in customary ownership, separate customary institutions are present and the country
                      has over 100 recognized indigenous languages. Project activities could interact with communities exhibiting
                      characteristics ESS7. The project's focus on coastal and oceanic fisheries, and its operations in Vanuatu, a country
                      with diverse traditional communities heavily reliant on marine resources, indicate the potential presence of such
                      groups within the project's area of influence. Therefore, E&S Scoping/Initial Assessment undertaken during project
                      preparation will confirm their presence, characteristics, and the nature of potential project impacts. If relevant, IP
                      considerations will be streamlined into all project instruments. IP measures will focus on culturally appropriate
                      approaches to stakeholder engagement, grievance redress and benefit sharing.
