 B. Introduction and Context

 Country Context
 Despite significant untapped potential for diversification, growth, and job creation in sectors such as tourism, mining,
 agriculture, and fisheries, Argentina’s regional economies face infrastructure and institutional gaps. Employment is
 dominated by non-tradable sectors, and firm density and productive integration outside the Buenos Aires Metropolitan
 Area remain low, with few opportunities for economies of scale and low integration into regional and global value chains.
 As the country’s economy stabilizes, interventions that support private investment and job creation are gaining
 importance, with an increase in the governance and decision-making responsibilities to be taken on by the provincial
 governments. The proposed Multi-phase Programmatic Approach (MPA) aims to support Argentina’s provinces in the
 Patagonia and Northwest regions in implementing strategic infrastructure and institutional measures that can create
 jobs, crowd in private investment, and ensure resilient and inclusive development.
 Sectoral and Institutional Context
 The province of Salta is characterized by incomes below the national average and remoteness from major economic
 centers but also significant potential in several productive sectors. Lithium mining and associated value-added
 activities have become a rapidly expanding source of formal employment and export revenues and generate strong
 spillovers. Moreover, the province is a significant agricultural producer, and tourism has the potential to create jobs
 and promote social inclusion. To take advantage of this productive potential, Salta must address its connectivity and
 infrastructure gaps, which imply high costs for the productive sectors and reduce their competitiveness. Improved and
 resilient road and rail connectivity and multi-modal nodes and logistics facilities will become increasingly important as
 lithium and other mining related cargo volumes expand rapidly, to reduce costs, achieve economies of scale, connect
 local firms to global value chains. Private sector participation will be essential, as it can bring investment capital,
 expertise, and operational innovation. In addition to infrastructure and logistics gaps, the province needs to reinforce
 its institutional capacity and technical knowledge base to manage the rapid growth of the mining projects, attract
 responsible investment, and safeguard environmental and social sustainability.
Project interventions aim at (i) supporting strategic physical infrastructure and institutions to improve market access and
leverage key strategic sector value chains, (ii) improving provincial connectivity and logistics to support economic
diversification and growth, (iii) enhancing resilience to climate risks in vulnerable areas, (iv) strengthening environmental
and social risk management systems of the mining sector, and (v) promote private sector solutions.

 D. Concept Description
Phase 2 of the MPA in the province of Salta includes five components, supporting resilient infrastructure investments
and institutional capacity building. These are:
▪     Component 1: Resilient road connectivity will support rehabilitation of national road (RN) 51 and strategic studies to
      promote private sector participation in the roads sector.
▪     Component 2: Development of infrastructure for logistics nodes will support Infrastructure improvement of the
      General Güemes Logistics Node and Industrial Park and Basic infrastructure investment and planning of the Olacapato
      logistics node.
▪     Component 3: Water and sanitation will finance infrastructure works in the localities of Olacapato (drinking water
      plant and new distribution works) and San Antonio de los Cobres (wastewater treatment plant) and a strategic study
      to improve water reuse in the province.
▪     Component 4: Strengthening mining sector governance and technical capacity will finance modernization of mining
      management and oversight; strengthening oversight capacities; and geological knowledge infrastructure.
▪     Component 5: Project management will finance the overall management of the Program by a Project Implementation
      Unit and citizen engagement and outreach activities.

The combined Environmental and Social Risk Classification is High (Environmental Substantial / Social High). Large
infrastructure is not envisaged and E&S risks and impacts are considered manageable with standard mitigation measures;
however, overall risk is classified as high at this stage because (i) although the mining governance component is designed
to deliver positive outcomes, some stakeholders may still raise concerns related to environmental and social management
of the promotion of mining itself, ii) potential indirect and/or cumulative impacts on biodiversity and water could occur
as a result of the promotion of mining and other development activities, and increased traffic in the rehabilitated road, iii)
while most works are planned on existing roads or fiscal land, minimizing land acquisition, project activities in selected
sites may cause physical/economic displacement, iv) project activities may affect lands under custumary use of Indigenous
Peoples, requiring Free, Prior, and Informed Consent and culturally appropriate benefit-sharing measures, v) labor influx
may heighten the need for vigilance around risks of sexual exploitation, abuse, and harassment, vi) the Puna area is highly
sensitivity to environmental pressures and the region’s high poverty and unmet basic needs increase the risk of inequitable
benefit distribution. The project’s E&S assessments will analyze all these effects and provide appropriate measures for the
design and construction of the activities in accordance with the ESF and the ‘mitigation hierarchy’ approach. A robust
Stakeholder Engagement Plan and the Indigenous Peoples Plan will ensure inclusive consultation with vulnerable groups
and other stakeholders sensitive to mining. E&S capacity development initiatives will be part of this project with a focus
on cumulative impacts, water management, consultation, strategic communication and conflict resolution.
