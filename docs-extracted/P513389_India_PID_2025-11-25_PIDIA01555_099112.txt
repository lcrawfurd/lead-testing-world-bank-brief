                     A. Introduction and Context

                    1.      Tourism is one of the most promising sectors for economic growth and job creation in Jammu and Kashmir
                    (J&K). With its clean air, glacial lakes, alpine landscapes, mountains, meadows, and valleys, the region attracts a significant
                    number of visitors each year. Tourist arrivals reached 23.5 million in 2024, nearly twice the size of the local population.
                    The sector accounts for 7 percent of J&K’s Gross Domestic Product and supports job opportunities for locals in areas such
                    as hospitality, transportation, and tour guiding, including for unskilled and semi-skilled workers, and often through
                    employment in micro, small, and medium-sized enterprises (MSMEs).

                    2.      Tourism has significant potential to generate further livelihood opportunities in J&K where unemployment
                    stands at 6.7 percent, and youth unemployment at 17.4 percent. It can serve as an important entry point for youth and
                    women into the labor market, making it one of J&K’s most promising sectors for economic diversification and job creation.

                    3.       However, structural challenges and persistent service deficits continue to limit J&K’s ability to fully harness
                    sustainable tourism-driven growth and opportunities for job creation that can help to reduce poverty and boost
                    prosperity. These challenges include volatility and being landlocked, ecological fragility, and vulnerability to climate and
                    disaster impacts. Additionally, inadequate last-mile connectivity and insufficient service infrastructure at key sites are
                    bottlenecks. Poor quality or absent visitor facilities and limited public amenities in many destinations reduce both the
                    attractiveness of sites and the extent of local economic benefits. Seasonal surges in visitors often overwhelm fragile
                    infrastructure, highlighting deficits in water supply, waste management, and other basic services.

                    4.      Climate and disaster hazards regularly impact tourism in J&K and pose a structural constraint on the sector’s
                    sustainable development. The region is prone to floods, landslides, avalanches, and extreme rainfall, which regularly
                    damage infrastructure, disrupt connectivity, and erode the reliability of visitor services. Rising temperatures and shifting

                    snowfall patterns pose a threat to the viability of winter tourism. Disaster hazards such as the August 2025 flash floods
                    that killed over 100 people erode visitor confidence, weaken the competitiveness of destinations, and increase the
                    vulnerability of communities that rely on tourism for their livelihoods.

                    5.     Additionally, environmental and social risks are growing because of unregulated development that puts
                    pressure on fragile ecosystems and undermines the foundations for sustained growth and job creation in the sector.
                    There is a risk of replicating the challenges seen in other mountain geographies, where overcrowding and poor
                    management have degraded destinations and the environment.

                    6.      Private sector interest in J&K’s tourism sector is increasing, but the development of a diversified and sustainable
                    tourism economy can benefit from greater private sector participation. Prominent hotel chains have recently entered
                    J&K alongside local hotels, and a robust base of family-run accommodations, providing more than 60,000 units. While
                    investor interest has grown, regulatory barriers and institutional capacity gaps continue to constrain momentum. Broader
                    improvements in planning, coordination, and investor services are needed to attract private capital and professional
                    market players that can generate more livelihood opportunities for locals.

                    7.       The benefits from tourism can be spread further if the handicrafts and horticulture sectors can be better
                    integrated into the tourism value chain. J&K boasts a rich cultural heritage and a substantial base of skilled traditional
                    artisans. The handicrafts sector is the second-largest contributor to livelihoods after agriculture, making artisan livelihoods
                    a significant contributor to employment and economic growth. The handloom and handicrafts sector has demonstrated

                    steady growth, with an annual output exceeding INR 8,300 crore (US$100 million) and an annual growth rate of more than
                    5 percent. However, MSMEs continue to face challenges related to production, innovation, and market access. Stronger
                    measures are needed to expand credit access, enhance training, and link producers to national and international buyers.
                    Support for women-led MSMEs is especially critical, as women artisans face compounded barriers to scaling production
                    and accessing markets. Without deeper integration into the tourism value chain, MSMEs risk being sidelined despite
                    growing investment and visitor inflows.

                    8.       Recognizing these opportunities and constraints, the Government of J&K has positioned the tourism sector as
                    a pillar of local development under its 2021 Tourism Development Policy. The Policy emphasizes equitable and
                    ecologically sensitive growth and calls for diversifying tourism offerings, creating an enabling environment for private
                    capital, improving branding and market access, and generating jobs while reducing poverty in local communities.

                    9.      The Government of India has requested World Bank support to advance the tourism agenda in J&K, aiming to
                    establish a new model for sustainable tourism that drives job creation, fosters private sector participation, and delivers
                    benefits for local communities. This proposed engagement aims to unlock the potential of tourism to create jobs and
                    growth while safeguarding J&K’s unique environmental and social assets. The engagement will combine improved tourism
                    planning with upgrades of service infrastructure. It will mobilize private capital, expand livelihood opportunities, and
                    ensure that tourism’s benefits reach the poor, are broadly shared, environmentally sustainable, and contribute to long-
                    term resilience.

                      D. Project Description

                    10.     The proposed Project will adopt a two-pronged intervention strategy: (i) Integrated Tourism Products,
                    Destination Planning, and Management: Strengthening planning capacity and market positioning to enable local tourism-
                    led economic development; and, (ii) Integrated Service Delivery: Implementing area-based investment packages to
                    enhance tourist experience and improve livability and livelihoods for host communities, thereby creating conditions for
                    sustained private sector participation.

                    11.     The proposed Project will target selected destinations, chosen on the basis of carrying capacity, community
                    ownership, private sector potential, and prospects for livelihood impact. Each destination will be developed as part of a
                    tourism circuit connecting niche products—such as adventure, handicrafts, and wellness—to broader value chains,
                    maximizing economic multipliers. These destinations will show how strengthened resilient service delivery and improved




Public Disclosure
                    tourism management can drive local economic growth and create local jobs. The proposed Project will include the
                    following components:

                    12.      Component 1: Strategic tourism planning and local economic development (total: US$60 million, of which IBRD
                    US$42 million). This Component will identify and design tourism circuits as part of a broader tourism development strategy
                    for J&K and define the complementary and unique role of selected destinations within the broader tourism ecosystem. It
                    will help create an enabling environment for enhanced private sector participation in the tourism industry and to bolster
                    the development of selected tourist destinations. It will also provide opportunities for institutional strengthening and
                    long-term sustainability, combining tools and capacity building in tourism management (including asset management)
                    with hands-on learning and strengthening of tourism agencies through their direct involvement in project implementation.
                    Key strategies and analytical studies will inform investments across critical aspects of tourism-led development.

                    13.    Component 2: Service delivery for growth and local jobs (total: US$486 million, of which IBRD US$340 million).
                    This Component will support interrelated investments to enhance tourist sites and improve service delivery in host areas
                    to ensure effective benefit-sharing. The aim is to enhance the tourist experience while improving livability, livelihood
                    opportunities, and resilience for local communities.

                    14.      Component 3: Project management (total: US$29 million, of which IBRD US$20 million). This Component will
                    cover the costs of project management and implementation. This includes staff salaries (excluding civil servants), the hiring
                    of consultants, and training for staff and consultants in procurement, financial management, monitoring and evaluation
                    activities, as well as environmental and social management. Additional activities under this Component include a
                    perception analysis of key stakeholders to better understand needs and expectations, a longitudinal study to establish a
                    baseline and track the Project’s impact from the tourist’s perspective, and communications and stakeholder engagement.

                    Legal Operational Policies
                    15.      OP 7.50 is applicable to the Project. The Bank will exclusively finance minor additions or alterations to
                    rehabilitate or repair existing water systems in tourist sites and local villages. These activities will not adversely change
                    the quality or quantity of water flows to other riparians and will not be adversely affected by other riparians’ possible
                    water use.

                    16.     This Project complies with all operational policies of the Bank. According to Bank policies, the Bank may
                    support such a project if others concerned have no objection or if the Bank determines that the special circumstances of
                    the case warrant the Bank’s support of the project, which includes the fact that the project is not harmful to the
                    interests of others concerned. Based on a detailed assessment of the Project activities and after considering all relevant
                    information, Management confirms that these requirements are met. By supporting the proposed Project, the Bank
                    does not make any judgment on the legal or other status of the territories concerned or the rights or interests of any
                    concerned parties, nor does it prejudice any determination thereof.

                    Summary of Screening of Environmental and Social Risks and Impacts
                    17.     The environmental and social risk for the proposed Project is assessed as Substantial. This rating reflects the
                    scale and geographic distribution of proposed investments across environmentally and socially sensitive areas and client
                    risk management capacities. Environmental risks may include erosion, slope destabilization, construction-related
                    pollution, vegetation loss, disturbance to local bird habitats, and air and noise emissions. The cumulative impacts of



Public Disclosure
                    tourism could degrade environmental quality and ecosystem services. Nonetheless, the proposed Project offers strong
                    potential to promote environmental sustainability by integrating climate-resilient designs, nature-based solutions, and
                    resource and energy efficiency into its activities. Socially, the project areas encompass rural and semi-urban communities,
                    whose livelihoods are closely tied to agriculture, pastoralism, handicrafts, and seasonal tourism. Anticipated social risks
                    include temporary access restrictions and livelihood disruptions, land acquisition or the use of community lands, labor-
                    related risks, and the potential exclusion of vulnerable groups, such as scheduled tribes. The proposed Project presents
                    transformative opportunities to enhance service delivery, create employment, local entrepreneurship, and community-
                    driven tourism. To manage these risks and meet the World Bank’s Environmental and Social Framework requirements,
                    relevant instruments have been prepared and disclosed as relevant. These include the Environmental and Social
                    Management Framework (ESMF), which provides procedures for screening, risk mitigation, and institutional
                    responsibilities. The ESMF consists of Labor Management Procedures, Environmental Codes of Practice, and templates for
                    Environmental and Social Management Plans, Resettlement Policy Framework, Tribal Development Framework, Cultural
                    Heritage Management Framework, and a Gender-based Violence Prevention Action Plan. In addition, the Borrower has
                    prepared a Stakeholder Engagement Plan with a grievance redress mechanism. An Environment and Social Commitment
                    Plan has been drafted to agree on the actions, timelines, and responsibilities. Sub-project-specific instruments will be
                    prepared and disclosed prior to implementation. A Strategic Environmental and Social Assessment will be conducted
                    during the first year of implementation to inform upstream planning decisions, promote cumulative impact management,
                    and integrate sustainability principles.

                     E. Implementation

                     Institutional and Implementation Arrangements

                    18.    The Project’s institutional and implementation arrangements encompass oversight, implementation, and local
                    coordination functions.

                    19.     Oversight: A Steering Committee will provide high-level oversight and policy guidance, and take critical decisions
                    related to project management and implementation.

                    20.       Implementation: The Tourism Department will be the lead agency for the Project implementation. It will host a
                    Project Management Unit (PMU) responsible for overall planning, coordination, reporting, performance monitoring,
                    fiduciary management, and compliance with environmental and social standards. Selected Implementation Agencies (IAs)
                    will implement the three project components under the aegis of the Tourism Department. The three
                    key identified agencies at this stage are (i) the Jammu and Kashmir Tourism Development Corporation Limited (JKTDCL);
                    (ii) Public Works (R&B) Department; and (iii) Jal Shakti Department. The JKTDCL will be the nodal implementing agency for
                    all three components and serve as the Project’s interface with the private sector.

                    21.       Local coordination: The Tourism Department will ensure coordination and local level monitoring of project
                    implementation at the destination and circuit level through its local representatives – Tourism Development Authorities
