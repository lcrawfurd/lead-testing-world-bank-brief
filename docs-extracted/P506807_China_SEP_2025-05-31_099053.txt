1 Objectives and Methods of Stakeholder Engagement

Stakeholders can provide local knowledge to project planners and decision-makers,
enabling them to understand potential risks and social conflicts in the project
implementation process early and help them take measures to eliminate these potential
social risks and social conflicts. Stakeholder engagement can improve the project's
environmental and social sustainability and acceptance and contribute to the successful
design and implementation.
The project's environmental and social impact assessment results show that the project's
environmental and social risks are Substantial. The Project Management Office (PMO)
has formulated a Stakeholder Engagement Plan following the World Bank's
Environmental and Social Framework requirements. Stakeholder participation runs
through the entire project cycle. The Stakeholder Engagement Plan (SEP) is a
dynamic document that will be updated periodically as needed.

1-1 Objectives of Stakeholder Engagement

The project uses a scientific and systematic method to identify stakeholders and
establishes and maintains continuous information communication with various
stakeholders in a manner suitable for each stakeholder's culture. The project will
especially maintain effective contact with project-affected stakeholders. The project's
Stakeholder Engagement Plan mainly achieves the following objectives.
(1) To identify the stakeholders, assess their attitudes and support levels towards the
project, and consider their needs and interests in project design and environmental and
social performance.
(2) To maintain continuous communication with stakeholders throughout the project
life cycle, mainly communicate with those affected by the project on issues that may
affect their production and life, provide adequate means and channels for participation,
provide channels and procedures for stakeholders to file complaints, and ensure that
project managers can respond to and manage the complaints and appeals promptly.
(3) To ensure that PMO can disclose information on the project's environmental and

                                           1
social risks and impacts to stakeholders in a timely, understandable, and appropriate
manner. It will also enable project managers to obtain stakeholders' needs and
suggestions.
(4) To establish a stakeholder management mechanism and adopt appropriate measures
and tools to balance stakeholder interests.

1-2 Methods of Stakeholder Engagement

1-2-1 Meaningful Consultation and Negotiation
PMO consults and negotiates with stakeholders on the project's planning and
implementation appropriately, allowing them to express their views on project risks,
impacts, and mitigation measures. Such consultations are free from manipulation,
interference, coercion, discrimination, and intimidation. Consultation and negotiation
activities will run through the entire project cycle. The contents of the consultation and
negotiation will be recorded and disclosed. The specific forms are as follows.
（1）Consultation meetings and seminars
During project planning and implementation, when determining significant issues such
as project composition and construction content, scheme comparison, land acquisition
and house demolition compensation standards, and resettlement method selection,
PMO organizes consultation meetings and seminars attended by government leaders
and experts to collect information such as project approval requirements and progress
of the project. In addition, their views on the project's potential environmental and
social impacts are consulted. Consultation meetings and seminars can be held offline
or online. The project also holds various seminars attended by affected groups and the
public, including ethnic minority groups, women, and low-income people. At the
workshops, the public can express their opinions freely without external manipulation,
interference, coercion, discrimination, and intimidation.
（2）Focus group interviews and in-depth interviews
PMO has commissioned the environmental and social impact assessment teams to
organize focus group interviews and individual in-depth interviews with the
stakeholders. The focus group interviews convene similar people to conduct free

                                              2
exchanges and discussions. The discussions focus on gender equality, protection of
vulnerable groups, compensation and resettlement methods for land acquisition and
house demolition, livelihood restoration and development, occupational health and
safety, and environmental pollution. Participants spoke freely about the focus topics
and expressed their views and interests. The environmental and social impact
assessment teams also selected some public members for in-depth interviews.
（3）Hearing
Before the LA and HD scheme is released, the government land acquisition department
will convene representatives of PAPs to hold hearings to listen to their opinions on land
acquisition compensation standards and relocation and the RAP to improve the plan
further. The hearings are conducive to communicating information, eliminating
barriers, reaching consensus, and coordinating stakeholders' actions.
1-2-2 Timely and sufficient Information Disclosure
Provide stakeholders with timely and sufficient project information so they can
understand the risks, potential opportunities, and impacts of the project as early as
possible. Information publication can effectively curb rumors and speculation,
eliminate PAPs’ resistance, and establish a trusting relationship between the PMO,
PAPs, and other stakeholders, which is conducive to the smooth implementation of the
project. Timely and sufficient information disclosure is the basis for meaningful
consultation and stakeholder negotiation. Considering the differences in how
stakeholders obtain information, this project will adopt multiple ways to disclose
information.
（1）Publish notices in affected villages and communities
PMO and the district government's land acquisition department will hold meetings in
villages and communities affected by the project to convey information on land
acquisition compensation and post all land acquisition compensation information (such
as the amount of land acquired for each household, compensation standards, and cash
compensation received by each household ) for public supervision. In particular, for
vulnerable groups such as the older people, single mothers, disabled persons, and low-
income groups, the local village committee will be responsible for conveying the
                                            3
information to their homes if they cannot attend the meeting or read the notice.
（2）Information disclosure in affected ethnic minority villages
In the four ethnic minority villages, PMO and the district land acquisition and house
demolition department will promptly publicize the information about land acquisition
and house demolition compensation in a language and manner acceptable to ethnic
minorities. PMO will also select two representatives from local ethnic minorities in the
ethnic minority villages as contacts to communicate with the district-level land
acquisition and house demolition agencies and promptly reflect the needs of local ethnic
minorities.
（3）Providing documents for stakeholders to review
When the project documents, such as the Resettlement Action Plan, Environmental and
Social Management Plans, Labor Management Procedures, and Ethnic Minority
Development Plan, are approved by the World Bank and local governments, PMO will
provide paper copies to each local resettlement office so that the PMO can easily access
them. All these approved documents will also be made public on the official website
for stakeholders to review.
（4）Publicizing project information through traditional media
PMO will select appropriate traditional media, such as television and newspapers, to
promptly publicize project information so that project stakeholders can understand the
progress of the project's implementation and other information.
（ 5 ） Publicize project information through official websites, WeChat public
accounts, and other self-medias
With the rise of the Internet, WeChat public accounts, and other self-media, PMO will
set up an official project website to publish project-related information promptly. PMO
will also send project information to stakeholders regularly or irregularly through
various forms such as text messages, WeChat, official websites, WeChat public
accounts, etc. Information disclosure is a two-way process. PMO informs the
stakeholders of information and promptly answers their questions. Meanwhile, the
public can quickly provide opinions and suggestions to project managers through
telephone, email, public accounts, etc.

2 Definition and Identification of Stakeholders

2-1 Definition of Stakeholders

The project stakeholders refer to the following individuals and groups.
2-1-1 People Affected by the Project
Individuals and organizations affected by the project are defined as Persons Affected
by the Project(PAPs). They mainly refer to stakeholders affected by the project's impact
or potential risk on their physical environment, health, safety, cultural practices, well-
being, or livelihood. These stakeholders may be individuals or groups, including local
communities.
2-1-2 Other Stakeholders
Individuals and organizations concerned about or can influence the project are other
stakeholders. They mainly refer to individuals, groups, or organizations interested in
the project, which may be formed due to the location, characteristics, impacts, or other
matters related to the public interest. For example, these stakeholders may include
regulators, government officials, the private sector, scientific groups, academics, trade
unions, women's organizations, other civil society organizations, and cultural groups.
Project stakeholders may be beneficiaries, risk bearers, or victims.

2-2 Identification of Stakeholders

To identify project stakeholders, PMOcollects information about them, distinguishes
their relationship with the project, confirms key stakeholders, identifies decision
makers providing resources or resolving disputes, direct influencers (the ones directly
affecting the project or directly affected by the project), indirect influencers, and
observers, and fully understands the stakeholders’ needs and interests. That is the
essential base for targeted management.
The project stakeholders include two categories: (1) the project affected people. They
are groups and individuals with legal contractual relationships with the project,
including owners, engineering contractors, design agencies, engineering supervision,

consulting agencies, families, enterprises, and organizations affected by the project land
acquisition. (2) Other stakeholders. They are groups and individuals with no formal
contractual relationship with the project. However, they are affected by or can influence
it, including government departments approving the project, project users, news media,
non-governmental organizations, local communities, and the public. The impact of each

3 Initial Stakeholder Engagement Activities

Stakeholder participation and consultation activities will continue throughout the
project cycle. In different project stages, stakeholders will focus on other issues.
Therefore, The PMO, the feasibility study agency, and the environmental and social
consulting agency have carried out activities for stakeholder participation, consultation,
and information disclosure during the project preparation stage.

3-1 Initial Stakeholder Consultation Activities

Stakeholder participation and consultation activities have been initiated since the
project identification stage and will continue until the project is closed. So far, PMO
has organized such activities (Table 3-1). In the project preparation stage, the focus of
participation and consultation activities is as follows.
3-1-1 Optimizing Project Design
The opinions and needs of local people are of great significance to optimizing design.
Involving local grassroots governments and people in project design activities can
significantly improve project design and minimize land acquisition, house demolition,
and environmental impact. PMO requires the design agency to have a deep
understanding of the needs of local people and incorporate their reasonable needs into
project design to increase the project's positive effects.
3-1-2 Improving Resettlement Action Plan
During the project preparation process, PMOnot only consulted the government land
acquisition and house demolition management department about the latest land
acquisition compensation policy but also focused on the resettlement method and
conducted full consultations with the representatives of PAPs. Participation of PAPs is
considered a meaningful way to improve the Resettlement Action Plan. Before the land
acquisition and house demolition, the government land acquisition department will also
hold hearings attended by representatives of PAPs to improve the implementation plan
further.


                                             8
3-1-3 Improving Environmental Management Plan
Aha Lake is an ecological park and public wetland with a beautiful environment and
rich species. It is necessary to avoid the adverse impacts of project construction on the
environment and protect species diversity. PMO consulted relevant government
departments and local people, conducted extensive consultations on development and
environmental protection issues, and provided valuable suggestions for optimizing the
project's environmental management plan.
3-1-4 Support for Vulnerable Groups
The project will affect some vulnerable groups, including single mothers, low-income
people, disabled persons, and lonely older people. Due to their low income and limited
ability, vulnerable people often find it challenging to cope with the changes in their
production and life brought about by the project construction. And they lack influence
on public policies. Therefore, their needs are more likely to be ignored. To this end,
PMO has specially surveyed vulnerable groups, identified the vulnerable groups
affected by the project, analyzed their family income levels and living conditions, and
understood their needs and interests so that PMO and local governments can take
appropriate measures to protect the rights and interests of vulnerable groups. Vulnerable
groups’ voices can be transmitted to project decision-makers promptly.
3-1-5 Development of Ethnic Minorities
The project-affected area is a multi-ethnic mixed area where ethnic groups frequently
interact and intermarry, use the same language daily, and have no significant differences
in lifestyle and production methods from the Han people. No groups rely on natural
resources used for generations or habitats left by their ancestors, and there are no
"voluntary isolation" or "first contact" ethnic minorities.
Each ethnic group has unique ethnic and cultural traditions and customs. To ensure that
ethnic minorities can fully participate in the project's design and implementation,
benefit from it, and minimize its negative impact, PMO commissioned a SIA team to
conduct investigations on the affected ethnic minority villages. The EMDP will be
compiled to protect their rights and interests and their traditional culture.

3-2 Initial Information Disclosures

During the project identification and preparation stage, PMO has extensive two-way
information communication with the stakeholders: on the one hand, it conveys the
Guiyang Green Development concept and the progress of the project to the government
industry authorities and the public; on the other hand, PMO actively solicits good Green
Development projects from the industry authorities and the public, and determines the
composition of the project sub-projects and optimizes the project design based on
information disclosure and extensive discussion. After the project construction content
and activities are determined, PMO promptly investigates and consults with the villages,
affected families, and persons, listens to their voices, and incorporates the main affected
stakeholders into the project planning and implementation as soon as possible. The
information disclosure activities currently carried out by the project are shown in Table
3-2.

During the implementation of the project, some PAPs may feel that they have been
mistreated and need to file a complaint to protect their rights and interests. Some

5-1 Complaint Channels and Procedures

During the project's implementation, the public can use two primary channels for
complaints: the permanent complaint platform and the mechanism of Guizhou Province

explicitly established for this project.The PMO will establish information and work
contacts mechanism with the permanent grievances platform and institutions of

has the authority to handle will be transferred to the PMO for processing. Grievances
that can only be handled by permanent institutions of Guiyang Municipal Government
will also be conveyed to the PMO regarding the grievances handling procedures and
results, ensuring that the PMO can record all grievances related to the project.

(2) Labor dispute resolution mechanism and procedures
There are two channels for labor complaints related to the project: the enterprise's
internal labor complaint channel and mechanism and the external labor complaint
channel and mechanism. The labor force can choose any of these mechanisms to file a
complaint or use both channels simultaneously.
① Internal Complaint Procedures
Employees who think they have been mistreated should first appeal to the company's
human resources department, labor union, or company leaders. The human resources
department is responsible for registering, investigating, verifying the appeal, and
proposing appropriate solutions.
A labor dispute mediation committee shall be established within enterprises. The labor
dispute mediation committee shall be composed of employee representatives, employer
representatives, and union representatives. There must be at least one female
representative among the representatives, and the Labor Dispute Mediation Committee
director shall be a union representative. The labor dispute mediation committee is


                                                       25
responsible for mediating labor disputes and deciding solutions. The human resources
department should pay attention to protecting personal privacy rights and promptly give
feedback on the handling results to the complainant. The enterprise union should play
a role in safeguarding employees' legitimate rights and interests.
If the complainant does not accept the solution decision, they may apply for mediation
or arbitration or file a lawsuit through external complaint procedures by the law.
② External Complaint Procedures
Suppose a labor dispute occurs between a project-related enterprise and its employees,
and they can not agree through inner labor mediation. In that case, the complainant can
file a complaint through the following three channels.
Labor mediation. The complainant may apply for mediation with a grassroots
mediation organization established by the law. Suppose a mediation agreement is
reached on the payment of overdue labor remuneration, work-related medical expenses,
economic compensation, or damages, and the employer fails to perform within the
agreed period. In that case, the employee may apply to the People's Court for a payment
order by presenting the mediation agreement following the law. The People's Court
shall issue a payment order following the law. After reaching a mediation agreement, if
one party fails to perform the contract within the agreed period, the other party may
apply for arbitration per the law. If the party is dissatisfied with the arbitration award,
they may file a lawsuit with the courts.
Labor Arbitration. After a labor dispute occurs, if mediation fails and one party
requires arbitration, the party may apply for arbitration to the Labor Dispute Arbitration
Committee. One party may also apply for arbitration directly to the Labor Dispute
Arbitration Committee after a labor dispute occurs. The applicant shall submit a written
arbitration application. Within 5 days from the date of receipt of the arbitration
application, if the Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee deems that the conditions for
acceptance are met, it shall accept the application and notify the applicant; after the
Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee agrees with the arbitration application, it shall
serve a copy of the arbitration application to the respondent within 5 days. After
receiving the copy of the arbitration application, the respondent shall submit a reply to
the Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee within 10 days. After receiving the reply, the
Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee shall serve a copy of the reply to the applicant

                                            26
within 5 days. If the respondent fails to submit a reply, it will not affect the arbitration
procedure.
The Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee adopts the arbitration tribunal system to
adjudicate labor dispute cases. The arbitration tribunal is composed of 3 arbitrators,
with a chief arbitrator. A single arbitrator can arbitrate simple labor dispute cases. The
arbitration tribunal shall notify both parties of the date and location of the hearing 5
days before the hearing. If a party has a legitimate reason, it may request a
postponement of the hearing 3 days before the hearing. Whether to postpone the hearing
shall be decided by the Labor Dispute Arbitration Committee.
Arbitration results shall be based on the opinions of the majority of arbitrators, and the
dissenting views of the minority of arbitrators shall be recorded. When the arbitration
tribunal cannot form a majority opinion, arbitration results shall be made following the
chief arbitrator's opinion. The arbitration tribunal shall adjudicate labor dispute cases
within 45 days from the date the labor dispute arbitration committee accepts the
application. Suppose the case is complicated and needs to be postponed. In that case, it
may be delayed with the approval of the director of the Labor Dispute Arbitration
Committee, and the parties shall be notified in writing. Still, the extension period shall
not exceed 15 days.
Litigation. Suppose the two parties to a labor dispute are dissatisfied with the labor
arbitration award. In that case, they may file a lawsuit with the People's Court within
15 days from receipt of the arbitration result. If one party does not file a lawsuit or
perform the arbitration result within the statutory period, the other party may apply to
the court for compulsory execution. If the labor arbitration fails to make an arbitration
result within the prescribed time limit, the parties may file a lawsuit regarding the labor
dispute with the court.
5-1-2 Guiyang's Permanent Complaint Channels and Procedures
Guizhou Province and Guiyang have established permanent complaint channels and
mechanisms. Governments need to understand public opinion and accept public
supervision. It is also an important channel for the public to file complaints. The
permanent complaint channels mainly include the following categories:
①    Guiyang 12345 Government Service Platform
Guiyang Government established the Guiyang 12345 Government Service Platform. It

provides service 24 hours a day. Citizens can make inquiries, complaints, and requests

has established a complete service process. The municipal platform is responsible for
hotline connection, problem distribution, follow-up supervision, and settlement and
cancellation. The district(or cities and counties) governments are responsible for
coordinating and handling the relevant issues reported by the complainant in their
jurisdiction and handling problems pertinent to the municipal platform as required. It
has formed an effective system to handle complaints. This comprehensive government
service platform can handle project stakeholders' inquiries, complaints, and suggestions.
②    Guiyang Petition Bureau
The Guiyang Petition Bureau is a government agency responsible for handling letters
and visits from the public and supervising and handling relevant petition matters. The
agency receives petition materials from citizens, legal persons, and other organizations,
then transfers them to appropriate departments or handles them independently.
③    Departments such as the Safety Supervision Bureau, Labor Supervision Team,
and the Ecological and Environmental Protection Bureau
If someone employed by the contractor finds hidden safety hazards in the contractor's
workplace, they can directly report and complain to the Guiyang Safety Supervision
Bureau. The Safety Supervision Bureau will organize safety and special inspections.
Suppose the complainant considers that the enterprise has violated laborers’ rights and
labor laws, such as forcing labor, not paying wages on time, or causing a labor dispute.
In that case, they can directly report and complain to the Guiyang Labor Supervision
Team. The Labor Supervision Team will accept the case, conduct an investigation, put
forward opinions promptly, and urge employers and workers to comply with the Labor

file a complaint to the National Ecological and Environmental Complaints Reporting
Platform and the Ecological and Environmental Complaints Reporting Platform of
Guizhou Province and Guiyang. These departments are responsible for ecological and
environmental protection. They will investigate and handle the case following the law
to prevent environmental pollution accidents.
