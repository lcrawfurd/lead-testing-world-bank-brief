0 Project Overview

    Guiyang Green Urban Development Project integrates the twin concepts
of low-carbon and nature-positive urban development to enhance urban
livability and mitigate climate change, with a focus on biodiversity
enhancement in the project areas while reducing carbon emissions from the
transportation   and   wastewater     sectors    and   enhancing     the   carbon
sequestration capacity of local ecosystems. Climate adaptation and resilience
will be enhanced by improving ecosystems and implementing nature-based
solutions. The project will also improve the accessibility and ecotourism
development of the National Wetland Park and provide opportunities for
sustainable economic development in the project areas. At the same time, the
project will support studies on low-carbon and biodiversity-related policies,
spatial planning and capacity building, including the establishment and
improvement of carbon emissions monitoring and accounting system that
promotes the achievement of carbon emission reduction targets as well as the
eventual realization of carbon peaking and carbon neutrality goals, and
explore the innovative model for green and low-carbon development in the city
of Guiyang.

    In order to mitigate the negative impacts related to the project and
guarantee the achievement of the expected environmental benefits, the
Environmental Management Plan (EMP) is prepared for this project, which
describes the key environmental management requirements for the project,
specifically including the arrangements and responsibilities for environmental
management organizations, environmental mitigation measures, monitoring
and management measures during design, construction, and operation
periods, and the environmental mitigation measures required in the EIA and
EMP will be incorporated into the engineering designs, bidding documents,
and civil works contracts, which will be implemented by the design institutes,

ecology and environment authorities. The effectiveness of these measures will
be assessed based on the results of environmental inspections and monitoring
to determine whether they should be continued, improved or adjusted.

    1.2.3 Environmental protection enforcement organizations

    During the construction of the project, the contractors will play a key role in
environmental    management,       pollution     control    and   implementation    of
prevention and control measures, therefore, the following requirements need
to be made for the contractors:

    (1) The contractors must have the relevant organizational structure,
personnel and budgetary arrangements to ensure that the environmental
management plan is effectively implemented;

    (2) The contractors’ bidding documents and bidding offers shall give full
consideration to the environmental impact mitigation measures and their costs
during the construction period, and shall eventually be implemented in the
project contracts as a contractual requirement for the project contractors.

    (3) The contractors, their designated environmental staff, and the
                                             8
  Environmental Management Plan (EMP) for Guiyang Green Urban Development Project


construction supervisors, etc., must receive training on environmental
protection and environmental management prior to the commencement of
work to ensure that they are competent to perform the work.

    (4) The contractors of the different sections are required to have a
sufficient number of full-time environmental staff to monitor and record
environmental activities during construction, and to provide environmental
records to the PMO and environmental protection supervision organizations on
a regular basis for supervision and review.

    (5) During the construction process, the contractors shall maintain
communication and consultation with the public in the area of the project by
erecting bulletin boards in the construction units to notify the public of specific
construction activities and construction hours. A contact person and contact
phone number will also be provided to allow the public to complain and provide
suggestions regarding construction activities.

supervision of the PIU, Guiyang PMO, and the supervision company. The
effectiveness of these measures will be assessed based on monitoring results
from the supervision and external monitoring consultants to determine if
adjustments and improvements to these measures are needed.

3 Environmental Monitoring Plan

    The purpose of environmental monitoring is to provide a comprehensive
and timely grasp of the pollution dynamics of the proposed project, to
understand the implementation and execution of the project's environmental
management measures, to provide timely feedback to the competent
authorities, to make timely adjustments and responses, and to provide a
scientific basis for the project's environmental management.

    Environmental monitoring during the construction and operation periods
will be entrusted by the Guiyang PMO to units with appropriate qualifications,
and the undertaking unit should be a national monitoring and certification unit
in the fields of environment and soil erosion, with complete equipment and
strong technical force, so that it can better fulfill the monitoring tasks it
undertakes.

    Sensitive points that may be more obvious will be used as monitoring
points to track and monitor the pollution during the construction and operation
periods of the project. The monitoring content selects sound environment, air
environment and surface water environment which have greater impacts. The
monitoring factors are determined according to the pollution characteristic
factors of the project. The monitoring and analyzing method adopts the

4 Capacity Building and Training Programs

    The training system for environmental management under the project
covers multiple subjects, and the trainees include supervisors and technicians
from the PMO, contractors, supervision and other organizations and the
community residents. The PMO of this project has experience in the traditional
projects funded by the World Bank, but lacks systematic recognition in the new
World Bank’s Environmental and Social Framework (ESF), and it’s necessary
to train the PMO on the ESF during the implementation of the project. The
PMO will be made fully aware of the environmental and social risks of the
project implementation, the raise of resource efficiency, and the protection of
biodiversity, and the effective public engagement enabled by effective
information disclosure, consultation, and feedback system. The concept of
ecological and environmental protection will be implemented throughout the
full cycle of the project implementation, the measures and requirements in the
EMP will be incorporated in the bidding documents, and environmental
protection will be fixed in the contracts as contractual terms, the contractors
will be urged to implement various environmental measures and management
requirements, and their awareness of environmental protection should be
enhanced, and their technical and managerial capacity should be improved, in
order to control the negative impacts at the lowest level.

    Environmental awareness training shall include community residents
involved in the project to better understand the relationship between wetland
protection, biodiversity protection, and their behaviors, while the community

5 Grievance Redress Mechanism

    (1) Grievance Redress Mechanism (GRM)

    In order to better safeguard the interests of the project communities and
residents, the project will establish an accessible, open and effective GRM,
whereby affected persons can lodge a grievance at any time regarding any
issue in the implementation of the project. This GRM is suitable for all affected
persons in the project area.

    The principles of the GRM include:

    1) Ensure the participation and consultation of the above beneficiary
groups in the implementation of the project, as well as ensuring that the project
groups benefit from the project, avoiding or mitigating negative impacts, and
minimizing the occurrence of problems or social risks.

    2) In accordance with the relevant requirements and the requirements of
the arrangement of our government's letter and petition system, as well as the
successful experiences in this regard at home and abroad, make full use of the
existing letter and petition system and institution, and establish and improve
the mechanism for reflecting and handling the opinions and demands of this
project in the PMO and the relevant governmental departments as well as the
management office of Lake Aha Wetland Park and other institutions, and run it.

    3) Enhance and improve the policy awareness, mass work consciousness
and methods, and democratic style of the cadres of the communities and
village neighborhood committees/village committees in which the projects are
located, fully listen to the opinions of the public, and solve problems and
mediate conflicts in a fair and reasonable manner, or reflect them to their
superiors and seek timely responses.

    4) Community/village organizations shall be set up on a community/village

                                          43
    Environmental Management Plan (EMP) for Guiyang Green Urban Development Project


basis, and a specialized agency shall be set up to reflect and deal with the
opinions   and   demands     of   the   public   on   this   project,   mainly   by
community/village organizations, and shall work and perform its duties with the
support of the community/village organizations.

    GRM specifically includes:

    (1) Grievances Receiving Organization

    The grass-roots grievance receiving organizations are mainly composed
of neighborhood committees/village committees responsible for coordinating
the handling of problems encountered by community residents/villagers in the
course of project implementation; matters that cannot be dealt with by the
community organizations/village committees are handed over to the
streets/townships; finally, the streets/townships will report the problems to the
district/county governments to deal with the problems encountered by the
community residents in the course of the implementation of the project that
need to be complained about, for example, environmental protection problems
and disputes arising from the project construction. In addition, they can also
appeal to the municipal and provincial environmental protection departments
at the same time.

    (2) Grievance Procedures

    Stage 1: If residents of the project area have opinions or feel dissatisfied
with the project, they may lodge an oral or written grievance with the
community/village organization; in the case of an oral grievance, it will be dealt
with by the receiving team and recorded in writing. For reasonable requests or
suggestions, they are usually dealt with within 2 weeks after receiving the
grievance. For problems such as noise and dust during construction , verbal or
written grievances can be made directly to the contractors, and a response is
required within 24 hours and recorded in writing.

    Stage 2: If the complainant is still dissatisfied with the decision at this level,
he/she may appeal to the street/township after receiving the decision; the
street/township shall make a decision on the handling of the grievance within 2
weeks after receiving the grievance.

    Stage 3: If the complainant is still dissatisfied with the handling decision at
this level, he/she can file a grievance in the district/county after receiving the
decision; the district/government or the environmental protection department
shall make a decision on the handling of the grievance within 2 weeks after
receiving the grievance.

    Stage 4: If the complainant is still not satisfied with the decision at the
district/county level, he/she can appeal to the Municipal PMO after receiving
the feedback, or at the same time to the national, provincial, municipal and
other environmental protection departments (12369 Environmental Protection
Hotline, or All-inclusive Ecological Complaints and Reporting Platform, etc.), if
the environmental protection problems and disputes are caused by the
construction of the project.

    Stage 5: If the complainant is still dissatisfied with the results of the
Municipal PMO and the provincial and municipal environmental protection
departments, he or she may file a lawsuit in a civil court in accordance with the
Civil Procedure Law after receiving the decision of the Provincial PMO and the
Provincial Department of Environmental Protection.

    Stage 6: Prosecution in the civil courts.

    Residents of the project area will be informed of the above-mentioned
channels of appeal through meetings and other means, so that they are fully
aware of their right to appeal. At the same time, media tools will be utilized to
enhance publicity and reporting, and opinions and suggestions on the project
will be compiled into information articles for timely study and handling by all

6 Monitoring and Reporting System

6.1 Purpose and Methodology

    In accordance with China's regulations on environmental management of
construction projects and the requirements of the Bank's operational policies,
the Borrower shall be responsible for the preparation of the Evaluation and
Monitoring Report on the Implementation of the Environmental Management
Plan (generally twice a year), with the aim of ensuring that all the relevant
requirements and measures of the approved Environmental Management Plan
have been implemented, and that problems are detected in a timely manner
and analyzed and summarized, so as to control the adverse environmental
impacts in the follow-up work of the project.

    Environmental management requires the necessary exchange of
information between different departments and positions in Guiyang PMO,
contractors, and operators as well as the organization's communication of
relevant information to the outside (interested parties, the public, etc.).

    Internal information exchange can be carried out in a variety of ways, such
as meetings and internal briefings, but there must be one formal meeting per
month, and all exchanges of information should be documented and archived.

6.2 Monitoring and Reporting Mechanisms

    The supervision and management and reporting mechanism of the
Municipal PMO, contractors and operators, and monitoring consultants, in the
implementation of the project, is organized as follows:

    (1) The project supervision engineer is responsible for the daily
supervision of the implementation of environmental protection measures
during the construction period, and records the implementation in detail in the
supervision logbook and monthly report, and submits the weekly and monthly

                                          48
    Environmental Management Plan (EMP) for Guiyang Green Urban Development Project


reports to and the PMO in a timely manner. The weekly and monthly reports
shall include the implementation of environmental protection measures.

    (2) The PMO conducts regular and irregular inspections of the project
throughout the process, supervises and checks the implementation of the
environmental management plan, and records the problems found in the
project and the process of dealing with them for inclusion in the semi-annual
report.

    (3) The external monitoring consultant is responsible for supervising the
implementation of mitigation measures during the construction and operation
periods,   focusing   on   monitoring    the   construction   and    progress    of
environmental protection projects, urging the timely resolution of problems
found in the supervision process, and actively coordinating the handling of
environmental protection technical problems to ensure that the environmental
protection measures are successfully implemented, and assisting the PMO in
the analysis and reporting work.

    (4) The monitoring consultant will submit the monitoring report to the PMO
and the external monitoring consultant in a timely manner after completing the
task entrusted to it, and will clearly give a conclusion on whether it meets the
standards and complies with them based on the monitoring data and briefly
analyze the reasons for not meeting the standards.

    (5) Guiyang PMO will prepare timely progress reports based on (1)~ (4).
The project progress report prepared by the PMO must include the
implementation status of the environmental management plan and the effect of
implementation, especially the results of environmental monitoring .

    (6) The implementation of the project's environmental management plan,
which may be submitted to the Bank on a regular basis as a chapter of the
semi-annual report on the project's progress, may include the following main

    (i) Progress of the project, a brief description of the main elements of this

    (iii) Implementation of environmental protection mitigation measures,
