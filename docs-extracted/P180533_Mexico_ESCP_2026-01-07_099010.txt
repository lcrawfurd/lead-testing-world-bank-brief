1. The United Mexican States (the Recipient) will implement the Mexico BioCarbon Emissions
   Reduction Program (the “Program”) and Emission Reduction Project (the “Project”) (jointly
   referred as the “Operation”), through, respectively, the National Forestry Commission (CONAFOR),
   with the participation of the Secretariat of Environment and Natural Resources (SEMARNAT), as
   set out in the Emissions Reduction Purchase Agreement (ERPA) and through CONAFOR as set out
   in the Grant Agreement (the Agreements). The International Bank for Reconstruction and
   Development (the Bank), acting as trustee of the BioCFplus-ISFL Trust Fund, has agreed to provide
   financing for the Operation, as set out in the Agreements.

2. The Recipient, through CONAFOR, shall ensure that the Operation, including its underlying
   activities and benefit-sharing, is carried out in accordance with the Environmental and Social
   Standards (ESSs) and this Environmental and Social Commitment Plan (ESCP), in a manner
   acceptable to the Bank. The ESCP is a part of the Agreements. Unless otherwise defined in this
   ESCP, capitalized terms used in this ESCP have the meanings ascribed to them in the Agreements.

3. Without limitation to the foregoing, this ESCP sets out material measures and actions that the
   Recipient shall carry out or cause to be carried out, including, as applicable, their respective
   timeframes; institutional, staffing, training, monitoring and reporting arrangements; and
   grievance management. The ESCP also sets out the environmental and social (E&S) documents
   that shall be prepared or updated, consulted, disclosed and implemented under the Operation,
   consistent with the ESSs, in form and substance acceptable to the Bank. Said E&S documents may
   be revised from time to time with prior written agreement by the Bank. As provided for under the
   referred Agreements, the Recipient shall ensure that there are sufficient funds available to cover
   the costs of implementing the ESCP.

4. As agreed by the Bank and the Recipient, this ESCP will be revised from time to time, if necessary,
   to reflect adaptive management of the Operation changes or unforeseen circumstances or in
   response to the Operation performance. In such circumstances, the Bank and the Recipient,
   through CONAFOR, agree to update the ESCP to reflect on these changes through an exchange of
   letters signed between the Bank and CONAFOR’s Director General. The Recipient shall promptly
   disclose the updated ESCP.

5. The subsection on “Indicators for Implementation Readiness” below identifies the actions and
   measures to be monitored to assess the Operation readiness to begin implementation in
   accordance with this ESCP. Nevertheless, all actions and measures in this ESCP shall be
   implemented as set out in the “Timeframe” column below irrespective of whether they are listed
   in the referred subsection.
