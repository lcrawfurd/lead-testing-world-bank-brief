II. SUMMARY OF PROJECT STATUS AND PROPOSED CHANGES

 Status: The Project has retained the PDO and IP ratings of Moderately Satisfactory (MS) over the past six ISRs. At
 present, ratings for all other components are rated "MS" or higher. This constitutes the Project's first extension.

 Rationale for the extension: Delays beyond the Project’s control including a 14-month lag in the Project’s
 effectiveness, hold-ups in the contract commencement orders by the Tribunal de Contas, COVID-19 lock-down
 (including visas issuance to consultants), all of which has lingered the approval and implementation of key large
 design/build contracts, which will not conclude by the current project closing date (3/31/24), as their implementation
 phase outruns the project length. The current situation holds at risk the PDO through the following:

 a) 40 000 home connections that will not be implemented, affecting 571 290 people who are underserved and or will
 remain unconnected;
 b) utility turnaround efforts will be lost, putting in jeopardy the sustainability of investments and services delivered by
 the utilities (EPAs) and
 c) sanitation investment pilots will not conclude, as such activities requires long term preparation and
 implementation.

 The team also confirms: (i) the Project is not subject to suspension of disbursements; (ii) the Borrower does not have
 any outstanding audit reports nor there are audit reports, which are not satisfactory to the Bank; and (iii) no additional
 safeguards will be triggered.

 Recommendation: To this end, a 21-month extension is proposed to allow the technical assistance and investments to
 conclude accordingly and ensure the PDO and results framework remains achievable.
